Context


Emma CEO and other employees are currently getting messages from very dissatisfied customers via their personal accounts such as LinkedIn. The unsatisfied customers will be forwarded to our CS priority Group known as "XX(Country ID)HLE", via a ticket raised in Jira. This is where you and your colleagues will come in to manage with top-quality and eye-to-detail cases that are already on a high level of complexity and some details might have been left unattended.


What to Expect


There are several reasons that can lead a customer to reach out Emma employees outside the proper channels, however the most common is the length of their ongoing issue, some cases drag for months without a proper solution.

Most common reasons are due to order delays and refund delays.

Resolution Process


  1. Once the Ticket arrives in the High-level escalation Group "XX(Country ID)HLE", open an outbound email (Outbound Ticket) and reply to the customer within 1 business hour after receiving their case, following the guidelines in step 2.

  2. Apologize for what has happened and confirm we will get back to the customer within one business day - use the canned response "HLE first response" and personalise where needed. (Outbound Ticket)

  3. Reply on the High-level escalation ticket following the template below and change the status to "In Progress".

    GDPR: Yes/No
    Ticket Number: ____________________ (HLE) / ____________________ (Outbound Ticket)
    Customer Inquiry: __________________
    Actions taken: __________________

    Please categorize the ticket accordingly with the customer topic/request as per usual.

  4. Investigate what caused this customer to escalate:

    • The High-level escalation ticket should contain the customer claim for guidance

    • Analyze historical tickets as often in these cases the customer already contacted recently about the same topic

    • Request further information to the customer (Note: this might have a further negative impact as customers don't appreciate having to repeat the same information several times therefore only proceed with this step if it's really necessary)

  5. Resolve the case accordingly:

    • If compensation is needed, ask your supervisor what the supervisor compensation is available for this case and follow it

    • If you need support from another team to resolve the case, escalate it via the dedicated Escalation Jira Portal and change the status to "Waiting for third party"

    • Make sure you manage the customer expectations properly:

      • If you need longer than one business day after the first reply to solve the case, update the customer in writing that you are still investigating and we will get back to them within one business day. (Outbound Ticket)

      • If you need longer than one business day after the second reply to solve the case, update the customer in writing that you are still investigating and we will get back to them within one business day. Repeat this till the third working day. (Outbound Ticket)

      • Once the case is resolved, provide feedback to the customer. (Outbound Ticket)

  6. Keep the ticket that was created in the High-level escalation Group up to date during the complete process until the case is resolved.

    * NOTE -> If the customer does not reply, the ticket will automatically be closed after 7 days on "Waiting on customer" status.

Make sure you use and follow the detailed step-by-step guide below!

Targets summary (SLA's):


First response time: 1 hour


Additional follow up time (when no additional information/action is required from the customer): once a day for 3 business days

Additional follow up time (when we require additional information/action from the customer): only one time, 2 business days after the original email requesting for more information or actions was sent


Average resolution time: 3 business days

Quality Score: 90%

Agent CSAT: 90%
