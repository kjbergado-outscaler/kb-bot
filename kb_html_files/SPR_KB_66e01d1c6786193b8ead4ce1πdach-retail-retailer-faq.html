Warranty claims / sagging

  • What is the tolerance for a sagging spot again?

Regulation / also for photos:


• Remove the mattress from the bed / for removable covers, remove the cover.
• Place the mattress core flat on the floor, not on the existing base or slatted frame, for measurement.
• Place a straight measuring stick/ruler on the mattress.
• Measure the clearance between the measuring stick and the mattress in the center.
• Do not press the measuring tool into the mattress.
• The clearance measurement must not exceed 15 mm.

Anything up to 1.5 cm under the above-mentioned conditions is acceptable. Anything over 1.5 cm will be replaced.

The problem with a creaking slatted frame is resolved as follows:

  • Ask if the bed is made of wood. If yes, the issue could be due to wood rubbing against wood. To test this, remove the slatted frame, place it on the floor, and put the mattress on top. Test if it still creaks. If it does, the slatted frame is the problem. In that case, we would need to ask HHK for replacement parts (within the 2-year legal warranty).

  • If it no longer creaks, the issue was with the bed or wood rubbing against wood. In this case, the interior of the bed frame should be completely or partially lined with felt.

Can mattresses deviate from the stated dimensions?

Generally, the production strives to keep the external dimensions of a mattress or topper exact, for example, to 90x200 cm. However, mattresses are primarily made from foams, spring cores, and textile materials, rather than rigid materials that are inflexible. This means that while production is as precise as possible, factors such as transport and storage can cause slight compression of the inner core due to its own weight. This does not constitute a defect. If a mattress or topper is not delivered exactly to size, it will expand again when placed in a bed frame. However, the product can still have some tolerance. The European standard EN1334 allows for a tolerance of minus 20 mm in both length and width.
