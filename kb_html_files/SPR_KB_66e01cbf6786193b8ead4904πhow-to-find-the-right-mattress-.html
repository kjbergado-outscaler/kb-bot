How to find the right mattress?

It is often a long way to the right mattress recommendation for the customer. This involves discovering the customer's needs, knowing the products and crowning this with a product recommendation. This article serves to help you.



Mattress property finder


Questions beside the finder


Before using the product finder, you should clarify some basic conditions with the customer. Which mattress size is required and do we have it in our range at all? What is the customer's personal preference* in terms of firmness? It should also be clarified whether the mattress will be used by only one person or by several people. This is often the case with the 140x200cm mattress size.



How to use the finder?


Go through each category of questions with the customer. The customer's answers will give you an idea of what materials are needed to provide the customer with the best possible mattress. Please note that not every material is available in every market.

Personal preference against mattress recommendation


In some cases, the recommended mattress may differ from the customer's preference. In some markets, customers prefer a firm mattress, which is not suitable for the customer in the individual case (Emma One hard or Emma O2 hard is returned because it is too firm).


Point out to the customer that the shoulders and pelvis must sink in properly and the waist must be supported, regardless of the customer's stature, weight, or height! You are the expert!



Why the sleeping position is not applied in the finder?


In the statistics presented, the average time spent by side sleepers, stomach sleepers and back sleepers is shown.



Two factors become particularly clear here:

  • A back sleeper spends almost the same amount of time on the side as a side sleeper.

  • A stomach sleeper also spends almost 25% of the night on his or her side.




Due to the progressive structure of the mattresses (more supportive from top to bottom), Emma mattresses are also ideal for stomach sleepers.
