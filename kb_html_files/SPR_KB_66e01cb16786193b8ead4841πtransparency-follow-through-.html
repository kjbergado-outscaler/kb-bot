Transparency is about clearly communicating the course of action of a situation, and with that ensuring the customer is on the same page as you.




Every day we are handling requests from customers related to orders. With time, words such as fulfillment, warehouse, barcode, and SKU become natural to our ears. Even the deadline for the order status to change from packing to shipped is quite obvious (when not facing an unexpected issue). But all those general knowledge is not the same for our customers.



Perception and Subjectivity


Perception is the way we interpret sensory data, but the way we do it, therefore, is subjective. We perceive things differently and we may react differently to situations we face.



Subjectivity is how someone's judgment is shaped by personal opinions and feelings instead of outside influences. As one example, the judgment of a "delay" or the adjective related to it can be very subjective, as the example below:


"Now that is fixed, the delivery will be fast".


"Fast" is subjective information as it can differ based on people's experiences.

Plus, ask yourself, how much time means "fast"? 2 Minutes? 3 Hours? 2 Days? One week? And maybe ask someone else, what does fast means for them? It may happen that people perceive the time differently leading to a misunderstanding, simply because using the word "fast" gives no precise information.


If you hear someone saying: "The difference in the price of this pillow is - affordable - ", you may face the same situation.

Affordable to one person could be completely different from another. It is just another example of how the wording can impact the way people will perceive information.


When handling customers' interaction, it is essential we provide specific deadlines and next steps as this not only gives customer control of the situation and clarity of what is happening, and how is happening, but also avoids a returning contact from the customer for something that could have been clarified before. But how to do it in an effective way?



Transparency Framework


The transparency framework is a supportive guideline to you manage customer expectations by being specific on what happened (acknowledge & reason) / the possible resolution and outcome of it (solve) / The clear next steps with specifics when and how & the following closure, ensuring all information was successfully transmitted with no open questions, in a precise and objective way (close).
