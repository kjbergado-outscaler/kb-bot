Introduction

This article contains the v3.0 version of our quality grid for frontline contacts. This grid will go live on Sprinklr for all EMEA markets on the 3rd of November, 2025. It is used in EMEA markets for frontline Customer Service quality audits.

4.
 Communication

 

 

 

 

 

 

Greetings
& Goodbyes (O)

Pass/Fail

Initial
Greeting:



Phone -> Agent greeted the customer, confirmed they contacted EMMA +
Market (Emma Spain, Emma Germany, etc.), gave his/her name, asked for the
customer's name and enquired about the contact reason.



Email / chat -> Agent greeted the customer, used the customer's name (if
available), thanked the customer for contacting us.



Wrapping Up:



Phone  -> After the initial
contact topic had been handled, the agent showed availability to assist
further and, if the customer had no other question, pitched the phone survey,
thanked the customer for contacting us, and wished the customer a good day.




Email / chat -> After the initial contact topic had been handled, the
agent showed availability to assist further, in case the customer had other
queries in the future. If no additional issues were present, the agent wished
the customer a good day.
Agent
did not respect the initial greeting and wrapping up guidelines.



Agent did not thank the customer for contacting us and/or did not show
availability to further assist the customer with any future queries/issues at
the end of the contact, after the initial contact topic had been fully
addressed.



Pitched the CSAT survey after saying farewell to the customer.



Did not pitch the phone survey correctly, using Emma's standard
template.



Pitched the CSAT survey on phone, email or chat, asking the customer to
give a specific score.

 

 

10%

Empathy and
Cognitive Harmony (S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
showed empathy in a truthful and impactful way. All statements/reactions made
during the contact felt genuine, were appropriate to the situation at hand
and showed a willingness to connect with the customer.

Agent
failed to react to the customer's state of mind, especially when the customer
was disappointed.



Agent said/wrote things/used emojis that were in cognitive dissonance with
the customer's state of mind and the situation at hand.



Agent failed to apologize for Emma not meeting the contact SLA's, when
applicable.



When confronted by the customer, the agent failed to acknowledge that he
had recently handled a query from this specific customer.

 

Agent's
empathy statements/reactions should resonate with the customer. The goal here
is to amplify a positive occurrence and minimize a poor experience.

20%

Emma Tone
(S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
communicated with the customer using the Emma tone of voice. As such, the
agent displayed a positive and engaging attitude throughout the interaction,
showed genuine willingness to help and was always professional, even if the
communication style was informal (unless market decided to have a formal
approach).

Agent
was numb or overly cold throughout the contact.



Agent engaged in negative communication or negative reinforcement.



Agent was over-apologetic and/or hyper-empathic, coming off as
insincere.



Agent used an overly informal communication stance. Even in a pre-sales
context, professional communication standards must be maintained.

 

Note:
Agents should always address the customer in an informal way, unless the
market specifically requested for a formal approach to be the standard or if
the customer had a negative reaction to an informal approach.

15%

Personalization
(S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
personalised any written communication with the customer and did not just use

Additionally, the agent did not repeat the same sentences/expressions,
throughout a chat or in an email, and avoided redundancy.
Agent
did not follow the internal contact guidelines regarding communication
structure.



Agent sent the same introductory or closing sentence in two or more
consecutive emails.



Agent started three consecutive sentences using the same word.



Agent failed to adapt any canned responses/text used to match the specifics
of the case.



Agent gave redundant information throughout the contact.



Agent gave the customer information/content that was unrelated to the case
by mistake.



Agent failed to adapt the language used when the customer was clearly
having difficulty understanding what was being said.



Agent added unnecessary information to an email or chat (example: Emma
contacts).



Agents should avoid over-branding (mentioning Emma too many times).

Time
Management / Hold (S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
took only the time required to handle the interaction.



Agent was also respectful of the hold options rules.
Agent
took longer than expected to handle the situation at hand.



Agent put the customer on hold without informing the customer first and
allowing them to accept or object to it.



Agent did not put the customer on hold by mistake and the conversations in
the production floor's background could be heard.



Agent did not put the customer on hold and just stopped talking while
investigating the situation in question.



Agent did not thank the customer for the time on hold after returning to
the call/chat.



Agent took longer than 2 minutes (4 minutes for chat) before returning to
the call/chat.



Agent stayed connected on a phone call long after the customer
disconnected.

 

While
on hold, agents need to give the customer feedback every 2 minutes. If the
agent feels the situation will take longer than expected to sort out, the
agent should give the customer the option to receive a follow-up contact at a
later date.



Chat hold time can be up to 4 minutes.
