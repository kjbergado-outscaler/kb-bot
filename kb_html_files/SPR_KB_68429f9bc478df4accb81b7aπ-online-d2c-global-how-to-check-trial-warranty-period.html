Introduction

This article will guide you on how to check the start or end date of a warranty or trial period. This can be useful in situations such as when a customer wants to return a product simply because they didn’t like it, or when they make a product claim and we need to verify whether it’s still within the warranty period — among other cases.

Access the PDP (Product Display Page)

​

Firstly, go to the product page. Just below the product description, you’ll find the duration of both the warranty and trial periods (see example below).

It’s essential to check this every time, as these timelines may vary by product or market and can be updated over time. Always refer to the product page to ensure you understand the correct trial and warranty periods for each case.

Check the Delivery Date

The trial and warranty periods begin on the date the product is delivered to the customer. Therefore, it's essential to always verify the product's delivery date.

  • If the product is delivered in multiple shipments, the delivery date of the last component should be used as the start date for both the trial and warranty periods.

  • If the order contains multiple products delivered on different days, each product will have its own trial and warranty start date, based on its individual delivery date.

  • In cases where a supplementary part was required for the item to function properly, the delivery date of the supplement should be considered the start date of the trial and warranty period.

You can find the tracking number and carrier information for the order in Fluent.
Once you have the tracking number, visit the carrier's website to verify the delivery date.

Calculate the Trial and Warranty period

Based on the information you've gathered — the trial and warranty durations listed on the website and the product's delivery date — you can now determine when each period ends.

The trial period is always calculated in calendar days.

For example, if the product page (PDP) indicates a 100-night trial and the customer received the product on June 6, 2025, the trial period will end on September 14, 2025. This would be the final day the customer can return the product simply because it wasn’t the right fit or they didn’t like it.

In the same scenario, if the warranty period is listed as 10 years, it would end on June 6, 2035, this would be the last day the customer could report a product claim and still have it covered at no additional cost, provided the claim is valid.
