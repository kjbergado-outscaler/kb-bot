Knowledge Tidbits


  • Changes in temperature can influence the circadian rhythm (cf. card on "circadian rhythm") that dictates our sleep-wake cycle, and ultimately affect our sleep.

  • Sleep onset usually coincides with the decline in the core body temperature.

  • Therefore, sleep characteristics can be influenced by the thermal property of the mattress.

  • A study showed that young individuals who slept on a high heat capacity mattress (HHCM) have more regular and pronounced sleep cycles over the course of the night as compared to individuals who slept on a conventional mattress.

  • Participants also showed elevated deep sleep values in the second part of the night which is an important sleep stage that enables proper brain functioning and learning.

  • A HHCM is able to efficiently conduct heat and remove excess body heat of the sleeper.

  • This facilitates a reduction in core body temperature, which can enhance the sleep experience.

  • Similarily, a high rise (HR) mattress topper is better at inducing a greater drop in core body temperature in the early stages of sleep than a traditional memory foam mattress.

  • This reduction in temperature was observed regardless of age.



Do innerspring mattresses allow better airflow and temperature regulation than foam mattresses?


  • A study (Forrest R. Cannon & Emiel A. DenHartog, 2019) was conducted to assess the effect of an inverted heat plate of 35 °C on three different types of mattresses:

    • innerspring mattress,

    • foam mattress, and

    • foam mattress with phase change materials (PCMs)

  • Surface temperature, heat flux generated, thermal resistance and the time required to reach steady state thermal resistance were considered

  • Differences in the thermal properties of the mattresses were found in the short term (initial 30 minutes) and long term:

    • The innerspring mattress required a significantly higher heat flux to maintain a test plate surface temperature of 35°C after the first 30 minutes and for the rest of the experiment than the foam mattress or the foam mattress with phase change materials --> indicator that heat was released more easily from the innerspring mattress, and retained more strongly in the foam / foam PCMs mattress

    • The innerspring mattress showed a lower average thermal resistance compared to the other mattresses both after the first 30 minutes and for the rest of the experiment --> meaning it released the temperature faster than the foam.

    • This explains why an innerspring mattress might be perceived cooler and more comfortable on hot nights

    • The spring mattress also attained the fastest its steady-state thermal resistance

  • However, it should be borne in mind that this study worked with a heated plate and not with human subjects

  • Humans have a more mixed interface between skin/possible clothing and mattress, with different pressure distributions and air layers between mattress and skin
