What is Consumentenbond?


The Consumentenbond is a Dutch non-profit organization which promotes consumer protection and is the leading test institution in The Netherlands. All its activities are aimed at strengthening the position of consumers and empowering them to make informed choices. They publish a wide range of magazines, books, and have the largest subscribers (+400,000) website in the Netherlands. It is one of the most trusted organizations in the country.


Escalation cases

Whenever there is an escalated complaint at the Consumentenbond, they will send this over to Emma internally. Someone at Emma will in return forward this to a contact center manager. 


These cases have the highest priority as they will impact our relationship with Consumentenbond


Whenever the file with escalated cases is forwarded to you, this is what you should do:


Contact center manager step-by-step guide:

  1. Confirm with the sender you received the file;
  2. Assign the cases to one or more senior agent;
  3. Whenever support is needed from Emma internal teams to resolve the case, reach out proactively;
  4. Make sure to send the file back to the sender by the end of the next business day.


Agent step-by-step guide:

  1. Once you get the escalated Consumentenbond case, investigate what caused this customer to escalate;
  2. Analyze historical tickets, as often in these cases the customer already contacted us recently about the same topic;
  3. Request further information to the customer if needed (note: this might have a further negative impact as customers don't appreciate having to repeat the same information several times, only proceed with this step if it's really necessary);
  4. If compensation is needed, ask your supervisor what the supervisor compensation is available for this case;
  5. Resolve the case accordingly - make sure you manage the customers' expectations properly: If you need longer than one business day to solve the case, update the customer in writing that you are still investigating and we will get back to them. Keep the escalation file up to date during the complete process until the case is resolved;
  6. Update the Escalation file on updates and outcomes accordingly.
