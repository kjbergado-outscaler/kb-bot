Introduction


The return deflection is the supreme discipline among arguments. It is about selling a product that has already been sold to the customer again. The collection of these arguments is the most common in the area of return defense. However, it is not always possible to ward off a return with individual sentences. For a successful return deflection, some questions must be clarified in the conversation - What arguments convinced the customer to buy the purchased product? What need should the product have met? Is there a complementary product to quickly fulfill the need?


Ask these questions to get more underrating about customer's concern:

  • What expectations about the product were unmet?
  • How long have you been testing the product?
  • What kind of discomfort is it about?
  • What is the source of the discomfort?
  • What can we do to convince the customer to stay with and trust the product?


Use these arguments for verbal deflection:
