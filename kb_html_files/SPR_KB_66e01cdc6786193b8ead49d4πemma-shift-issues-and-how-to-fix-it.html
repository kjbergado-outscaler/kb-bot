General or chamber issues



Q. What happens if I do not close the valve handle after pushing it?


The air pressure will remain stable, however, to prevent any destabilization of the air pressure, we recommend always closing the valve knob after opening it and setting your desired firmness level. This also prevents any accidental push of the valve.



Q. I feel the firmness level changing after a while even though I locked it at one particular firmness. Why is it so?


As the mattress core works based on air pressure, over time the atmospheric pressure outside can cause a decrease in air pressure inside. We, therefore, recommend resetting the air pressure after a few months. You can do so by simply following the same steps as if you wanted to increase the firmness level.



Q. I cannot feel the firmness level changing from hard to soft while I lay on the mattress. How do I change the firmness level?


The reduction of the air pressure inside the Emma Shift depends on your individual body weight. If you have a lower body weight you can try to sit on the mattress instead of laying on it to create more force for the air to escape.



Q. I still hear air escaping the mattress even after closing the valve. Why?


No air should escape the mattress after closing the valve. However, if the valve itself is not screwed in tightly, the air might escape. Please make sure that you screw in the valve in the valve opening properly before using the mattress.



Q. Once I open the roll-packed mattress, how long can I lay on the Mattress?

After unpacking the roll-packed mattress, it usually takes 30-60 min for it to recover before you should lay on it. You should also screw in the Valve after 30-60 min into the mattress.



Q. I don’t see the mattress core recovering. What do I do?

In case the mattress does not recover after 60 min, keep the valve open and let the core recover for 24 hrs. If after 24 hrs the core does not recover, please contact our customer service.




Smart Valve issues



Q. The Valve is stuck or does allow any air flow. Can I return it?


If it has passed 24 hours and you still feel that is not working correctly, we can test some things to try to identify where the problem is coming from. Please, start by removing the valve entirely and sitting on the mattress, and let's notice what happens:

  • After removing the valve and sitting on the mattress, if it is deflating/inflating, is normal. Then, if you put the valve back and it doesn't inflate anymore, we have a problem with the valve. If this is the case, the valve needs to be replaced it.

  • But if after removing the valve and sitting on the mattress, it is NOT deflating/inflating, then we have a problem with the chambers inside the mattress. In this scenario, the whole mattress needs to be replaced.

We, therefore, recommend resetting the air pressure after a few months*. You can do so by simply following the same steps as if you wanted to increase the firmness level.


* The time depends basically on the weight of the person using the mattress. Generally, we recommend 6 months. But according to the load that the mattress will be subject to, it will be better to do the air "reset between 3-4 months.
