Ergonomics


The healthiest posture for the spine is standing upright. Good mattresses try to bring the spine to a horizontal position during sleep. In the upright position, the vertebrae and intervertebral discs sit on top of each other and distribute the resulting pressure optimally. With load and movement, the intervertebral discs are stressed. Therefore, it is important that the shoulder and pelvis sink into the mattress, the waist finds support and the spine lies straight.



Poor ergonomics on a mattress leads to:


  • Frequent turning on the mattress

  • Back pain

  • Neck pain

  • Tensions

  • Headaches

  • Stress

  • Tiredness

  • Sleeplessness

  • Depression.

Ventilation


A well-ventilated mattress ensures that moisture is better removed and the sleeper has a better microclimate.

Humans lose nearly 1L of body Fluids every night. Half of these are sweating. The other half we lose through the airways.

80% of the sweat-damp is discharged over the duvet. Heavy sweaters should go for a Pocket Spring Mattress, an Emma Diamond, or an Emma Fusion.
