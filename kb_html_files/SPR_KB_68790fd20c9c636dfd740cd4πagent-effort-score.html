Purpose: This article intends to inform UK and Belgium agents of a 10-second ACW survey they will be asked that includes two questions.​ The ES, FR, and PT markets were added.

What is agent effort score:

To measure you efforts while handling customer requests we will now have a questions appearing at the end of the interaction with our customers.

This will only happen to 10% of the cases, so don't expect it to happen regularly.

What is the question?

"On a scale from 1 to 5, how easy was it to resolve this case considering the available tools and processes? (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy)

​

​

​

​

Agents in FR, PT, and ES must now complete the AES survey for every resolved case, following the new structure:

​

Rate the ease of resolution (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy). This is mandatory and must be entered as a digit.

  • Unclear Process

  • System Issues

  • Missing Information

  • Permission Restriction

  • Delayed on Internal Hold

  • Delayed on External Hold

  • Other (only if none of the above apply)

  • Ensure responses are accurate and reflect the true driver of effort.

  • If unsure which driver to select, consult your team lead or refer to the provided examples in the KB.

Why AES Matters:

Boosts Agent Satisfaction

  • High-effort tasks reduce productivity. Lower effort supports a healthier work environment.

Enhances Customer Experience

  • When it’s easier for agents, it’s usually easier for customers too.

Reduces Churn

  • Difficult interactions frustrate both agents and customers, risking loyalty.

Drives Process Improvement

  • AES highlights inefficiencies in systems, enabling targeted optimizations.
