Mattress


Q. What are the main differences between models?

The main features of our mattresses are their height and material.


Basically, the higher a mattress is, the more comfortable it will be. However, this doesn't necessarily mean that the position of the spine improved. The Emma One series, for example, has won several awards globally despite ""only"" being 18 cm high, because of its great lying properties. More height usually means that more material is used to absorb body weight and that the pressure on the mattress is distributed better. Simply put, you'll feel more comfortable. In addition, getting in and out of bed is usually easier on a higher mattress. Our higher mattresses are also often international test winners because they impress with their good lying properties. Emma mattresses are usually either 18cm or 25cm high.


The second difference is the material; our mattresses are either made of a spring core or foam. Foam is usually stronger when it comes to adapting better to the body. Spring cores have their strengths in ventilation, but also in support properties.



Q. Which mattress is ideal for those experiencing neck and back pain?


While the combination of your mattress and pillow is crucial for the ideal position of the spine, a mattress shouldn't be too firm or too soft. Mattresses like the Emma Original Hybrid absorb the more pronounced body contours around your shoulders and pelvis and distribute the pressure. The spring core in the lower part of the mattress supports the curvature of your spine so that it's in an optimal position. For neck problems, the right pillow height is the solution. Try our Emma Original pillow. You can adjust it to suit your needs and preferences with the different inserts it comes with.



Q. Which mattress has the best spinal support?

Emma mattresses have a firm base and become more elastic towards the surface. As a result, light people tend to sink into the upper soft layers, while heavier people are more supported by the base of the mattress. This makes Emma mattresses suitable for a wide range of people. However, if your shoulder area is wider, our sleep experts discovered that a foam mattress like the Emma Original is the better solution; if you have a wider pelvis, Emma Original Hybrid's spring core might suit you more.



Q. What's the difference between a spring core and foam?

Like all materials, foams and innersprings have their own strengths. Foams are more pressure-relieving. If you're a side sleeper and get the feeling that your hands fall asleep, a foam mattress is probably the better solution for you. Innersprings are usually a little firmer and therefore give a little more support. However, the biggest advantage of the innerspring core is that it's more ventilated. If you sweat a lot or live in a very humid climate, the spring core is worth considering.



Q. Do Emma mattresses lose their shape over the years?

We rely on high-performance materials for our mattresses. In our state-of-the-art test lab in Frankfurt, Germany, we test the durability of our materials and how well they go together. If you experience a material failure, our 10-year guarantee will automatically apply.


The 10 year guarante apply for most of the cases, but it can be differ per country. Please make sure to inform the customer according to the market you work for.



Q. What is Diamond Degree and how does it work?

Our normal core body temperature is 37°C. However, the body lowers its temperature by up to 1.5° during the night. This is known as lowered sleep temperature and helps achieve the best possible quality of sleep. The Diamond Degree helps the body to lower the temperature faster. Through millions of tiny graphite particles, the heat from your body that accumulates on the mattress is dissipated and the body reaches the deep sleep phase faster and more efficiently. Diamond Degree is included as a layer in our Diamond Premium Mattresses or you can add a Diamond Degree as Topper to boost your mattress for more deep sleep.



Q. Which mattress has better motion transfer?

If you tend to feel your partner move at night and this wakes you up, we recommend a foam mattress with very high pressure-relieving layers of Airgocell and Viscoelastic foam, such as the Emma Original. These layers are ideal for filtering movement. If you have problems with movement in bed and need more resistance from the mattress to move, you need less pressure-relieving materials. Mattresses from the Emma One series are made with only 3-4 cm of Visco or Airgocell foam, depending on the firmness. This helps you feel better on your mattress.



Q. What do the different firmness levels mean?

For us at Emma, we prioritize ergonomics and the feeling of well-being on the mattress. Ideally, you lie on an Emma mattress so that your shoulders and pelvis sink into the mattress and you are supported where your spine curves; this ensures the best ergonomics. Along with the right pillow, you can combat back pain or prevent it from developing in the first place. There's also your own individual lying sensation, which is why we offer some of our mattresses in different versions. These versions aren't degrees of hardness, but rather lying sensations. So even if you weigh 120 kilograms, you can choose the medium version without any problems. If you lie more on your back or stomach, we recommend a firmer mattress. If you lie more on your side, the mattress should be more elastic and absorb the contours of your body.

Still unsure? Maybe a hybrid mattress is an answer. The firmness of the mattresses is usually in the middle between hard and medium and combines the best of both worlds.

Toppers


Q. What is the added value of a topper?

A topper acts as an increase in the adaptable layers of the mattress and provides better relief for the body. You can also boost your deep sleep with our Diamond Degree Topper. The graphite helps your body reach the ideal sleeping temperature of approx. 35.5 degrees faster and can improve the quality of your sleep in the long run. With two individual mattresses, the topper is also excellent for eliminating the unloved "visitor's crevice".



Q. Should I buy a topper or do I need a new mattress?

If your mattress is still relatively new, but you feel like it's a little too firm, then a topper can be a solution for that. The complementary layers allow your shoulders to sink in better and you immediately feel the pressure relieve once you lay on it. If your mattress shows signs of wear, such as a hollow, the topper is of no use. The topper would adapt to the base of the hollow and would not have enough support to solve this problem. In this case, we'd recommend replacing the mattress.



Q. What are the differences between the topper models?

The most obvious difference is height. The Flip Topper is 5cm while the Diamond Topper is 8cm. The Flip Topper is a reversible topper in the entry-level price range, with two lying sides of different firmness. The benefit for you is that you can turn the topper at any time and adjust it to your needs or preferences. The different layers of Airgocell foam and HRX foam guarantee either a soft or firmer lying position. The Diamond Topper is a premium topper with a layer consisting of millions of tiny graphite particles that help you reach the ideal sleeping temperature in record time. Comparing the two toppers, the Diamond Topper is more supportive and therefore behaves more firmly than the Flip Topper. As an added bonus, the underside of the Diamond Topper is also equipped with an anti-slip coating.



Q. Does the mattress underneath the topper make a difference?

Yes, because like a house, the mattress forms the foundation. Depending on the foundation, the lying properties also change. If the mattress is too firm, the topper helps you lie more comfortably. If you already have your perfect mattress, the topper could potentially have a negative effect, because the shoulder or pelvic area may sink too deeply into the sleep system.



Q. Does a topper make the mattress "hotter"?

This depends more on the material and less on the topper itself. The Emma Diamond Topper, for example, has heat-conducting properties that help the body reach the ideal sleeping temperature in the shortest possible time. Toppers made of Airgocell foam, such as the Flip Topper, also reduce increased sweating because of their open pores. The materials have a restoring force and so the body is not so strongly absorbed by the sleep system. Viscoelastic foam is an exception to this rule. This foam reacts not only to pressure but also to heat which makes it more elastic. Due to the high density of the viscoelastic foam, you would sleep hotter on a topper made of pure visco foam.



Q. In what circumstances would a topper be necessary?

We recommend using a topper with a Boxspring Bed. They have a firmer structure compared to conventional systems of a slatted frame and mattress, so in order to achieve the ideal point elasticity and really get the hotel feeling of the Boxspring Bed, a topper is indispensable.



Q. Are Emma toppers compatible with non-Emma mattresses?

Emma toppers can be used on all mattresses and Boxspring Beds.



Q. What is the difference between a mattress and a topper?

A topper is usually only a fraction of the height of a mattress. This means that a topper cannot replace a mattress, but more of complements it. Emma mattresses are constructed in order to provide support in the lower layers with the use of springs or HRX foam. While the upper layers are made with elastic materials such as Visco foam, Airgocell foam, or Diamond Degree foam to relieve pressure. Our toppers are mostly made of the latter materials on the surface. This improves the pressure-relieving properties and supplements them with other elements such as improving deep sleep. A topper cannot replace a good mattress, but it can improve it.



Q. Does a topper reduce the "efficiency" of a mattress?

It depends. Our toppers are developed to complement the benefits of a mattress. However, it's important to note that not all combinations can be effective to have a topper. For example, if you have a mattress that already has a Diamond Degree layer on the surface, the topper won't be a good option.



Q. What is Diamond Degree foam and how does it work?

The Diamond Degree technology helps the body cool down by itself, without the risk of overcooling. It's designed for all body types and provides full body support; your spine stays aligned whether you are a side, back, or stomach sleeper. After just an hour, you will be in a deep sleep phase and your body temperature will have dropped by 1.5°. On a normal topper, the heat from your body has nowhere to go, making the foam under your body warmer. This can disturb your deep sleep phase and wake you up. The Diamond Degree Topper works to release the heat from the foam under you so that you can maintain a lower temperature and stay in your deep sleep phase longer.



Q. How does the Flip topper work?

The Emma Topper Global fits all mattresses and it's developed to give full body support, keeping your spine aligned no matter what position you sleep in. The Topper adapts to pressure and responds to body movement to give you the ideal comfort during sleep. It's also high-point elastic, which means that the supportive bottom layer helps the body move effortlessly, requiring less strength and effort to turn over while sleeping.

Q. What external influence must be considered before buying a pillow?

The first thing to consider is the body shape of the person, because someone with wide shoulders or simply tall, would need a higher pillow. And for those with smaller frames, a lower pillow would suit them better. A second point that's also important is the mattress. If you have a soft and elastic mattress, which you'd sink deeper into, we'd recommend a flatter pillow. However, if your mattress is a bit harder and you sink just a little, your pillow should be slightly higher.



Q. Which is the best pillow for side/back/belly sleepers?

For side sleepers, who lay slightly elevated, a good option can be the Emma Foam Pillow. For stomach sleepers, who are more likely to sleep flat, the Emma Microfiber pillow can be the best one, when you remove the middle layers. In general, the most ideal position for a night of good sleep is normally the supine position (back sleepers), and for that the Emma Diamond pillow can be the right choice.



Q. Ergonomically speaking, which type of pillow is the ideal one? And why?

The pillows that have more than one layer and allows adjustment are the ideal one. In Emma, we have the Emma Foam, Emma Diamond and the Emma Microfiber. The reason they are better ergonomically is because of the layers that can be removed depending on the person and the sleeping position. For example: One layer is ideal for stomach sleepers that have a soft mattress and very narrow shoulders. Two layers are recommended for side sleepers with small shoulders or side sleepers with wide shoulders but a soft mattresses. Three layers are ideal for side sleepers with wide shoulders and hard mattresses; or back sleepers with reflux problems.

Slatted Frames


Q. Why do I need a new slatted frame?

We'd recommend replacing a slatted frame together with the mattress, as they are a sleeping system. Wooden slatted frames react to moisture and heat and usually deteriorate after 8-10 years. If in your case your slatted frame is broken, we recommend replacing it, because a broken slatted frame can even damage your new mattress.



Q. How are you supposed to set up the adjustable slates?

For sleeping, the adjustable slatted frame should always be set to the flattest level. An exception to this is for people with reflux problems, in which case, it's recommended to set the slat to the lowest notch of the head section. By moving the sliders outwards or inwards, you can change the firmness of the slats. For wider body parts, we recommend moving the sliders inwards. For less pronounced body contours, we recommend setting the slatted frame to a higher support force by moving the sliders outwards.


Pro-tip: Raise the headboard before covering the mattress, it will make things much easier.



Q. How many slats are there in the slatted frame?

The Emma Slatted Frame has 28 wood slats, and the Emma Premium Slatted Frame has 14 slats.



Q. What are the benefits of the Emma Premium Slatted Frame?

In addition to being more adaptable for the sleeper, the Premium Slatted Frame does not react to moisture and heat, giving them a longer service life than wooden slatted frames. We guarantee the service life of this product is 25 years.



Q. Are the slatted frames delivered together with the mattress or separately?

Our slated frames and mattresses are usually produced in different factories, so they often come on different delivery dates.





Duvets


Q. Which is the best size of duvet for my mattress?

The duvet size should be less about the mattress and more about you. Who are plus sizes suitable for? For everyone, of course. Every time you roll over in your sleep, you automatically uncover yourself. In this case, a comfort-sized duvet would prevent this.



Q. What material is the duvet made of?

Our duvets are made out of the same material as sportswear—polyester. The advantage of this is that polyester can dry very fast and ensures a good climate for your body. During the winter, polyester is preferred because it gets warm very fast.



Q. How warm/cold is the duvet?

If you offer both regular & light: More than 60% of people are seasonal sleepers. This means that they want a warm duvet in winter and a cooler one in summer. To make the choice as easy as possible for you, we offer two different temperature levels: Regular and Light duvets. We'd recommend having different duvets for each season, in the same way, people generally have a summer and winter wardrobe.


If you only offer one thickness: The hollow fibers in our duvet allow the filling to stay cool in hot conditions. When it gets cold, these act as insulators, which prevent sweating and keep you comfortable at night.



Q. Which duvet is best for the summer and winter months?

How different temperatures feel is a very individual thing. In perfect circumstances, you can go with one duvet for the whole year. But most people would use the winter duvet from October to February and the summer duvet from March to September.



Q. It is possible to clean the duvet in the washing machine?

Yes, you can machine wash the duvet at a maximum of 60°C. However, do not bleach it, and use only laundry detergent. It's also possible to use the dryer, with a low tumble dry setting.

Q. The mattress protector is noisy, what can I do about this?

Due to the membrane polyester insert, it's not uncommon for the mattress protector to "crackle" during the first few nights. This crackling disappears automatically after a short time, making the friction from sleeping on the cover softer. To speed up this process, you can give the cover another go in the washing machine.



Q. Is a mattress protector necessary? When do I need to buy one?

A mattress protector is a shield between your mattress and things that can shorten its lifespan. We'd consider it as a must-have item to increase the longevity of your mattress. It will protect your mattress from infestations, spills, and allergens and to keep your mattress as clean as possible without washing it. So if you have a dog, or a cat or you just tend to sweat, it's the perfect protection for your Emma mattress.



Q. It is possible to clean the mattress protector in the washing machine? How often should I do it?

You can wash it up to 40°C in a washing machine. Be careful to never put it in hot water when washing it because it can damage the fabric. We'd recommend washing it only once a month unless the protector is visually dirty and compromised. In case something spills or gets on it, it would be ideal to clean the protector before anything spreads to the mattress. Don't dry clean or iron press it.



Q. What mattress sizes are compatible with the mattress protector and is there any limit in terms of height?

We have different mattress protector sizes available that perfectly fit different mattress sizes. The minimum height is 10cm.



Q. What is the difference between a regular mattress cover and an Emma Mattress Protector?

Compared to standard waterproof pads, our mattress protector is unique because it's not only covered with four corner rubbers around the mattress, but it's structured like a fitted sheet, so the fit is much more precise. In addition, we've incorporated membranes into the cover itself. This means that the mattress cover is not only waterproof but also breathable at the same time. Most other mattress protectors use a continuous polyester cover, which causes sweating easily. The Emma mattress protector not only protects your mattress but promotes the ideal sleeping climate for you as well.
