Upselling and cross-selling are two techniques used in sales to increase revenues.


Those techniques offer customers a greater variety of solutions on top of their request, to fully satisfy their needs. However, they should not be confused because they are not the same thing.


Upselling
It means offering the customer a more premium product that has more features and is usually more expensive. For example, the customer wants to buy a printer and you offer them a scanner that is able to fulfill more objectives on top of the printer ones.


Cross-selling
It means offering the customer more products (for example accessories) that can be used in tandem with the main product. For example, the customer wants to buy a notebook and you manage to sell them also a set of pens. These two items are usually sold together and allow the customer to fully fulfill its/her needs.

These strategies are used by many companies to boost revenues. To give an idea of the impact of such techniques Amazon is able to increase its revenues by 35% thanks to the good use of upselling and cross-selling.

However, the main objective of these techniques should be always to increase customer satisfaction. Several studies have been made on the topic. One of them found out that the probability of selling to a new customer is between 5 to 20 percentage points while the probability to sell to an existing customer is between 60% to 70%. What does it mean? It means that upsell and cross-sell are great in increasing revenues because they deal with customers that have already bought or are interested in one of your products. Exploiting this fact, we can be sure that the probability of increasing AOV or convincing them to make another order strongly increases.

The 4 keywords for upselling and cross-selling

NEEDS

It is known that when a customer contacts the company the first thing that should be explored are the needs. Customers call to request advice, relying on the fact that agents, being experts in what they do, will be able to help them in finding the right product to buy. However, needs are not always well defined. For this reason, identifying the real issues and exploiting your knowledge is fundamental, guiding the customer through a route useful in finding out what are the more "ancestral" needs the customer wants to satisfy.

VALUE

The second keyword that can be identified characterizing upselling and cross-selling, and probably the most important one, is value. Everything related to these techniques aims at identifying the best solution for the customer. But how are we able to find the best solution? After having identified a need there could be many different solutions able to tackle that. Reducing the overall choice for the customer, providing a simple, but effective solution is the key to increasing the actual and also perceived value.

What if the need is not that well defined and so it is difficult to find a solution? We should create a need!

How do we do that? Think about all the products Emma offers Every single offer is related to certain needs. For example, pillows can be helpful in improving neck pain. However, seeing one product-related only to certain needs is reductive. We should think of our products as a system. Of course, every product is better at satisfying some issues and has specific characteristics but, at the end, they are all linked! The final aim is to improve people's sleep through everything we can offer. The more products are used by the customer, the more their sleep is going to be improved. Having this view and a complete product's knowledge is fundamental in guiding the customer to ask the right questions. Having a sort of mental, or also written map, that links all the questions that can be used to find upsell and cross-sell opportunities, defining some paths that could be taken to guide the customer.

TIMING

Timing is also very important. Solving one need at a time, proceeding by steps, and not being too pushy is extremely important in conveying value. Remember that we usually have only one opportunity to upsell but multiple opportunities to cross-sell. Upselling usually comes at the beginning while cross-selling usually happens after upselling. Understanding when to end the call is very important, too. We don't want to convey the idea that we want to sell everything at all costs.


TRUST

Since solutions to the customers are our main objective, being trustworthy is often the key to converting the prospect into a customer. Listening carefully to what the customer has to say and showing a deep understanding of the topic we are speaking of, build trustworthy relationships. We need to guide the customer thanks to our expertise and empathy.
