​1. Medical Devices in the Italian Market

​

1.1 What is considered a Medical Device

​

In the Italian market, a medical device (presidio medico sanitario) is a product designed, manufactured, and marketed with the primary purpose of preventing, diagnosing, treating, or alleviating a disease or disability.

For mattresses or beds, this means the product must have specific orthopedic, postural, or therapeutic functions, and it must be certified according to EU Regulation 2017/745 (MDR) with a CE marking as a medical device.

Only products that meet these requirements can be classified as presidi medici and qualify for related tax benefits.

​

​

1.2 Why some products qualify

​

A product can be considered a medical device if:

  • It supports or relieves medical conditions (e.g., back pain, posture correction, pressure sores prevention);

  • It has been tested and approved according to medical device standards;

  • It is listed in the Ministry of Health database as a registered medical product.

If a product does not meet these requirements — even if it offers comfort or ergonomic design — it cannot be sold or promoted as a presidio medico.

​

​

1.3 Tax Benefits for Customers

​

When purchasing a certified medical device, Italian customers may be entitled to specific fiscal advantages:

​

  • VAT Reduction: Customers with disability recognition under Legge 104/1992 (Law 104/1992)* may purchase medical devices with reduced VAT (normally 4% instead of 22%).

    • *Law 104/1992 is an Italian regulation that provides support to people with disabilities. Under this law, individuals with recognized disabilities are entitled to specific tax benefits, which also extend to the purchase of medical devices, including specialized mattresses.

      In the case of mattresses, the purchase of a mattress considered a Medical Device allows the customer to receive a refund of the VAT paid directly from the Italian State.

      ​

  • Tax Deduction (19%): All taxpayers, even without Legge 104/1992, can claim a 19% tax deduction on the total amount spent on medical devices during their annual tax declaration (detrazione fiscale).
