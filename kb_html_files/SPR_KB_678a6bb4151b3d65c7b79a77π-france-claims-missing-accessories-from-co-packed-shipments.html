Introduction

Since November 2024, accessories in France are shipped in packages containing multiple products (toppers are excluded). It can happen that a customer claims that their order was incomplete at delivery.

Below you can find the step-by-step guidance on how to deal with these cases.

Always add at least 4 keywords (two words max to help identify the article).

Examples: replacement; supplement; wrong product; new product; product information

1.     How to identify a copacked order:

A.     Look for the order in Fleunt and click on Fulfillment ID

B.     Check the tracking label: if the tracking is identical for all items, it means that the items were shipped in a single package.

​


2.     Use the tracking label to check the carrier's page:

-         For DPD, visit DPD Tracking.

3.     Compare the recorded weight with the expected weight:

-         Use the weight information provided by the carrier.

-         Compare it with the reference file: Indicative weights of Accessories eligible for copacking .

4.     Calculate the expected weight

-         Consider the total weight of all accessories that should have been included in the shipment.

5.     Take action if there is a discrepancy

-          If there is a clear mismatch between the recorded and expected weights, proceed to either supplement the order or issue a refund, based on the customer's preference.
