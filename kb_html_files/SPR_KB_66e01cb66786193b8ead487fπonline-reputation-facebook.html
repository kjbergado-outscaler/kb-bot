Introduction


This article is meant for the agents working in the Online Reputation team. It summarizes why we use Facebook, how we answer customer queries via this platform, and how to log in Sprinklr.


What is Facebook and why is it important?

Facebook is a website that allows users, who sign-up for free profiles, to connect with friends, work colleagues or people they don't know, online. With Facebook, users can not only post comments and photos, but they can also look through their local news feeds, create live videos, make an event plan, send messages to friends, ask for recommendations, and buy or sell items. Facebook tends to have a wider audience than most social media sites.

Of all the social networks, Facebook is the largest and most recognized social media network worldwide. It exposes our business to the broadest audience and offers the most comprehensive set of business tools. These tools help us build our online brand, create meaningful connections with customers and potential employees.


What does the customer see?

Customers will have access to Emma's Facebook page, all the posts and comments, according to the selected domain.

How to access the Facebook cases

Meta/Facebook is already integrated with Sprinklr, caseswill be created automatically following a customer's direct message or comment on one of our Facebook pages.

What kind of cases can be created

Customers can either send us a Direct message or reply to a post or leave a commemt on Emma page.

How to handle a Facebook ticket (step by step)

The main goal is to provide good customer experience: you are Emma's voice and it falls on you to understand how you can improve this customer experience by empathizing, relate to him and, in the end, provide a solution.

Usually customers who use Social Media platforms such as Facebook are looking for a fast answer, either for pre-sale questions, as for post-sales issues, you will have to assess what the customer needs and provide an answer for it.

How to handle Direct Messages

DMs may lead to a longer interaction than a post comment. The customer is looking for more than just a one shot conversation, and not just stating something, he is looking for a reply.

When you are replying you can see the conversation history and a broader view over the issue. If the customer provided his email and that information was already logged in Freshdesk, you will be able to see all his information, such as previous orders and/ or tickets.


In the Reply box, besides being to write a reply and post it, you will also have canned responses, you can attach files, look for suggestions, and if, that is the case, send a link for a survey.


Keep in mind that this is a public platform and all information is easily sharable or made public, remember to use tone of voice and comply with GDPR guidelines.



How to handle Comments on Posts


Unlike DMs, posts are visible to everyone, they automatically portray customers' feelings and how we react to either good and bad feedback.


In the same way, our reply will also be public, so it is a good opportunity to turn a bad experience into a better one and to showcase our care in customer service to other people who will visit the page.



As in other platforms, positive comments are easier to handle, usually there's no further intervention needed than to appreciate the customers' feedback, thanking for choosing Emma's products and the effort it took him to publicize the good experience.
So, what to do here:

  1. In your reply be empathetic, address the customer by name, thank the customer for choosing our brand and appreciate his effort

  2. If there are additional questions make sure to provide answers or solutions

  3. If needed, ask the customer to send us a DM so you can take the topic on a private channel

  4. You can use emojis to easily portray emotions

  5. Create your own set of canned responses so it will be easier and faster to reply to simple situations and keep using different replies and portray a more customized approach.


In contrast, negative comments will most likely always demand further analysis and a follow up. Customers will use FB exposure to force us to provide a faster and a more convenient solution.
when you encounter a negative comment always:

  1. Keep Emma's tone of voice, address the customer by its name, acknowledge the issue and sympathize with his problem

  2. Request the customer to send more details via DM so you can analyze the situation more thorough. Add the customer email to the ticket for Freshdesk to integrate previous tickets and orders information to the customer block.

  3. You can still use emojis but more soft as the customer is already unhappy and requesting immediate

  4. Canned responses do not work as well here as you will have to adapt to multiple situations


Once again, is crucial that you remember of the importance of a good Online Reputation and that you will publicly represent Emma.
