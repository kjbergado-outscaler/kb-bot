What happens when you are sleep-deprived?




Not getting enough sleep leads to sleep debt, which is the difference between the amount of sleep we get and the amount we need.


Consequences


Find below some consequences of it:


Sleep and obesity

  • Lack of sleep alters levels of hormones that affect appetite and cause metabolic changes that can increase the predisposition to obesity.
  • In fact, individuals who sleep less than 7 hours per night are more likely to have higher average body mass indexes and to develop obesity than those who slept for more than 7 hours.


Sleep-Immune crosstalk

  • Lack of sleep will suppress the immune system.
  • In fact, people who sleep six hours or less a night are four times more likely to catch a cold when exposed to the virus, compared to those who sleep more than seven hours a night.


Sleep to ward off diseases

  • Inadequate sleep can raise the risk of many diseases including cancer, hypertension, coronary diseases, stroke, depression, and diabetes.


Sleep and accidents

  • Drowsy driving has been associated with a shortened lifespan due to high risk of car crashes. Sleeping six hours per night can increase your risk of having a car crash by 33% compared to sleeping seven or eight hours a night.


Sleep and memory

  • Without getting the adequate amount of sleep, it is much harder for our brain to absorb, process, and recall new information.
  • Sleep deficits further lead to impaired judgments and poor vigilance and concentration.


Sleep and mood

  • Sleep deprivation may lead to irritability, impatience, and moodiness.
  • Sleep abnormalities co-occur with various mental disorders, such as anxiety disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder, depression or bipolar disorder.




Causes


Personal choice

Some people don’t realize that the body needs adequate sleep, and prefer staying up late.


Illness

Illness such as colds and tonsillitis can cause snoring, gagging and frequent waking and effect directly on sleep


Sleep disorder

This includes sleep apnea, snoring and periodic limb movement disorder can disturb a person´s sleep.


Work

Jobs that have shifts or frequent travels can disrupt the sleep-wake cycles on a regular basis. Airline crew tends to have erratic sleeping patterns


Poor sleep hygiene

Drinking coffee or smoking cigarettes close to bedtime habit can stimulate the nervous system and makes you sleepless.


The sleeping environment

sleeping in an environment such as a room that is too hot or cold, noisy neighbors, and a snoring partner can disrupt your sleep.


Medications

To treat epilepsy or ADHD, the drugs that are used can cause insomnia.


Babies

Parents mostly experience sleep deprivation because their babies are frequently waking up in the night for feeding or comfort.



Sleep Problems and Disorders

Sleep problems or sleep disorders refer to conditions that affect sleep quality, timing, or duration.


Sleep problems can be triggered by numerous factors, including psychological (e.g. stress, anxiety, loneliness, high-performance pressure), physiological (e.g. problems breathing or other illnesses), and environmental (e.g. aging, jet lag, ambient temperature, diet).
