Article Summary

In this article, you will learn about how to do Ticket Categorization and which are the correct options when filling in the field Category on the ACW window.

Reminder: It is MANDATORY to categorize the tickets.

Ticket Categories and Sub-categories

We have 15 Categories as the main options for you to choose from. After selecting one, you will have a Category-related (Level 2) to mark, and in some cases still a Sub-Category (Level 3).

The main categories are:

  • Product Information;

  • Services & store information;

  • Ordering issues;

  • WIMO (Where is my Order?);

  • Edit order;

  • Payment;

  • Invoice;

  • Cancellation;

  • Product Claim;

  • Logistic Claim;

  • Return;

  • Refunds;

  • Injury Claim;

  • Legal & Privacy;

  • Others.

A customer is unhappy with a significant

Refunds -> Inquiries and issues related to refunds, discounts, friend referral requests,
and price adjustments.

 

Sub-category
 (Level 2)


Description


Examples


Dependent
 field (level 3)


Refund status



Customer inquires about the status or
details of an ongoing refund process.



- Customer requests the status of their
refund after returning an item; 


- Customer asks for the date their refund
was processed; 


- Customer inquires about the payment
method used for the refund or proof of the refund;


- Customer seeks clarification on the
monetary compensation they were promised due to a product or logistic issue;


- Customer wants to know if their price
match request was approved and how it will affect their refund.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Refund issues



Customer encounters issues with their
refund, such as incorrect or delayed refunds.



- Customer claims they received an
incorrect refund amount (over or under the expected amount);


- Customer reports not receiving a full
refund; 


- Customer complains that their refund is
taking longer than the promised timeline;


- Customer states that they were charged
a restocking fee that was not previously disclosed.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Friend referral



Customer wants to participate in the
referral program and requests the promised bonus.



- Customer wants to know how to receive
their referral bonus;


- Customer requests assistance with
redeeming their referral bonus;


- Customer inquires about the referral
bonus amount and applicable terms;


- Customer asks for help tracking the
status of their referral bonus after referring a friend.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Price match



Customer requests a price adjustment due
to a sale, promotion, or retailer's lower price.



- Customer asks for a retrospective price
match due to a sale or promotion;


- Customer requests a price match because
a retailer is selling the same product at a lower price;


- Customer notices a lower price on the
company's website shortly after placing their order and requests a price
adjustment.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Retrospective discount application



Customer requests his refund related to
discount code that (s)he forgot to apply or that has not worked, as
previously aligned with the CS team.



- Customer forgot to apply a coupon code
and asks for the respective discount;


- Discount code did not work in the
check-out process and it was aligned with customer service to apply the
discount retrospectively after the order was placed. 



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Others

(A comment explaining the contact reason is mandatory) 



Inquiries that do not fit into any other
sub-category.







Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field


 

 

 

 

Others -> Any other contact reason that does not fit the main categories or
their sub-categories, including spam messages, duplicate or merged tickets,
data deletion requests (GDPR), and customer feedback.

 

Sub-category
 (Level 2)


Description


Examples


Dependent
 field (level 3)


GDPR requests



The customer makes a Data Subject Request
(article),
asking for data access, data deletion, etc, in compliance with GDPR
regulations.



Or the customer contacted us to report a data breach.



Customer sends an email requesting for
all his data to be deleted; 


 


Customer submits a request through a web
form for data access. 




Customer had his order or private data shared incorrectly with a third party.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field



Spam Messages



The contact is clearly spam and will not
be acted upon. This includes unsolicited advertisements, phishing attempts,
or irrelevant content.



Unsolicited promotional emails;


 


Messages containing suspicious links;


 


Irrelevant or nonsensical content.



N/A 



Duplicate or Merged Tickets



The ticket has already been resolved in a
different ticket or merged into an existing ticket. This sub-category is used
to keep track of redundant tickets.



Customer submits multiple requests for
the same issue;


 


Two different agents are working on the
same issue, and the tickets need to be merged. 



N/A



Customer Feedback



Customers provide general feedback
(positive or negative) about Emma, which does not necessarily require
follow-up action. This includes feedback sent via social media, email, or
other channels.



A customer shares their positive
experience with Emma's products on social media



N/A



Collaboration Opportunities



Inquiries about potential collaborations,
partnerships, sponsorships, or affiliate opportunities with Emma Sleep.



Also, all communication with charity partners about organizing
returns-donations.



Influencer collaborations; 


 


Charity partnerships; 


 


Co-branding opportunities. 



N/A



Others

(A comment explaining the contact reason is mandatory) 



The customer is contacting us for a
reason that does not fit into any other main or sub-category. A comment
explaining the contact reason is mandatory, clarifying why the ticket could
not be categorized elsewhere.



Mandatory to also fill in the Order
 number field
