Summary of the product


The Emma Motion Base is another product of the new technology line of Emma's Smart Products. It is an adjustable base suitable for both Emma Motion Shift and Emma Motion Sense mattresses, however, you can combine it with any mattress.


In addition to supporting the mattress, the Emma Motion Base is a smart slat base that ensures sleep tracking of almost medical quality. Together with the tracking, the slat base offers other comfort options such as a massage function or an anti-snoring function. A person can adjust the structure of the bed by remote and app control, in order to help, for example, with muscular issues and keep a track of vital functions. Our goal is to guarantee the best possible comfort and to bring sleeping comfort to a new level.




How Emma Base works


The Emma Base combines 3 technological features: Sleep tracking; Well-being features, and the Emma Sleep Assistant App.

Sleep tracking

The Emma Motion Base bed base understands your needs without words. Because with it, you can call up all your favorite relaxation positions at the touch of a button during the day, activate the absolutely weightless floating mode at any time, or have a massage. At night, the Emma Motion Base monitors your sleep and also wakes you up fully automatically at the desired time with your preferred wake-up position. In addition, it gives you the opportunity to get to know your vital functions along with your sleeping habits and to counteract these in the event of a sleep disorder. Thanks to the flowing Ergo movements, the Emma Motion Base works almost silently and imperceptibly.

Well-being features

The adaptive technology of the Emma Motion Base enables effective relaxation even when awake. Three main functions ensure this: Activate the Muscle Relaxation Mode and low-noise, high-frequency vibration units start massaging your neck and feet. You can choose between three intervals and three intensities. For total relaxation, the Floating Mode puts you in a state of weightlessness that is easy to circulate. With the anti-snoring function, the Emma Motion Base recognizes when you are snoring, even when you are reading or watching television. Other abnormalities in your sleep behavior such as cardiac arrhythmias or breathing pauses are also recorded and can be shared with a doctor via the app if desired. The best prerequisites for feeling carefree during the day.


Emma Sleep Assistant App

The Emma Motion Basep's sleep tracking technology makes interactive real-time sleep monitoring possible. It provides you with data of almost medical quality every night - and analyses it too. All results are available to you at any time via the app. Even third parties can be automatically informed about your sleep behavior on request, without them being on site. But the best thing is that Sleep Tracking gently intervenes in your sleep when unhealthy situations arise due to snoring. In such cases, it initiates a correction of your lying position and returns to the original position as soon as the situation is defused. For you, this means an enormous plus in comfort, vitality, and safety.

The Sleep Assist App


Once you have registered and paired your Emma Motion Base with the Sleep Assist App, you will be automatically referred to the start page of the app. With the app you can control the bed base in the same way as with the remote control, but via the app you can access unique functionalities and the true smartness of this bed base.


For example, there is a sensor in the bed base, located under the chest area, that reads the heart rate and breathing rate based on the principle of Ballistocardiography (BCG). The sensor analyzes your breathing and heart rate patterns and builds sleep stages from this information. With this, you can also easily share through email with your doctor in case you see some abnormalities. Abnormal breathing and heart rate might indicate several things, for example, that you are getting sick before you feel any symptoms.


App features


The principal features of the App are: Sleep score; Sleep duration; Snore intervention times; Average respiratory rate; Average heart rate; Body movements count; Real-time data; Sleep care; Alarm position preset.



Sleep score


This is an indicator of the user's overall sleep quality, ranging from 0 to 100. A number between 80 and 100 is a good goal. Total sleep is the most important factor, with sleep efficiency, time spent in different sleep phases and the number of wakeups contributing as well. A good goal is a consistent score of 80 or higher.


  • 90 -> Sleep very well, continue to keep;
  • 80 -> General quality of sleep;
  • 70 -> Need to pay attention;
  • 60 or less -> Poor sleep.




Sleep duration


It measures the total sleep time, including time spent in different sleep phases, including time asleep and time awake.


Snore intervention times

The Emma Motion Base allows not only detects snoring, but actively works to prevent it. So, when the bed base detects that the breathing slows down in a particular pattern, it detects snoring and lifts the head area just a little bit to help you clear your airways. When the breathing normalizes, it lowers the head area to the normal position. The headboard will raise very lightly and will not wake the user. So, this feature will track the number of times that the system triggered the snore intervention function. It’s possible for the user to turn off this feature.


Average respiratory rate and


It is the number of breaths the users takes per minute. For healthy individuals between the ages of 16 and 65, a resting respiratory rate between 10-22 breaths per minute is considered normal.


Average heart rate


It represents the average value in beats per minute over the duration of the user's sleep recording. A heart rate between 40 and 85 is considered healthy. An average of the number of heartbeats per minute. In adults, the normal heart rate is 60 – 120 in a quiet state.


Body movements count


This function will be possible to count and understand the number of times that the body moves during sleep.


Real-time data


The app tracks and generates information like near-medical data in real-time. If any abnormality is detected, the system informs you and your family.


Sleep care


This feature allows one user to know the sleep status of another family member, even in a different residence.


Alarm position preset


The user can set the alarm individually for the side of the bed. At a specified time the bed will gently vibrate or lift the head part to wake up the user, without waking up the other person.





App buttons


Inside the App is possible to see 4 button options: "Home", "Data", "Remote" and "My Bed". Also, there is an icon of a person in the top left corner that drives to the "Profile".




On the principal page of the app, the option named "Home", shows the Sleep Overview where is possible to check the principal data, for example: total sleep time last night and some patterns (heart rate; respiratory rate; snoring; body turnovers). It shows the most recent values of the past 10 seconds under real-time data.


On the “Data” tab, it's possible to go into all of the features mentioned above. Also, the user can review the information by day, by 7 days and even check the sleep quality for the past 30 days.


The next option is the "Remote" that allows changing the positions of the bed per area: head, lumbar and foot. For example, elevating the head to watch TV or to read; or elevating the legs to help blood circulation. There is already a preset preprogrammed on the remote to set you into the best position for relaxing: it’s called “Zero G”. This preset is verified by research to give you the best position to impose the least strain and relax your muscles. Additionally, it's also possible to set up a massage for 10, 20, or 30 minutes. This is a nice option if the user wants to relax before going to sleep and doesn't have to worry about turning it off since it will do it automatically.


Finally, the last button is the "My Bed" that will be more used for those who purchase the double bed (2 Emma Motion Bases), since allows adjustment to only one side of the bed, without harming the setting of the other user.

Safety measures


To reduce the risk of electrical shocks, burns, fire or injuries:

Connect the bed base to an overvoltage protection device (not included in the delivery) for the optimal level of safety and optimal operation. The bed base should be plugged only directly into a wall socket or an overvoltage protection device (strongly recommended). Always pull the mains plug out of the socket before you perform maintenance on parts of the base. To reduce the risk of electrical shock, pull out the main plug from the socket before cleaning. For safe disconnection, ensure that the base is in a flat position and all motors are switched off, and then pull the mains plug out of the socket. Keep the main cable away from heated surfaces. Never operate the Emma Motion Base if the air openings are blocked. Keep the air openings clear of lint, hair and similar. Do not let objects fall into an opening and do not put objects into them. Stop using the bed base and contact an authorized service centre if a cable or plug of the base is damaged or if it does not work correctly or has fallen into the water. Use this bed base only for the intended use as described in this manual. Do not use accessories/add-ons that are not recommended by the manufacturer. An in-depth inspection is required if the bed base is used by or near children, convalescent patients, handicapped persons, or pets. Incorrect connection of the device can result in electrical shocks, electrical fires, or faulty operation of this bed base. If the plug does not fit into the socket, please contact a qualified electrician to have him install a suitable socket. Unauthorized modification of a wall socket or overvoltage protection device or not using overvoltage protection may invalidate the part of your warranty that applies to the electronics.


Pets and children:

Dispose of all packaging materials immediately, as they can represent a risk of suffocation to small children and pets. To prevent injuries, it is not recommended to permit children and small pets to play on or under the bed. Children should not use the Emma Motion Base without supervision by an adult.


Pacemakers:

Some products contain neodymium magnets, which might interfere with devices such as pacemakers, intensive care instruments, and other devices that are sensitive to magnetic fields. It is STRONGLY recommended that the owners of such medical devices consult with their physician before using products containing neodymium magnets. It is also possible that some pacemakers interpret the optional vibrations of the massage functions incorrectly as a movement/physical activity. This occurs frequently with products that generate a vibrating motion, which might not interfere with the pacemaker. Please consult with your doctor before you use the massage function.


Additional safety requirements:

Rollers (optional leg accessories) are equipped with brakes to prevent unintended movements. Even if they are locked and cannot roll, the rollers may slide. Head and foot motors with enabled activation serve for lowering the mattress by running them in only with the force of gravity and never by actively being pulled down. This helps minimize crushing points.


Lifting/lowering mechanisms:

The lifting/lowering function exerts a low humming noise during operation. This is normal. During the operation, the lifting wheels touch the platform support of the base. This can result in metal contacting metal or metal contacting plastic. It causes a slight tension of the movable components and the resonance is reduced to a minimum. If excessive noises or vibrations occur, please revert the movement of the base (to the top or bottom) by means of the remote control. This should align the activation mechanisms of the base to the correct operating position. In normal operation of the base, the wheels, which allow the bed to keep a distance from the wall, contact the steel platform supports of the base, which causes a contact noise. When weight is placed on, removed from, or shifted on the base, this can make an audible contact noise when the wheels make contact. This is normal.


Materials:

The adjustable Emma Motion Base comprises various materials, among them wood, metals, plastics and fabrics. Tension, pressure, or motion work on the frame.


Home use and hospital standards:

The adjustable Emma Motion Bases are designed exclusively for home use. The sprung base was not designed as a hospital bed and does not meet hospital standards. Please do not use this sprung base with oxygen therapy instruments for which tents are used or anywhere near explosive devices.


Massage mode and setup site:

The massage function makes a noise during the operation. This is normal. With the increasing intensity of the massage, the noise gets louder. The volume of this sound directly depends on the setup site. If, for example, the demonstration takes place in an exhibition room, the environmental noises cover some of the massage noise. If the bed is installed in a domestic setting on wooden floors, with or without carpeting, the massage noise is louder in contrast. To minimize this resonance, place carpet or rubber bumper feet under each leg or each roller of the base. If the fastening screws are not tightened firmly, vibrations or noises can be caused by the outer bedframe, the head part support, and the assembly bolt of the head or foot parts. The massage intensity of levels 1 to 3 represents the rotations of the motor per minute. Level 3 means the maximum rotation speed. However, this does not mean that the vibrations are felt the strongest. Depending on the material and the thickness of the mattress, as well as the position of the user, the vibrations are felt differently. We recommend the user find the best-suited setting for the massage intensity. It is important to understand the physics of oscillations. In the same way as a motor can run unsteadily idle, also the vibrations change with increasing rotation speed.


Tolerance:

The Emma MOTION Base is designed and manufactured so it works within the defined quality control parameters. The base is subject to careful and strict inspections during the quality control process to ensure that it works within these standards when operated normally. Normal use for regular operation is defined as follows: The adjustable base is to be operated with a mattress and a load. Test procedures require lifecycle endurance tests with a distributed load of up to 120 kg. The angle for different configurations of the mattress support platform between the rear section and the flat section must be ± 3°, which is equivalent of tolerance of up to 4 cm. The angle for different configurations of the mattress support platform between the flat section and the foot section must be ± 3°, which is equivalent of tolerance of up to 2 cm. An adjustable base conforms to the manufacturer’s quality control standards when it can be assumed that it will stay within these attributed tolerances

“New product smell”:

Some people notice a slight odor coming from their new adjustable base – the source of this smell can by our manufacturing process. This odor is normal and usually disappears before you receive the product. However, sometimes the small can still be noticed. It normally disappears after a few weeks if the product and the surroundings are ventilated sufficiently.


Breaking-in period:

You can shorten the breaking-in period by simply spending time in the adjustable bed, for example, to read, watch television or play with your children or grandchildren. The additional movement helps the adjustable base to respond to weight and temperature changes. Some of the materials in our adjustable bases such as wood, metals, and plastics that are used for the platforms, the frame and the components respond sensitively to moisture concentration and temperature and can cause noises in colder environments.

Data security & Remote Control

Many customers are concerned about their data security. Emma takes this issue very seriously. Therefore, the Base app has been designed in such a way that no data transfer takes place between the customer's mobile device and Emma. This means that the app works like a remote control. In addition to the change from automatic to manual, as well as a detailed setting of the desired hardness, the sensitivity of the sensors can also be changed via the app.


If you want to be on the safe side, or if you don't have a mobile device, the Emma Motion Base also comes with wireless remote control. Sleep Tracking without the app is not possible.
