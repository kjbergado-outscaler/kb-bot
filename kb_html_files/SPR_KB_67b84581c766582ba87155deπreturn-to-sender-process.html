Return; Return to Sender; Unable to Deliver; Delivery Rejection

In this article you'll find information about how to deal with customers reaching out about fulfillments with a Return to Sender status (often associated with Late Cancellations, Rejected Delivery or Unable to Deliver cases).

Definition

A shipment is considered returned to the sender when the delivery failed, and the carrier was not able to complete successfully the shipment. An order can be sent to the sender (fully or partially) for several reasons:

  • Late cancellation : customers changed their mind about the purchase when it was too late to cancel it on Fluent.

  • Delivery rejected: customer could reject the delivery for the reason above or because they were not the recipient of the order.

  • Impossibility to find the address/ customer not home: some details were wrong or missing, or customer was not at home when the delivery was.

For all the scenarios above, we are willing to refund customers for the value of the order that they haven’t received. As this is not an automated process, it’s important for the customer support agent to identify correctly these cases to provide a seamless and smooth refund experience for our customers. For all the scenarios above, no supplement order should be created.

Exceptions

  • If it’s a return to sender case and the customer still wants the order, claiming they didn’t receive any information about the delivery or attempted delivery, please check Parcel Lab, the Order Status Page, and the tracking link to verify what information the carrier has provided. If the carrier did failed to provide clear information to the customer or in the system, proceed with the supplement process under the Logistic Claim flow (it can be due to lost parcel or carrier misbehaviour if it's not customer's fault).

  • If you feel like your customer has an exceptional situation and needs further escalation, please with your Supervisor if you should involve Emma Internal team.   

Instructions

  1. Validate the fulfillment status

Most of the cases, when an order is returned to the sender it will be reflected on Fluent, under the fulfillment tab with the input “return_to_sender_complete” (it will appear on where there's the "picked_up" highlighted in green).

If this status is not updated on Fluent always make sure to check the tracking page of the carrier , to confirm if, at least on their website, the information is updated.

  • If it is (carrier page confirms the return was return to sender), please take a screenshot that will be needed to inform Order Management (OM) team (delivery rejected, returned to sender) to cancel the order/fulfillment.

  • If is not, and the tracking doesn’t show the order as returned, please contact the carrier to have updates on this delivery. Only when you receive a written confirmation that the order was returned to the sender, then you can move forward with opening the Jira to correct team.

Closing the loop

If you’ve followed correctly the process, OM and Refund team will take care of the refund request. At this point, you can simply inform customers that the refund request has been triggered, and you can close the case.
