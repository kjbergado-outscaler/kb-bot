Intro

The goal of this page is to help you understand how cases move between teams and stakeholders, so you always know who’s responsible for what and what to expect at each step. You’ll find a breakdown of the most common types of tickets and escalations, who owns each one, and how the workflow works behind the scenes.

With this info, you'll be able to:

  • Escalate cases to the right teams faster;

  • Avoid back-and-forth or delays;

  • Make sure customers get the help they need, quicker.

Think of this as your go-to guide whenever you're unsure where a case should go or what happens after you escalate it.

What is an escalation and what are the escalation types?

A ticket escalation is when a customer support case (or ticket) is passed on to another team, person, or level of support because it couldn’t be fully resolved at the current level. Bellow you'll find the most common escalation types:

Type of escalations

Definition

Escalated cases

Occurs when a customer cannot resolve their issue with customer service and requests higher-level intervention (ex. ask for a supervisor on and on, express extreme dissatisfaction on +3 emails, and threten us to go to press, overly rejects the the given solution - 4 emails, etc).

Legal Cases

Cases already involving legal action or reputational risks (ex. court letters, lawyers contacting Emma, GDPR breaches and cases that seem to require urgent attention from higher management or legal teams).

Complex Cases

Happens when a customer’s issue remains unresolved despite repeated contact, requiring intervention from other teams beyond the initial support level (exceptional situations: tried to follow standard process multiple times and it's not working, lack of information on Knowledge base to address the case, lack of responsiveness of internal team, possible errors from internal teams).

Examples:

- Logistic claim/product claim/refunds/returns/order delays that we don't have a solution and issue is on hold > 5 days.

- Tried to supplement an SKU more than one time and the wrong product arrives

- Quality issues that are persistent on the same product again and again, etc.
