Introduction and disclaimer

This article is meant to act as a guideline on what we expect a live channel contact with Emma to look like. However, it is not meant to be seen as a set of restraints, but rather a platform on which agents can build upon.

Our main goal will always be to have our live contacts come as close as possible to an actual conversation between two human beings.

Hence, why allowing agents to find their voice and be naturally empathic is so important. We want contacts to be fluid and personalized.

Emma is a very agile company, so the agents representing us should never be (or sound) rigid.

This article is applicable for phone contacts, chat contacts, direct messages on Instagram and Facebook.

Live contacts structure breakdown

The five major dividers in a chat and phone contact are:

1. Introduction

* Opening the contact *

On a chat contact

At this stage of the contact, the agents should:

  • Greet the customer;

  • Introduce themselves;

  • Thank the customer for contacting us;

  • Get the customer’s first and last name (if this wasn’t already provided to the chat bot);

  • Rephrase the customer's issue/query to make sure everything is clear and that the queries were understood correctly, and offer/promise to help.


Notes: How the agent does this, what sentences are used to accomplish these goals and in what order these take place, all of that is entirely up to the agent. Always greet the customer; by the first name, unless the customer's username on the platform is a nickname. In those circumstances just use a generic greeting without using any name.

Key words: welcoming, friendly and helpful.

2. Information gathering

* Framing the problem *

​

On a chat contact

At this stage of the contact, the agent should, in order, do the following:

1) Authenticate the customer following G.D.P.R. (if the contact concerns an existing order);

2) Gather all relevant information regarding the customer’s queries, if this has not already been provided to the chat bot;

3) Show empathy if the situation calls for it;

Notes: Agents should not be afraid to ask que customer questions. Until everything is crystal clear for the agents, they can (and should) ask the customer for further clarification.

Key words: inquisitive, thorough and meticulous.

3. Investigation

* Checking and verifying the facts *

On a chat contact

At this stage of the contact, the agent should, in order, do the following:

1) Ask the customer to hold while we look into his query;

2) Consult all relevant tools regarding the customer’s queries;

3) Clarify with the customer any doubts that may have come up regarding the situation at hand;

4) Decide on a course of action in order to assist the customer;

5) Take all relevant actions (internal notes, escalations, etc) using our tools.

Notes: Customers should not be holding for more than 4 minutes without the agent getting back to them. If the agent feels the situation will take a long time to investigate, the customer should be informed of this and given the option of receiving a follow-up contact later in the day.

Key words: investigative, analytical and diligent.

4. Solution

* Providing a resolution *

On a chat contact

At this stage of the contact, the agent should, in order, do the following:

1) Thank the customer for holding while the agent looked into his query;

2) Explain what happened and (if possible and applicable) why it happened;

3) Inform what was done on our end and what the next steps are;

4) Set expectations regarding timelines (when applicable);

5) Educate the customer on our processes and procedures (when applicable), in order to avoid repeated contacts for issues the customer could have tackled by himself/herself.


Notes: Clarity is key at this stage, everything should be explained thoroughly to the customer and we should make sure evertyhing was understood on his end.

Key words: clear, structured and reassuring.

5. Closure

* Giving an open-ended farewell *

On a chat contact

At this stage of the contact, the agent should, in order, do the following:

  1. Offer availability to assist with other queries;

  2. Thank the customer for contacting us;

  3. Pitch the survey (optional);

  4. Bid the customer farewell in a pleasant way.

* No signature required.

Key words: warm, available and polite.

Engagement opportunities

* Making connections *

Opportunities:

  • Agents should always make sure to adapt to the customer. Although our approach should be informal and fluid, if we encounter a customer that doesn’t respond well to this, we should change our approach to suit that customer’s needs (Ex: A customer that wants to be addressed as “Mr. Smith”, instead of “John”). Research has shown that messages that fit with a customer’s attitudes and dispositional motives are processed more fluently and evaluated more positively than incongruent messages.

  • Always look one step ahead. Customer interactions unfold like a chess match. If we know a certain action on our end will cause a reaction on the customer’s side, agents should make sure to take that into account when planning their next steps. Providing context and explaining why things are how they are, will always go a long way. Transparency is key, if we want our customers to trust us.

  • If a customer is excited about a product or a resolution, we should try to match their energy in our responses. On a phone call, intonation is key for this. On a chat, using appropriate punctuation marks and choosing the right words will be fundamental throughout the contact.


  • During a chat, if the customer is using emojis and the context is favourable, or the situation was resolved with a positive outcome for the customer, agents can, in moderation, use emojis as well. This will act as positive reinforcement for the interaction and will enhance the connection between customer and agent. When using emojis, if, by default, the platform does not allow for full, colourful emojis during text entry, type Windows logo key + . (period). The emoji keyboard will appear.

Things to avoid

* Dodging communication traps *

Things to avoid:

  • Agents should always make sure to say and spell the customer’s name correctly. This is a huge red flag and will immediately convey a poor image of our service standards. Customers should always be treated with respect.

  • Avoid repetition. Starting most sentences using the same words (Ex: We... We... We...) or using the same expressions multiple times (Ex: Please note... Please note...) will just reinforce the negative bias, many customers have nowadays, that CS agents are just copy/pasting templates.


  • When something goes wrong, focus on the solution, not on who is to blame for an issue. We are one team, we should all be pulling in the same direction.

  • Do not use unnecessary branding. It is fine to refer to products using our brand name (Ex: “Emma One mattress”). However, we should not be overusing the brand name on spoken or written communication.

  • Negative reinforcement should be avoided at all costs. Acknowledging that something did not go according to plan is one thing, reinforcing the negative aspect of a customer’s experience with us is another altogether. Always try to focus positively on the solution and what we will now do for the customer.

  • Information should be consistent. Agents should not provide conflicting instructions or guidelines to a customer.

  • When asking for additional information from a customer, agents should always make sure they request everything they need at once. If we have to ask the customer for more information/pictures afterwards, this will just cause frustration and create the belief that we are artificially blocking their path towards a solution.

What to do when the customer is not replying?

* How to proceed with unresponsive customers *

If a customer is not replying to us on a chat conversation we will send two messages:

1) After 3 minutes of not receiving a reply or message from our customer, we will send a message saying "[Insert customer's first name], are you still connected?".

2) After sending the above message we will wait an additional 2 minutes for the customer to reply.

3) However, if after these 5 minutes (3 + 2) the customer still did not reply, we will send the following message "Unfortunately, since we have not received any message from you in the last 5 minutes, we will need to close this chat. Nevertheless, if you still need our support, please feel free to reach out to us again via chat or through email: [insert market customer support email address]. Thank you and have a nice day." and close the chat.

* NOTE: The above is not applicable at the end of a contact, when the agent asks if there is anything else we can assist the customer with. On that occasion, the agent should only wait 2 minutes and, if there is no reply from the customer, they should close the chat without further delay.

What to do when the customer is is being disrespectful and/or overly aggressive?

* How to proceed with disrespectful customers *

If a customer is being disrespectful and/or overly aggressive agents should do the following:

1) After trying to calm the customer down, the agent should provide 1 warning: "I understand that this situation might be frustrating for you, and I am here to help. However, I must ask that we maintain a respectful tone during this conversation. If you continue to behave this way, I will be forced to terminate this call/chat".

2) If the customer accepts the agent's advice, the call/chat will continue. If the customer does not, and continues to be disrespectful or overly aggressive the agent should say "I am sorry, but given what I previously mentioned, I will now terminate this call/chat".

3) In case the customer contacts again after that and behaves appropriately, the agent that takes the contact should handle it normally. In case the customer continues to behave disrespectfully, the new agent should follow the steps on points 1 and 2. At this point it is recommended that the case/customer is transferred to a Supervisor/Team Lead.
