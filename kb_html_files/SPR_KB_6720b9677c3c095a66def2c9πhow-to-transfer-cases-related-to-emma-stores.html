Introduction

This article focuses on transferring Emma Store cases to the designated Store Agents. Please have a look at the scenarios in which you have to transfer a case to the Store Agents and also how to do this action on the system.

ATTENTION: We only have Emma Stores in the following countries: PT, ES, DE, NL and UK. In case you need more information about the Stores, please check the articles HERE.

When to transfer?

If you are a CS agent who handles only orders bought directly from Emma's website and received a customer request related to an order bought at one of the physical Emma Stores in Europe. In those situations, you must transfer the case to Store agents by following the indications presented below.

IMPORTANT: If you need to transfer the case, but it is NOT an Emma Store related topic, please follow the instructions in the article "How to: Transfer Cases".

How to transfer cases to Emma Store support-agents?

DISCLAIMER: The Emma Store support-agents can only handle Email cases, due to this we need to make sure the cases are transfered following the guidelines below to guarante they will be properly handled.

1) For Email cases:

If you receive a customer email with a request related to Store order, just transfer to the correct Line of Business (LOB) by clicking on "Apply Macro" and then on "Change LOB":

2) For Live Chat or Social Media cases:

  • Step 1: You must handle the customer interaction and explain to the customer that their order was purchased at an Emma Store, and due to this, the request needs to be handled by the designated support team, and for this you will transfer the case internally and the team will reach out to the customer via e-mail. You can also share the store support team contacts (listed above) with the customer.

  • Step 2: Make sure the customer email address is available at the custome profile at Sprinklr. Check this before transfering the case. If there is no email there, please ask customer to provide their email address.

  • Step 3: Finalize the interaction with the customer and then click to "Apply Macro". DO NOT fill the ACW information on this scenario. You need to Transfer the case first, and after the ACW will not be needed.

    ATTENTION: It is MANDATORY that when filling the Macro to transfer the case, you select the "Channel Type" as EMAIL, even if the case was originally a livechat or any other. It has ALWAYS to be transfered as "Email" type.

3) For Phone call cases:

  • Step 1: You have to handle the customer phone call and explain to the customer that: their order was purchased at an Emma Store and due to this the request needs to be handled by the designated support team, and for this you will transfer the case internally and the team will reach out to the customer via e-mail. You can also share the store support team contacts (listed above) with the customer.

  • Step 2: It is MANDATORY to have the customer email address available at the custome profile at Sprinklr. Check this before transfering the case. If there is no email there, please ask customer to provide their email address.

  • Step 3: Leave a note on the "Case Collaboration" section summarizing what was discussed/requested from customer on the call with you. Please write down important information that will be needed to the other colleague be able to handle the case properly.

  • Step 4: Finalize the interaction with the customer and then click to "Apply Macro". (DO NOT fill the ACW right now, we must apply the macro first, the ACW will be the next step).

    ATTENTION: It is MANDATORY that when filling the Macro to transfer the case, you select the "Channel Type" as EMAIL, even if the case was originally a livechat or any other. It has ALWAYS to be transfered as "Email" type.

  • Step 5: After the macro is applied, it is mandatory to fill in the ACW information. You can check your cases pending under the "Pending ACW". On the ACW window, you must select "YES" under the option "Sent to Store" and add the customer email address. It is MANDATORY to select this for cases related to Emma Store.
