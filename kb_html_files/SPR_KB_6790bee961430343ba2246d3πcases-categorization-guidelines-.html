Introduction 

This article sheds light on some common doubts and questions related to how to categorize customer's contacts (Cases Categorization (List)). First, you can check some FAQs and the respective answers, and at the end, you can explore the table showing the tickets categories and the effort level.

 

FAQ's 

Q. Who is responsible for the accurate categorization of a ticket?

The agent that is currently assigned to the ticket. Agents are responsible for categorizing the tickets correctly, even when that means amending an automated categorization done by the IVR, chat bots, etc.

Q. For cases marked as "No response required" does the case still need to be categorized?

Yes, even interactions marked as "No Response required" are mandatory to be categorized

​

Q. If a ticket is already categorized, can we change its categorization?

Agents should only change the ticket categorization if the initial categorization, pertaining to the initial contact reason/s was incorrect. Otherwise, agents should not change the original ticket categorization, even if the contact has now transitioned to a different topic.

​

Q. How many categories can we have in a ticket?

A ticket can only have one category selected. Some categories will have sub-categories, and in those cases, selecting a sub-category will be mandatory, but some categories (like "injury Claims", for example) will not have a sub-category.

​

Q. How to categorize an email when there are multiple requests in the same contact?

When an email contains more than one request, although it is necessary to reply to both requests, the categorization should always reflect the most important topic (i.e., the one that will take the longest to resolve).  

For example, in an email where the customer requests an address change for delivery and also inquires "Where is my order (WIMO)", the WIMO topic generally takes longer to resolve, so that would be the appropriate category. (please check the table at the end of the article to understand the "effort" differences between topics)


Q. What to do when there are two or more requests in the same email with equal effort scores? 

When an email has two or more requests with equal effort scores, you may choose one of the topics and categorize it accordingly, but please leave a note explaining that the email contains multiple requests, indicating the other categories that would also apply. 

​

Q. What to do when a ticket initially belongs to one category but eventually transforms into another? 

A common example is when "Where is My Order (WIMO)" inquiries related to delivery delays result in cancellations. In such cases, the correct approach is to maintain the initial category (WIMO), even if the case ends in a cancellation. However, if the follow-up contact results in a new ticket being created (because it happened 72 hours after the initial ticket was closed), a new categorization is possible (in this case, "Cancellation due to Delay"), since this is now a new ticket and the classification is done according to the current contact reason.

​

Q. If a customer sends an email asking about the delivery date on the last day within the Promised Delivery Date (PDD), should we consider a WIMO "Delivery Still on Time" or "Delivery Delay"? 

In that case it is advisable to check if there is an Updated Delivery Date available in the OMS. If there’s a new Updated Delivery Date, please categorize the ticket as a Delivery Delay and inform the customer accordingly. If not, then categorize it as a Delivery Still on Time. 

​

Q. How to categorize a compensation request after a delay? 

This request should be categorized as "Where is My Order" > "Others."

How to use Other's

Mandatory Comment for "Other" Cases

  • Agents must provide a brief explanation when selecting "Other" as a category or subcategory unless the case is SPAM.

  • This will help identify gaps and potential new categories.

  • Comments are mandatory.

 

How to Optimize the Existing Categories for Main Topics

  • Customer Service Channels & Process Gaps

  • Chat:

    • If the customer provides information while interacting with the bot before reaching an agent, the agent must categorize the ticket based on the request.

    • "Other" should only be used if no information was provided before the chat turned idle.

  • Phone:

    • If a customer does not provide any information about their inquiry, or there are technical issues (e.g., the customer cannot hear us, we cannot hear them, or they hang up), categorize it as Other – Other.

 

  • Refund & Compensation Requests

    •  Refund Follow-up: If customer is contacting our support to follow-up on their refund request, to chase it or know the status of it, it should be categorized as Refund – Refund Status. 

    •  Compensation Requests: For any compensation-related inquiries, categorize them as Refunds – Other.

 

  • Returns & Product-Related Issues

    •  Old Mattress Removal Service: If a customer inquiry is about the Old Mattress Removal service, categorize it under Return – Other.

    • Comfort Layer: If a customer requests a Comfort Layer or if it is offered as a return deflection option, categorize it under Return – Other.

    • Missing Items:

    • If the customer is missing

      a cutter or booklet:

      1. Beds: Categorize as Product Claim – Other.

      2. Other items: Categorize as Product Claim – Product Type and Component (select Packaging).

    • If the customer is missing a part of the product or an entire item, categorize it as Logistic Claim – Lost or Stolen – Product SKU.

 

  • Services Provided

    •  If customers inquire about Emma’s services (Guarantee, Warranty, Assembling, etc.) and no specific subcategory exists, categorize it as Services and Store Information – Other.

 

  • Order & Delivery Inquiries

    •  Order Confirmation: If a customer has not received an order confirmation email and inquires about the order status, categorize it as Ordering Issues – Other.

    •  Cancellation: If Emma sends a mass communication about order cancellations and customers contact us to verify or follow up, categorize it as Cancellation – Other.

    •  Delivery Inquiry (Product Name Mismatch): If the name on the box/label differs from what was purchased on the website, categorize it as Product Information – Other.

 

  • Online Reputation & Escalations

    •  If it’s general feedback, use Other – Customer Feedback.

Categorize tickets based on the topic raised by the customer.

This applies to all Online Reputation channels and whenever a customer asks to speak with a Supervisor (Manager Escalation).

  •  If the complaint is, for instance, about a delayed refund, categorize it as Refund – Refund Issues.

Special Handling for Online Reputation Cases

  • When agents need to create a new ticket for Outbound or Follow-up, the original ticket should be closed as Other – Duplicated or Merge.

 

Handling Cases Not Covered by This Guideline

  • If a case does not fit any of the categories listed, agents should identify the main concern and classify it under the most relevant main category – Other.
