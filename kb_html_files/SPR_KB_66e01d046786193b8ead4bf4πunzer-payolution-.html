General Information



Different countries have already available Unzer (also previously known as Payolution).


Unzer is a payment service provider (PSP) we use in our online store (website) that allows customers to pay the order with different payment methods. Unzer is a 3rd party provider between Emma and customer, by proceeding the payment in our system, and collecting the money from the customer afterwards, in accordance with their directly agreed conditions.



In our order management system, whenever the customer chooses Unzer, the automatic process is:




As per the flow above, our system is configured to issue the invoice when an item is shipped. If an order with more than one item has items shipped on different days, customer will receive different invoices (separated in accordance to its shipment).


Payment installments option: when selecting the payment method in installments, the customer needs to pay on a fixed day to Unzer due to the agreement between both parts - we don’t have access to details of the payments (dates, amounts, etc) - this is direct with the payment provider company.


When an order is placed in our system with the Unzer payment method, we have 60 days to trigger it to capture the amount:: this means a deadline of 60 days to ship the order. If the order is not shipped under this period (let's say the item is out of stock), the order will flow automatically, but we can't invoice it anymore and the payment won't succeed in Magento. For that, our teams are constantly checking to avoid possible issues around it.



The information above is crucial to handling possible Customer Inquiries related to Payment issues.

How can we identify an Order placed using Unzer


Inzer will pace order information requests directly to our Customer Support, when they do it they provide order-related information as:

  • Customer email
  • Customer Name
  • Unzer Reference
  • Order Reference

However, the Order reference is not the same available at Magento or COS therefore we will need to follow an alternative.


To properly identify the order we will need to search using the alternative information available, the best alternative is using the customer emails to locate the order but we can also use the customer name.


This step will make sure that you can locate the proper Unzer order.


Transaction ID / Payment reference



You can find the Payolution / Unzer payment reference (transaction ID) in the Order Management System (Magento) in the Payment information section.


You can use both information available to localize transactions in the Payolution portal (Payone Transaction ID or Payment reference).

Summary of the transaction


How to read the summary of the transaction:

  • Open amount: Open balance to be paid by the customer
    *may also be a negative amount, referring to a refund to be processed to the customer shortly.
  • Fees: Payone fees
  • Captured: amount requested in total
  • Refund: If there is a refund from our side
  • To be captured: Not been captured yet (not invoiced yet, or invoice was captured offline)
  • Incoming payments: Already paid by the customer
  • Outgoing payment: Anything Payolution has to refund to the customer
  • Open installments: visibility of remaining (open) installments/ per total of installments.

Managing a Transaction


Move dunning date


Please be aware move dunning date must only be done by the Emma's Payment team


All transactions have a dunning date, which is a deadline the customer should proceed with the payments directly with Unzer.

The set-up dunning date triggers a notification to the customer related to the open payment. There will be scenarios you may need to Move the dunning date to push the notification to the customer.








  • Dispute
    Could be a payment dispute done by the customer related to a claim. As an example, the customer does not want to pay as he is unhappy because the product was delivered with a defect and searching for a solution with the customer service team, so he may dispute the payment and does not agree to pay it.

    Move dunning date: push the notification while we handle the related claim with the customer – so the customer won't be pushed to pay by the Payolution.

  • Fulfillment delay
    Logistic claim: Emma's order is delayed, the customer did not receive the product and do not agree to pay.

    Move dunning date to postpone the deadline of the payment – should not be pushed to proceed with the payment.

  • Direct Payment
    Payment was done to Emma directly - instead of Unzer proceeding with the payment.

  • Payment Open
    The payment is placed by the customer and proof of payment was shared – CS team checked and the Iban is correct.

  • Refund
    The customer canceled or return the item and won't pay – Emma will refund Payolution as long we receive the parcel back to the warehouse.

    Move dunning date to avoid payment request notification as the product is under a reverse logistic journey.


Capture of Orders in Unzer

Due to technical issues, some orders might not be automatically captured in Unzer, it this happens and there is no option to invoice in Magento, you should reach out to Unzer to capture the order.

Unzer Requests






Request Documents



Unzer requests Emma documentation related to the delivery of the item(s), as the customer is not proceeding with the related payment and they need proof of purchase to follow up on the payment request.




The documentation they may request:


  • Invoice
    If more than one invoice is available in the order due to different items shipped at a different time, ensure you either combine the items in one single document or send it via email to gather access to all related items invoice.
  • Order confirmation
    Magento: What & How to - review Order confirmation
  • Delivery confirmation
    You can download the proof of delivery by accessing the related delivery company's portal.







Return proofs


Sometimes we are not aware of return requests, and Unzer may notify us that the customer is requesting the money back due to a returned item.


In those cases, Unzer requests Emma to validate the proof of return. Those requests will be listed under the Return proof page:



Actions:



Support request: send a request to Unzer

(in case you need assistance. E.g. refund is not possible, fees should be canceled, etc).


Further delay: push the request, and leave a note confirming the refund (after the amount is balanced, the task will disappear).


Action log: see the history/ overview of our actions in that transaction.






Customer - Self Portal


As you already have seen through this article, customers may request a payment extension, which means pushing the deadline provided by Unzer related to the payment of their order.


The common reasons for those requests would be:

  • Not all products have yet been delivered (but all were invoiced).
  • The customer is returning the product (and it is in the processing phase, which means the refund has not been processed yet and the customer may be requested to pay).
  • Possible product or logistic claim



Customers can now do a self-service request for a payment extension via Unzer Portal.

Unzer will be reviewing the requests and providing terms and conditions for payment extensions.




Self Portal access


  1. Customer needs to login on to the customer portal
    Customer Portal link
  2. Provide their Payment reference number and date of birth



In the portal, customers are able to get proceed with the specific order for a payment extension.




Installment change: Pay order in full


The customer can request to cancel the installment and pay the order in full.

This is possible and should be requested directly to Unzer.

Customer should send an email to info-vie@unzer.com and confirm the debit cancellation.

Unzer will provide instructions and appropriate bank details and payment information.
