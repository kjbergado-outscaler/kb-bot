What is the Right to Rectify/Correct?


The "right to correct" means that individuals have the right to update or correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data held by organizations about them. This right ensures that people can maintain the accuracy and integrity of their personal information.



Example:


Let's say John recently purchased a mattress online from Emma. During the checkout process, he accidentally entered the wrong delivery address. After completing the order, he realizes the mistake and contacts the Emma’s customer service to correct the address.


John exercises his right to correct by informing Emma of the inaccurate delivery address and requesting that it be updated to the correct one.



Other scenarios:


Let's check other examples of access requests you may receive:


Name Correction:

    • "Hello, I recently made a purchase on your online store, but I noticed that my name is misspelled on the order confirmation email. Could you please correct my name to 'John Smith' instead of 'Jon Smith'?"

Address Update:

    • "Hi there, I recently moved to a new address, and I need to update it in my account information. My current address is [Old Address], but I'd like to change it to [New Address]. Could you please update it for me?"

Phone Number Correction:

    • "Hello, I've noticed that my phone number is incorrect in my account details. The correct number is [Correct Phone Number]. Could you please update it accordingly?"

Email Address Change:

    • "Hi, I've recently changed my email address and I need to update it in my account settings. My new email address is [New Email Address]. Can you please make this change for me?"

Billing Information Update:

    • "Good afternoon, I've noticed that the billing information associated with my recent mattress purchase is outdated. Could you please update it to reflect my current billing address: [New Billing Address]?"


In each of these examples, the individual is exercising their right to rectify by requesting corrections or updates to their personal information held by the company. This ensures that their data is accurate and up to date, which is essential for smooth transactions and effective communication.



How to handle requests for "Right to Rectify/Correct"?


The Data Protection Team is technically unable to modify order information, so please directly handle this data correction request (e.g. you modify the information as requested on the order information by yourself) and respond to the customer within 15 days from the date of their request.


Once you have completed and responded to the request, please send a confirmation to the requesting customer (you may use the template response below). You do not need to forward order information correction/rectification requests to the Data Protection Team. We will only receive notification if you do not address the data correction request after 15 days.

Sample response:


Thank you for your email. We have received your request to correct your personal data in our system. We would like to inform you that we have updated your order information as requested.
