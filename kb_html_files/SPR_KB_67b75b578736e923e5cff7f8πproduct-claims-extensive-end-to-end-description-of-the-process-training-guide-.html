2. Explanation of subprocesses under the Product Claim Flow

2.A) Information you need to ask from the customer

Ensure all the below information is logged on the case before you close it.

  • 1 photo of the overview of the product: The whole product must be visible, including the affected area.

  • 1 Photo of the affected area specific.

More information and example images can be found in the guidebook related to the product type.

Clear and readable pictures of the item's product label. This can be taken either from the product box or from the product itself.

Examples can be found in the Product Quality Guidebook, for each product type

It is mandatory to leave a note on the ticket with a clear description of the issues the customer encountered with the product. This note should always start with "CLAIM DESCRIPTION:" and should be as complete as possible.

Examples:

Example 1: "CLAIM DESCRIPTION: Customer had the product for 3 days, used it and then noticed that, due to sweat, the foam was getting stained."

Example 2: "CLAIM DESCRIPTION: Customer unboxed the product and immediately noticed that the pillow had a yellow stain on the cover."

If you were unable to obtain the above information, please inform your supervisor on how to proceed.

2.B) Verify if the images are legit

Always double-check what kind of photo is being provided. Ask yourself these questions:

Does the photo provided match the product the customer ordered? You can check this with Fluent Commerce / Magento.

Does this photo look like something taken off the internet?

Suspicious behavior

If you suspect the photo may be a photo that was scrapped (taken off the internet), there is a simple way to check that:

  1. Open Google Reverse Image Search

  2. Click on the "Search by Image" option (camera icon):

  3. Select the "Upload an Image" option, and select the photos provided by the customer

Once you have done this, Google will show you if there are any visually similar images on the internet, which may lead to the conclusion as to if they are legitimate proof of claim.

If you find that the pictures are not original or not from an Emma product, reject the claim and inform your supervisor.

Each instruction page contains the following items

  • Claim Component: The part of the product that is affected

  • Claim reason: Classification of what is wrong with the product

  • Description​: Description of the defect reason

  • Acceptance Range​: If the issue from the customer is within the range, the product is not faulty and you must reject the claim (look at the 'Note' section). If the issue is out of the acceptable range, we must accept that the product claim is valid.

  • Note​: A description of what you can tell the customer to explain why we can or cannot reject their claim.

  • Pictures: Example pictures of the information you need to collect to process the product claim case.

2.D) Determine the Outcome of the Case

To know which one you should do, it is important to follow the below steps in the correct order.

Depending on the description and the images you received, use common sense to determine if the product is still usable.

Offer the customer two options:

Option 1: Compensation

  • Offer the customer a compensation, according to the compensation matrix.

  • Explain to the customer that "The raised issue does not affect the usage of the product." Because the product didn't match the customer's expectations, you can offer them a compensation.

  • Outcome of the claim = "Compensation request created"

​

Option 2: Offer a replacement

Especially if you notice the product's usage will likely be affected,

  • offer the customer a free replacement for their faulty product.

  • Initiate a return to have the customer send us back the faulty item.

  • Outcome of the claim = "Return and supplement created"

If the product is no longer usable

Proceed to the 'Decision point 2'.

You must first decide if this issue can be resolved by sending a spare part. For instance, a broken bed stand can be replaced by a new stand - no need to send an entire bed.​

​Not sure if a spare part is available?

  • Check the Product Quality Guidebook

We currently have spare parts available for mattress covers (depending on your market).

In Belgium, Netherlands, Germany, Austria, Switzerland and France, we also have spare parts for Box Bed v5 (BBv5), Storage Bed and the Slatted Frame bed.

Option 1: Spare part avalailable

  • Proceed with the process for sending a spare part .

  • ​Outcome of the claim = "Supplement request created"

​

Option 2: No spare part available

If there is no option to send a spare part, proceed with checking if a replacement is available.

You must be sure that we have a replacement SKU available in stock. That way, the customer will be sure to receive a replacement.

Option 1: Same SKU is available for replacement

  • Replace the faulty item with a new one, by

    • Creating a supplement, AND

    • Initiating a return of the faulty item.

Need a reminder how to create supplements? Review the information here .

​Outcome of the claim = "Return and supplement created"

​

Option 2: No supplement is available

Go to the next decision step 'Is a comparable SKU available'?

For detailed instructions how to check for stock, review the below content.

Check if you can replace the faulty item with a comparable item, using the file corresponding to your market in this 'SKU Replacement ' folder.

Option 1: Comparable SKU is available for replacement

  • Replace the faulty item with a new one, by creating a supplement using a comparable SKU.

  • Initiate a return for the customer to send us back the faulty item.

Need a reminder how to create supplements? Review the information here.

​Outcome of the claim = "Return and supplement request created"

​

Option 2: No comparable SKU supplement is available

  • Proceed with a full refund for the customer.

Outcome of the claim = 'Return and refund request created'

2.E) Logging the Outcome of the Case using the Guided Workflow in Sprinklr

Need a refresher on how to use the Guided Worfklow in Sprinklr? Visit the article 'Claims: How to Manage Claims (for both Product & Logistic Claims)'.

There can be five outcomes to any product claim

  1. Claim Rejected by Agent: The product claim was not valid.

  2. Compensation Request Created: The customer accepted a monetary compensation for the quality issue.

  3. Supplement Created: Only possible in the case of a spare part.

  4. Refund Request Created: This option should never be selected for Product Claims

  5. Return and Supplement Request Created: The customer will receive a replacement item. This can also be spare parts.

    1. Replaced with the same SKU

    2. Replaced with a comparable SKU

  6. Return and Refund Request Create: The customer will return the faulty item and will receive a full refund.

2.F) Process the outcome of the claim

  • Log the outcome of the case in Sprinklr as 'Claim rejected by agent'.

  • Ensure all claim information is logged on the case.

  • Close the case.

That's it.

Give the customer a compensation, according to the compensation matrix .

Submit the compensation via the AR D2C Jira Portal. Use the following fields:

  • 'Refund topic': Refund initiation

  • 'Reason for refund': Compensation - Product claim
