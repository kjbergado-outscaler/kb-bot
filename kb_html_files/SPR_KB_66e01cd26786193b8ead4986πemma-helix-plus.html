What does PLUS mean?


Mattresses Naming


The mattresses with the new cover variant will have Plus added to their names.

For example, Emma Original Hybrid is now called Emma Original Hybrid PLUS.


The New Cover Variant


Without changing the appearance when compared to the previous covers, the new mattress top cover fabric is treated with ‘SweatFree’ technology.


It has the function of immediately absorbing the sweat and evaporating quickly for a cool and comfy snooze time. It allows to keep the body fresh all night long. The technology adapts to all types of sleepers, but especially to hot sleepers who sweat a lot during the night. Read more about it under Product Specification in this article.

Point-elastic Airgocell


The top layer adapts to your body and provides good ventilation due to its open pores.

Benefits

  • Temperature regulation = Breathability

  • Moisture (sweat) will be absorbed and then will evaporate, so the customer doesn't wake up sweating

  • Mattress keeps its shape during its whole life since moisture doesn't affect properties on the mattress after it dissipates.

  • Low rolling resistance

    • What does it mean? If the rolling resistance is high, the body needs more strength to toss and turn and then the person will wake up more during the night. It has much higher responsiveness than memory foam, which has a slower response. Because of this, using this foam on the top layer will provide bounciness on a mattress.

    • How does it work? The bottom layer is supportive, it helps the body to move effortlessly, which means there is not a lot of strength required to turn over while sleeping.


3 Zone Pocket Spring


The 12cm pocket springs, with an average of 250 springs/m2, is encased by supportive HRX cold foam


Benefits

  • Providing 3 zones of dynamic adjustment to the body, especially after changes in sleeping position, optimal air transfer to drive away excess heat and moisture

  • A spring mattress feels bouncier than a foam mattress. Each spring works independently. If two people share the same bed, the mattress will support each of them individually, giving them appropriate support.

  • The hip zone is harder than the rest of the mattress. Thanks to this, the customer does not sink too much into the mattress (=guarantee good spinal alignment)

  • Barrel shape which allows the mattress to adapt to the shape of the person + a motorized slatted frame (Vs square springs)

  • The ventilation of a spring mattress is better than that of a foam mattress. A spring mattress stays cooler than a foam mattress (important in summer).

Cover FAQ

Are there any visual differences from the new cover variant?


The two variants look exactly the same in appearance, and the only difference is the added label next to the care label inside of the cover, marking the new cover treated with "Adaptive" technology (shown in the picture below).


This special label works as a trackable mark in case consumers complain that they suspect receiving the same product as the standard variant.



What if customers complain that they can't perceive the benefit?


The technology is designed for all types of sleepers, but it might have some limitations for a certain group. In case consumers complain that they cannot feel the benefit, we can refund the extra money they've paid compared to the standard variant.

For instance, if standard Emma One Foam costs 199 euros, and Emma One Foam Plus costs 205 euros, we can refund the extra 6 euros to avoid the potential return of the product.


Certifications


Emma mattresses have been awarded the Oeko Tex seal for product class 1 (number 16.0.93305), which is issued by ÖTI Institut für Ökologie, Technik und Innovation GmbH. This class for baby items meets the highest standard of the four Oeko Tex 100 product classes.

This certification attests that all Emma Premium mattress components are free of harmful substances and 100 percent safe.
