Foams


How foams are made?


Foam is a product resulting from mixing oil-based chemicals together, that react and change from a liquid to a solid-state. More specifically, it is a mix of Polyols and Toluoldiisocyanat (TDI). Check below how our foam mattresses are made:


Emma Manufacturing by Luma Moreira




After the reaction, foams are rolled with high pressure, so the pores can open. The foams are stabilized and colored by additives and are not heated.


The foams differ in density, resilience, consistency, open pores, and compression hardness.

Let's understand a bit more about each one of those characteristics.


Density


The density of a foam is defined by the number of kilos of material per cubic meter.


The higher the density, the more stable will be the mattress and consequently heavier.


The higher density, the lower is the breathability of the mattress.


Important to note that density is not a decisive factor in the hardness of a mattress, but there is an influence.




Compression hardness


Compression hardness refers to the structure created within a foam by the action of pressuring the foam:


Design



When we talk about compression


A low compression hardness means the structure of the foam is linear, arranged on top of each other, offering low resistance. The result: the mattress is very soft.


Increasing the compression hardness means changing the structure of the foam. The pressure exerted from above is distributed downwards in a pyramid shape, by supporting the foam by acquiring a significantly higher restoring force. Result: The mattress is havier.


How do we use Pocket Springs at Emma?

The pocket springs can be found in our Emma Hybrid Mattresses. The perfect alignment between foam and pocket springs gives our products more elasticity. This happens because the pocket springs move individually and are connected only by small pockets.
