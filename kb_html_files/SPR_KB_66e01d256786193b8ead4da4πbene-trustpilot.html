What is Trustpilot, and why is it important?

Trustpilot [TP] reviews drive growth by helping our company get seen, get sold, and get better. The TP business tools make it easy to get more reviews, promote your best feedback, engage with customers, and analyze the results to improve. TP say we can see up to 44% in additional revenue with their reviews. If our Trustpilot rating is shown beside your Google listing you could see a 17% increase in CTR. (Google Seller Ratings are based on an average of scores from their list of trusted online review sites – one of which is Trustpilot).

Reply to reviews

Overview

From the left panel, you can go to reviews --> Service reviews to have the list of all the reviews from the most recent to the oldest. You can also click on the top of the left panel to switch between Belgian domain and our Dutch domain.



  1. Read the customer's review (2 minutes)
  2. Look up the existing communication in Freshdesk and Magento (5 minutes)
  3. Formulate the plan of action (1 minute)
  4. Contact the customer, informing them you've read their review and are willing to resolve the issues mentioned in the review. Preferably on call, send an e-mail if customer is not available (5 - 10 minutes)
  5. Act upon the discussed plan of action: (3 minutes)
    • Creating comfort layers
    • Processing refunds
    • Escalate delays
  6. Respond to the review in Trustpilot (1 minute)
  7. Create ticket in Freshdesk to log interaction (1 minute)
  8. Waiting till follow-up date (2 weeks)
  9. Follow-up with the customer to request their feedback and convince them to edit the review (2 minutes)


All reviews must be answered, but in case of backlog prioritize bad reviews and then good ones.


Good examples & good practice


How to respond to public reviews? There are a million ways of responding to public reviews and posts, so I cannot provide you a one-size-fits-all response. But below you'll find some handles to keep in mind when making a public response:


ANGRY CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE - Even if customer is angry, has a huge negative experience…

  • Suppose: You have already made contact, you can start the review with 'I am glad that we have just spoken personally ... blah blah further' … Readers often only see the first part of a review, if there are positive words (I am glad). All the better for the view


NO COPY/PASTE

  • If a potential customer scrolls through reviews and sees the same text 3 times, different name … Well … Creating a feeling of our customers are just number can't be made more obvious.


NO EXCUSES

  • DO NOT make excuses like, because of circumstances, because of this, because of that…
  • DO apologize for what happened and move on to something practical in the response.
    • Put yourself in a potential customer position, and you see comments like: because we were very busy or because of issues with …
    • I will think that … as soon as I order, the company will have another reason for my delay…

PUBLIC DOMAIN

  • One purpose of independent reviews, is creating a platform for consumers to have a way to express their feelings about a company, without that company intervening in their opinion. This is harsh and honesty without a way around it, but also a great opportunity for US to express our products, services and ways of handling things in the public domain!
  • Put on your velvet gloves when responding to any customer review, and keep in mind the 1000 potential buyers reading any review!

Flag bad reviews


You will see that a couple of 1-2 star reviews might not be from real customers, sometimes it can be that competitors or "haters" write fake reviews. You can flag those review to the Trustpilot team easily for them to investigate so they could either delete it or provide more information on the customer.


ONLY reviews that are organic + no indication of order number can be flagged.


1. Find reviewer


The first step to flagging a review where we can't verify the customer is to use the "Find customer" tool --> It will email the reviewer, giving them the chance to provide more information. They have 3 days to answer, after that we are able to flag the review.


2. Flag for appropriate reason


After waiting a couple of days you will be notified if the customer doesn't answer within the 3 days. Then by clicking on the little flag on the review (on the right of "Find reviewer") you can select the reason. It will usually be "Not based on a genuine experience"



3. Check status


Once flagged, it usually takes 2-3 days to have a decision. You will be notified by email but also via the Trustpilot platform by clicking on "Flagging activity" in the "Service review"
