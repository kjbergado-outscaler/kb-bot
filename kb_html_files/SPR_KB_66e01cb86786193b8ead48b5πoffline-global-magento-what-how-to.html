Magento: Order Management System



Magento is the order management system we have been using to manage orders until 2022. With Emma COS, we are switching from Magento to Fluentcommerce.

Check out the CS Tools: OMS - Fluentcommerce (EmmaCOS) folder for more information related.


If your country has already migrated to Fluentcommerce, you may receive customer orders from the past that were placed via Magento.


As an order management tool, Magento is a platform that centralizes all order information placed by the customer. It tracks sales, orders, inventory, and fulfillment (barcodes).


It enables the people, processes, and partnerships necessary for products to find their way to the customer who bought them.


From a CS point of view, it's the tool we use to identify order status, check customers' information, and take required actions such as handling claims, returns, and refunds.

Update Delivery information



Custom delivery date - Up to Prepared for Export


Whenever the customer would like to change the delivery schedule time, is possible only up to Prepared for Export.



Important information


The custom delivery date you will change the date the barcode will be transmitted - not the arrival date of the order.

This means you should always customize the date counting with the estimated delivery time. Let's see in practice:



Example: customer would live the order to be delivered on the 30 of June.
Estimated delivery time 3-5 days.

Custom date should be the 25th of June.


Count of days should be only business days - never include weekends or local holidays.







Address/ Telephone details - Up to Prepared for Export

  1. Address
  2. Telephone
  3. Delivery scheduled time (Custom delivery date)


Order details modification related to the address is only possible up to Prepared for Export (same as a cancellation) as any change need to be done before the information of the order is transmitted to the warehouse.

How to: Cancellation


Order Cancellation


Cancel an order in Magento is only possible up to the barcode Prepared for Export. When the order status changes from it to the next status (Order submitted to the warehouse), it means the order has been transmitted to the warehouse and the cancellation in the system is not possible, as it won't stop the processing steps.


1. Check barcode status of the items.



If the order is up to the Prepared for Export, the Cancel button will be available under the Order Action bar.



After clicking on the Cancel button, a window will pop up to add related cancellation request details:


  1. Partial Cancellation(details below)
    Or
    If whole order cancellation: select reason
  2. Select outcome: No Refund or Refund or cancel payment (based on customer payment method)
  3. Sent
  4. Send email to customer (always select the option)






Partial Cancellation


Choose partial cancellation if you want to cancel a single or several items of the order, but not the whole order.


If partial cancellation, the following window will pop up:

  1. Select the items you want to cancel.
  2. Select the cancellation reason in the drop-down
  3. Select the customer should receive a confirmation of the cancellation (should always be the case).
  4. Select whether the items to be canceled should be refunded.
  5. Click on 'Cancel selected items'.


Barcode Update for Partial Cancellation


For partial cancellation only: After cancellation request has been created, barcodes should be updated to ensure that the cancelled products will not be shipped and/or delivered to customers.

Note: When updating the barcode, it is important that the cancellation request has been initiated first to avoid error in creating the cancellation request.


1. Go to Catalog > Barcodes > Manage Barcodes


2. You will be routed to Barcodes screen. Search for the order which the barcode needs to be updated. You can search as many orders as needed.



3. Select the item that needs to be updated, usually the barcode of the parcel/item that needs to be cancelled is Requested Cancel. Choose Change Status on the Actions dropdown.


4. Choose Cancelled and click Submit.


5. Notification will be shown once the update was updated, and you can see the change of barcode status to Canceled.


After cancellation


If the cancellation has been requested and has been placed in Magento, it will end up in the Cancel Requested queue and the cancellation button will turn gray and can no longer be clicked.


When the cancellation request has been confirmed by Order Management (Refunds), the refund is initiated.

How to: Supplement Order


Every time an item should be added to an order with no cost (zero value) to the customer, a Supplement Order should be created and added to the original one.


This action on the system will be triggered in specific scenarios in which we need to send an additional item to the customer associated with his original request.



When to create a Supplement Order?


In the scenarios that the customer is entitled to have a Replacement (product is defective and we should replace it), or when it's a case for an Add-on (we are sending a pillow to the customer as a claim compensation due to a delay).


1) For reasons Replacement, we consider:


  • Logistic claim for wrong product sent (from Emma's side);
  • Logistic claim for lost/stolen;
  • Product claim (any).


Examples: Customer ordered a 140x200cm mattress but received a 160x200cm; Or if the product if the customer ordered a bed and didn't receive all the parcels for the assembly; Or if the customer gets a product that has a broken foam or a dimension deviation.



2) For reasons Add-on, we consider:


The products are sent to the customer as an added value to the order or original product.

Examples: Sending a pillow as an extra due to a delay case; Or sending a comfort layer on return deflection cases.



When NOT to create a Supplement Order?


We have certain scenarios in which we SHOULD NEVER create a Supplement Order. Those are the following:


  • If the customer does not like his SKU or wants to return it during the trial period (exchange color of the product, for example);
  • If the customer ordered the wrong SKU (due to a fault of his own);
  • If the customer does not like an SKU that is part of a bundle;
  • If the customer bought a phased-out SKU;
  • If the customer bought a “limited stock” SKU that is no longer available;


-> The correct process is: the original product needs to be returned and the customer refunded; and then the customer will have to place a new order in the webshop.


  • In addition to that, please also NEVER create Supplement Orders to unblock orders (any scenario that the order is not on the normal flow).


The correct process is: to escalate via JIRA to the Order Management team or to the Back-Office team (if your market has a back-office team).

How to: Discount


We may need to provide a monetary discount to an existing order from a customer as an example, when a cupon code does not work properly and the order is confirmed with no discount applied.


We can always trigger a refund over the paid amount by the customer via Magento by creating a Discount.


Before we proceed, let's recall some core information related to the Refund Escalation Guideline.

Access Refund escalation article here.




As per the screenshot above, The discount should always be applied to the ROW total of the order, with exception of Forgot the coupon code or Best Match claim. For those two reasons, you should always apply the discount only for the related product and not for the whole order.



Creating a Discount:


  1. With the order opened, click on the Discount button and wait for the new form to load.
  2. Refers to Item:
    Entire order or
    Specific SKU (only in case of Forgot the coupon code or Best price Match claim).
  3. Select the Discount Reasoncategory based on the Magento Reasons Category guideline.
    Acess Magento Reason category article here
  4. Select Specific Discount Reason
  5. Add comments related to the discount
    Access Note article here
  6. Add pictures (if required)
  7. Finally, Save the changes.

How to: Product Return



There may be different scenarios in which customers may request to return their product after having received it.


Reason can be related to the 100-night-trial, as an example, based on customer adaptation or even hardness preferences. Or it can be also linked to a defective product we may need to change. Either way. the way to trigger the return of a product in Magento will always be the same.


Access the order-related page, and for triggering the refund you should provide related information about it to ensure the country operations team takes the proper steps to it.


  1. With the order opened, click on the Return button and wait for the new form to load.
  2. Return Products: Select the related item to be returned in the first collum.
  3. Quality: select if the mattress is packed or unpacked (as this will directly influence the pick-up logistic.
    if packed: the mattress returns to the warehouse
    if unpacked: the mattress will be picked up in accordance to the country police (donation/refurbishment / etc).
  4. Refund to the customer: select if the customer should receive a full refund or no refund at all.
  5. Pickup/Return-Service: select the pick-up company based on your country.
  6. Reason for the Return: select a related reason for the return.
    Follow the return Reasons guideline - access Handling Return article here
  7. Comments: add related return information (include pick-up day if appointment) and follow the Notes guidelines.
    Follow the Notes guideline - access article here
  8. Finally, Save Order.


After triggering the return in Magento, you may need to create labels or arrange the pickup appointment. Check your country-related article if there are any pending steps to ensure the return is triggered properly.

How to: Product Claim



Product claims are complaints of the customers related to the quality or de production of our EMMA Products.

Claims can lead to different outcomes such as a return, replacement, or even monetary compensation (which is a possible refund of a certain amount of the cost paid by the guest, based on the country-specific Compensation Matrix).


Remember, all steps should be followed before registering the claim in the system. Ensure the claim is validated as per our Product claim Guideline - article here


  1. With the order opened, click on the Product Claim button and wait for the new form to load
  2. Select the related product from the order
  3. Select the component of the product related to the claim
  4. Select the related claim reason
    Acess Magento Reason category article here
  5. Add validated pictures
  6. Add comments related to the claim
    Access Note article here
  7. Define outcome: View order, Supplement Order, Return the product, or Compensation.











How to: Logistic Claim


  1. With the order opened, click on the Logistic Claim button and wait for the new form to load
  2. Select the related Item related
  3. Select the Phase (reason) of the claim:
    Delay (if the order is already out of the expected delivery date
    Carrier Misbehaviour (if customer has a claim related to the carrier behaviour)
    Damages (if the package/product has arrived damaged)
    Lost in transit
    Incorrect Shipment
  4. Select the related Reason Category
    Acess Magento Reason category article here
  5. Add validated pictures
  6. Add comments related to the claim
    Access Note article here
  7. Define outcome: View order, Supplement Order, Return the product, or Compensation.
