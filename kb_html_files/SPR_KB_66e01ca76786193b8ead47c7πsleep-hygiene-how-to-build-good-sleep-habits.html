What is sleep hygiene?


Sleep hygiene is a set of habits you develop to get a good night's sleep on a day-to-day basis.

It refers to a set of behavioral and environmental recommendations to help you to achieve your best during the night and Awaken your Best during the day.


These recommendations relate to different areas of life and are usually quite straightforward so that they may be tailored to suit individual needs and preferences.



How to build better sleeping habits?


  • Build an environment that promotessleep hygiene.
    E.g. Block electronic devices from your bedroom or have dimmable light.
  • Set routines so the behavior/sleep hygiene measurements feel automatic within the time. Habits can solidify through repetition until we perform them almost automatically, so you don't have to force yourself every day whether you should stick to them.
    Set a fixed bedtime, turn off your mobile phone 2 hours before going to bed every evening.
  • Make the new routines enjoyable.
    E.g. spoil yourself with a special bath for your warm bathing in the evening. The experience of enjoyable moments prompts your brain to release dopamine, a hormone involved in the regulation of emotion, motivation, and movement, which strengthens the habit even more.
  • Train your habits as a muscle. Some adjustments may take a great deal of willpower and self-control. But the good news is will power can be strengthened over time.
  • Find your perfect formula.
    Ideal sleep hygiene varies among individuals, we should test out various adjustments to find out what suits us best.
  • Do not put yourself too much pressure.
    (Especially when it comes to calming down or falling asleep!) and do not try to implement each recommendation at once! This could most likely lead to unrealistic expectations, disappointment, and discouragement. Small steps are a good start, and you can go from there!

Tips for getting a good night's sleep


  • Stick to a Sleep schedule
  • Exercise at least ~30 min/day but not later than 2-3 hours before bedtime
  • Avoid caffeine (coffee, cola, black/green tea, chocolate) and nicotine
  • Avoid alcoholic drinks before bed
  • Avoid large or heavy meals and drinks late in the evening
  • Avoid (if possible) medicines that delay or disrupt your natural sleep pattern
  • Do not take naps after 3pm
  • Give yourself time to relax and calm down before going to bed (not by watching TV!)
  • Take a hot bath, cuddle with your beloved or make yourself comfortable in a fluffy, warm blanket before bed
  • Keep your bedroom dark, col and free from any phones, tablets, laptops, TVs, and the like
  • Have the right sunlight exposure in the morning
  • Do not lie in bed awake
  • Last but not least: Invest in a comfortable, high-quality mattress, pillow, and bed linen!!
