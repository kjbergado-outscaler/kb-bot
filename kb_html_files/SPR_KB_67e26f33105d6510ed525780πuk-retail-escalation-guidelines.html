Overview


This document outlines the escalation process for handling customer issues across the three lines of business: Wholesale Partners, Marketplaces, and Own & Operated Stores. The escalation process ensures timely resolution, adherence to service level agreements (SLAs), and proper routing of issues based on their nature and severity.

​

Service Level Agreements (SLA)

​

  • Wholesale Partners: 24 Business Hours

  • Marketplaces: 24 Business Hours

  • Own & Operated Stores: 24 Business Hours

Escalation Handling by Source

​

  • End Customer Escalations: Addressed by the Supervisor and handled through the Escalations Channel.

  • Account Escalations (Wholesale Partners & Marketplaces): Directed to the Customer Excellence Manager (CE Manager) via Outlook Email.

  • Escalations Triggered by Operations Manager or Key Account Manager: If a customer reaches out to the Operations Manager or Key Account Manager, the issue should be formally escalated following the process outlined below.

Communication Channels

​

  • End Customer Escalations: Through the Escalations Channel to be addressed by the Supervisor.

  • Wholesale Partners & Marketplaces Escalations: Sent via Outlook Email to the Customer Excellence Manager for resolution and follow-up.

  • Escalations from Operations Manager or Key Account Manager: Should follow the structured format below and be sent to the Customer Excellence Manager via Outlook Email.

Escalation Report Structure (For Operations Manager & Key Account Manager)


When raising an escalation, the following structured format should be used to ensure clarity and efficiency:

  • Subject Line: ESCALATION – [Issue Type] – [Customer/Account Name]

  • Customer/Account Name: [Wholesale Partner / Marketplace / Own & Operated Store]

  • Issue Description: Detailed explanation of the problem, including any relevant context.

  • Previous Actions Taken: Outline any previous steps taken to resolve the issue.

  • Impact & Urgency: Explanation of how the issue affects the customer and its urgency level.

  • Requested Resolution: Suggested action or resolution expected.

  • Attachments: Any supporting documentation (e.g., email threads, screenshots, order details).

​

This guideline ensures that escalations are handled efficiently while maintaining service quality and meeting SLAs. Any unresolved or urgent cases should be promptly flagged to higher management for further intervention.
