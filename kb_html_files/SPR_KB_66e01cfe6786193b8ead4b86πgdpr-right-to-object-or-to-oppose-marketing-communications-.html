What is the Right to Object or to Oppose (to marketing communications):?


The "right to object" to marketing means that people can say they don't want to receive certain types of marketing messages. For example, if someone asks not to get any more emails promoting products or services, that's them exercising their right to object to marketing. Similarly, if they request to stop receiving phone calls, texts, or any other form of marketing communication, that's them using their right to object.


In this case, you must forward the data subject request to privacy@emma-sleep.com.

Example:


To give an example, Alice has been receiving marketing emails not only from our online sleep products store but also from third-party companies promoting Emma’s products. However, she finds these emails irrelevant and wishes to stop receiving them. Alice contacts the customer service and specifically requests to opt-out of marketing communications (from Emma’s or from third parties on Emma’s behalf), asking that her personal data not be shared with any third-party companies for marketing purposes.


Other examples of right to oppose requests you may receive:


I've been getting a lot of emails from your company, but I don't want them anymore. Please stop sending me marketing emails."

    • This is a clear request to oppose marketing. The person doesn't want to receive any more promotional emails from our company.


"I'm receiving too many promotional emails from your company. Could you please unsubscribe me from your mailing list?"

    • This is a request to unsubscribe from our mailing list, which is a way of opposing marketing. They want to stop receiving promotional emails.


"I'm tired of getting emails about your products from this third party. Please remove ask them to remove me from the mailing list. I do not want to receive emails from your products anymore"

    • Similar to the previous example, this is a request to be removed from a third-party mailing list to stop receiving marketing emails.


"I don't want to receive any more emails promoting your products. Can you make sure of it?

    • This is another request to be removed from our marketing list to stop receiving promotional emails.


"Please ask your company not to send me any more email marketing. I don't want to hear about your products anymore."

    • This is a direct request to the recipient to take action on behalf of our company, asking them to stop receiving email marketing from us.


"Please don't share my information with other companies for advertising."

  • This is also a request to oppose marketing, specifically third-party marketing. They're asking us not to share their data with other companies for advertising purposes.


How to handle requests for "Right to Object or to Oppose"?



Step 1: Forward the request to Legal & Compliance


If you receive the request via Freshdesk, chat, email, call or face-to-face please forward the request right away to:


privacy@emma-sleep.com

The Legal & Compliance team will reply to the DSR.




Step 2: Reply to the Customer using Canned Response


Suggested Response to Reply:


Dear (Customer's first name), Thank you for your inquiry regarding personal data. We have forwarded your request to our Data Protection team, and they will reply to you in due course. Thank you, Your Emma Team



ATTENTION: Mandatory for marketing unsubscribe requests


If you receive a request to be unsubscribed/opposing from marketing, don’t forget to add this in your DSR acknowledgment response:


“If you have received a marketing communication of Emma products and wish to opt out from receiving it, please reply to this e-mail including the communication as an attachment. Although this is not obligatory, we will be able to identify the sender more easily and communicate your intention to opt out more quickly.”
