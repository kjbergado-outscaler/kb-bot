This article describes how to handle return requests that fall under the 10-year warranty period, in Denmark and/or Sweden.

Per September 2024 Emma no longer sells products in Denmark and Sweden. However, customers can still ask for returns when there are claimable issues with their products.

Step-by-step guide

Step 1: Check the validity of the claim

Emma’s offer of the 10-year warranty period remains valid. That means we will follow the normal process and must verify if the return was created within 100 days after the last item was delivered to the customer.

If the claim is invalid, the process stops here.

Step 2: Check if the product is still usable and propose a compensation

As with any claim, we must first check if the customer is willing to keep the product due to a defect that does not affect the quality - e.g. a colour issue. If that is the case, you will first offer monetary compensation (which is a possible refund of a certain amount of the cost paid by the customer. The compensation can be calculated with the Compensation Matrix.

Step 3: The product is no longer usable: Offer a full refund

Because we no longer operate in the Danish and Swedish markets, we can no longer offer replacements. We will proceed with issuing a refund, and we propose the customer to keep the product.

Inform customers that we will refund them, and they can keep/discard/donate the product.  
You can share the below message with the customer:  

"We are writing to inform you that we will be issuing a refund for your recent purchase of [Product Name]. As an exception, there is no need to return the product to us. You are welcome to keep, discard, or donate the item as you see fit. If you wish to confirm this refund and proceed with the process, please reply to this email at your earliest convenience. Our team will ensure the refund is processed promptly. Thank you for your continued support and understanding."

If the customer accepts, move to step 4. If the customer insists on having a pick-up service, move to step 5. 

​

Step 4: A refund without a return 

  1. In Fluent Commerce, create a RETURN with REFUND. 

  2. As the Carrier, add "Customer kept the product" and move it to ORGANIZED. 

  3. Ensure you have written confirmation from the customer that they accept the refund, before you proceed. 

  4. Afterwards, move the status of the return to COMPLETE. This will automatically trigger the full refund of the item. 
    On the comment-field in Fluent Commerce, write down "Customer kept the product".

  5. Remember to inform the customer that it can take ~14 days for the refund to be processed. 

OR

Step 5: The customer insists on a return: offer a full refund and organize a pick-up with a Charity.

  1. Ensure you have written confirmation from the customer that they accept the pick up from the charity.

  2. Create the Return in the OMS
    - In Fluent Commerce, create a RETURN with REFUND. 
    - Fill Customer details, Product condition, Return reason as "Product Claim" and in Carrier add "Charity".

  3. Check the charity that collects in that region and send them an email to collect that item (list below).

  4. In the return (in Fluent) fill the Tracking code with the charity name and move it to ORGANIZED and then mark it as COMPLETE.

  5. After marking it as complete, customer will receive their refund.
    > Remember to inform the customer that it can take up to 14 days for the refund to be processed.
