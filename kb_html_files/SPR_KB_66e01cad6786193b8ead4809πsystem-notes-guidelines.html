Why are the internal notes so important?


Internal notes are essential to ensure that we keep track of our customers contacts, requests and also of Emmies' actions, especially when these are connected to an order. By checking the notes done after a contact, one should be able to easily get an understanding of the customer's request, so that an accurate follow-up can be done.

Notes allow us to know if a contact was compliant with legal requirements such as GDPR authentication, and help us understand what the customer's contact reason was, what steps have been taken to assist said customer and what is still pending action on our side or theirs.

It is also through notes that our different teams at Emma, such as Product Quality or Logistic teams, can gain a quick and detailed understanding of a claim that was raised by a customer.

With that in mind, and since Emma is a truly multicultural company, it is of utter importance that all notes are created in English, so that anyone, from any team, can easily assimilate the information included in these notes, without the need for translation (that would create a serious risk of miscommunication). As such, agents should also refrain from using abreviations, as these might not be understood by other agents or other POCs.

​

General notes guidelines

  • Make sure to follow the templates mentioned on this article at all times when leaving notes;

  • Be accurate and concise, inserting all relevant information in the notes but avoiding irrelevant or redundant things;

  • Do not leave important information out: if you promised the customer a refund for X amount or if there's a new deadling for the parcel's delivery, this information should be present in the notes;

  • Stick to the facts: your personal opinions on the customer should not be present in internal notes;

  • And, finally, always insert notes in English.


Notes in an order management system (OMS)

​

These notes are mandatory while handling tickets that involve existing orders, and should always be inserted in the original order. The only exception being Fluent Commerce supplement orders that were created from a Magento order, in those circumstances, the OMS notes should be left on the supplement order in Fluent Commerce.


Template to be used to insert notes in Magento:

​

GDPR: Y/N ("Y" if the customer was authenticated, or didn't require authentication / "N" if the customer was not successfully authenticated)
TN: ____________________ (Sprinklr Ticket Number)
ENQ: __________________ (Enquiry)
ACT: __________________ (Actions taken and any Sprinklr flow errors encountered)


Template to be used to insert notes in FluentCommerce:


AGT: ___________________ (Agent's username, as shown on Sprinklr)
GDPR: Y/N ("Y" if the customer was authenticated, or didn't require authentication / "N" if the customer was not successfully authenticated)
TN: ____________________ (Sprinklr Ticket Number)
ENQ: __________________ (Enquiry)
ACT: __________________ (Actions taken and any Sprinklr flow errors encountered)


** Additionally, please note that FluentCommerce order notes currently have a 200 characters limitation. As such, if the agent requires more than 1 entry to leave complete notes, these should be divided into PT1 (part 1), PT2 (part 2), etc.


IMPORTANT:
When contacted by a third party, after the original customer has been authenticated, and the contact has proceeded with said third party, the notes on Magento / Fluent Commerce should reflect this fact. These notes should mention the original customer authentication and the fact that we then proceeded with the contact with said third party in question, noting down this person's name.


Notes on Sprinklr

Note: All internal notes on Sprinklr MUST BE added using the the "Profile Collaboration" section. Please check this article that explains the right process for leaving notes on Sprinklr.​

Leaving notes on Sprinklr on the "Profile Collaboration" is MANDATORY in the following scenarios:

​

1) Escalations, when escalating cases to other related teams (ex: Emma Internals, Supervisors, etc.).

​

TN: _______________ (Sprinklr Ticket number)

ON: ______________ (Order Number)

POC: ______________ (Person with whom matter was escalated with)

ENQ: _____________ (Enquiry)

ACT: ______________ (Actions taken)

JIRA: ______________ (JIRA ticket number, if applicable)

​

High-Level Escalations

This should be used when replying to the high-level escalation (HLE) ticket only.


GDPR: Yes/No
Ticket Number: ____________________ (HLE) / ____________________ (Outbound ticket number)
Customer Inquiry: __________________
Actions taken: __________________
