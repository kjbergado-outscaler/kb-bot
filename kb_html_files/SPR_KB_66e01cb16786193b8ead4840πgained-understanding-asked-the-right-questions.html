When a customer is contacting us, it is essential we understand the reason for the contact in order to provide a resolution.


This article will help you to understand what is the best way to open a conversation with a customer and from that, take the proper direction to make an impact on the customer situation.



Let's think in a practical way:




With the right question in place, you can deflect a return and turn it into an upselling opportunity. Customers may not be aware of the variables that could be influencing their experiences, or what could be the right pillow to fit their needs. That's where our expertise comes in place, but for that: we need to listen to our customers.



But, what is the difference between hearing and listening?


Hearing is a passive act. It's about perceiving audible sounds with your ear.

Listening, on the other hand, is trying to understand what the other person is saying and feeling.


Active Listening


Active listening is sincere listening, attentive and open.

Able to reflect the content of the teller, able to reflect the feeling of the teller.



We have different behaviors that you can demonstrate active listening that are essential for the customer, such as verbal nods, taking notes, asking questions, paraphrasing, and summarizing.


By active listening, you will make customers more confident that you are there available, and willing to help. Plus, support you to avoid misunderstandings and mistakes, providing accurate and efficient resolution.



Effective Questioning

But let's say the customer is not providing enough information, or you may need to confirm if you understood what happened with the order of the customer. There are different styles of questions that can help you to guide the conversations. Techniques that can help you to control the conversation, they are the Open and closed questions, reflective or confirming and the probing questions.





When putting into practice, the idea is for you to imagine your questions as a funnel, filtering the details provided by the customer and ending up on your pain point: the topic you need to resolve to make your customer happy. For that, you can always use the funnel technique.




Investigating the information


With practice, you will notice that sometimes, the problem presented by the customer is not always the root cause of the issue.


As one example, the customer may call us saying his delivery is delayed and the root cause of this issue could be different reasons such as the delivery delay from the carrier, or delivery delay from our warehouse partner, or even delay from the warehouse not because of their capacity, but because they are out of stock. All of them require us different teams, and different follow-up actions, so it is crucial to find out the root cause of the problem.


That's why every time we are handling a customer inquiry, we need to combine our skills with a proper investigation by checking all available tools & information related to it.


  • Check notes left in the system by previous colleagues (any past contact from the customer/note left by another team - senior agent or logistic team?)
  • Check order-related information (barcode status or related case information available)


By doing so, you will be able to provide the proper resolution and make an impact on our customer experience.
