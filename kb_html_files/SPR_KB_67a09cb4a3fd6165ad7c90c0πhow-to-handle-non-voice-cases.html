Viewing All Cases

  • Click in the Console option in the left panel;

  • Select My Work → My Works;

  • In the My Works Dashboard, all cases will be visible regardless of the channel (voice, social, or email);

  • Select the case you want to work on.

Handling the case

IMPORTANT:

We DO NOT transfer the case to another agent even if the customer is asking to speak with another colleague or if the case was previous handled by a colleague. When you receive the case, you have the onwership to handle it from now onwards. Make sure that you review the previous interactions and get a understanding of what was happen before.

Authentication & Set Email Features

The Authenticate and Set Email features functions in the same way as they do for voice cases:

Authentication

  • Click on the "Authentication" button located in the top-right of the Care Console;

  • Fill in all the necessary fields to be able to authenticate the customer → Next​;

  • Sprinklr will then make an API call to check if the details are correct​;

  • If the process is successful, you should see the Authenticated status​.

Set Email

  • Click on the "Set Email" button located in the top-right of the Care Console​;

  • Enter customer’s name and email address;

By doing this, in the Reply Box you will get another option to reach out to the customer via email​. This is specially useful when you need to follow-up about the customer’s query in the future.

Trigger ACW Macro

You must apply the Trigger ACW macro to non-voice cases. This will change the status of the cases as necessary, after you’ve answered the customer.

  • Click Trigger ACW in the top right corner → Trigger ACW;

  • Select the Case Status;

  • Select the correct case category and fill out all relevant fields → Next.

Switching Dashboards

  • To view cases you have under different statuses, switch to the My Cases Dashboard.

Macros I should apply on social/email cases

  • Assign To X: this macro will change the status of cases to Assigned and assign cases to the agents/supervisors that you select in the drop-down menu​;

  • Create JIRA: this macro is pending and not ready to use yet. The goal is to direct you directly to the Jira Portal.

  • Send To Team: this macro is used to send cases to different departments​;

  • Trigger ACW: this is the macro that you will use to:​

  • Put cases on Awaiting Response​

    • When you move cases to this queue and the customer replies, the system will assign the case back to you if you have capacity to handle it;

    • If you do not have capacity to handle it, the system will assign the case to the next available agent​;

    • If the customer takes more than 7 days to reply after putting the case on Awaiting Response, the system will auto-close the case​;

  • Put cases On Hold​

    • You need to ensure that you clear the on-hold cases before the end of the day​.

  • Put cases on Pending Resolution Confirmation​

  • Close ​

    • Fully close cases​.

Note: as soon as you apply any of the above macros, the cases will move to their respective queues (e.g. Awaiting Response, On Hold). To be able to see these cases, you will have to open the My Cases Dashboard.

How to send Outbound emails

Access the "Create Case Email" Option

  • From the left-side menu, click on the "Create Case Email" button.

  • Fill in the customer’s e-mail, first name and last name;

  • Click Next;

  • Fill in the e-mail subject;

  • Click Create, in the bottom-right of the page.

Open the Case in the Care Console

  • After the case is created, click on 'Open Case in the Care Console' to access the case in the Console.

Write and Send the Email

  • Write the body of the email in the "Reply Box";

  • Once the email is complete, review it and click Send to deliver the email to the customer.​

  • Your e-mail will then be visible in the case.
