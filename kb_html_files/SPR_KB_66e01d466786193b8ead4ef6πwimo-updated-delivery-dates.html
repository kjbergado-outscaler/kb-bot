Introduction

​

In this article, you will learn how to proceed and what to inform customers based on the information shown in the option "Updated Delivery Date" (UDD) in FluentCommerce. You will know there has been an update on the order by the red banner that will appear at the top of the order page in FluentCommerce.

​

​

Updated Delivery Dates Scenarios

Every time an order is placed in the system, once is Booked we should have a "Promised Delivery Date" (in blue at the image) informed there, which indicates the precision of when the customer will receive their items. However, if the order is facing any delay, a new estimation of the delivery arriving to the customer will appear at the field "Updated Delivery Date" (in orange).

​

​

The field Update Delivery Date (UDD) may show 3 different types of information, let's see below each one of them:

​

Scenario 1: Updated Delivery Date = Empty

If the field doesn't have any information, it means there is no update and you can assume the Promised Delivery Date will be met.


-> How to inform the customer?

Explain that the delivery is estimated for the promised delivery date that they also received via email. In case of any problems, ask the customer to contact us again.

Scenario 2: Updated Delivery Date = "Date"

If the field shows a new date it means that we had a delivery delay and now we have a Update Delivery Date to be provided to the customer.


Scenario 3: Updated Delivery Date = "UDD is not available"

We may face some cases where is not possible to calculate the Updated Delivery Date, even though there is an update to the Promised Delivery Date. In those cases, we cannot determine yet the new delivery date, but we will still have the description of what happened.

​

-> What to do in scenario 2 and 3 depending on the Updated Delivery Date Description?

​

  • It is mandatory that we never mention to customers that the product is "out of stock", due to legal reasons. Please explain that we are facing logistics issues that is impacting the order delivery.

  • If the Order Is Delayed, we provide compensation but only if it's > 14 days delayed from the promised delivery date. There's no more upfront compensation. More info here.

What if the customer wants to Cancel the order because of this?

Please proceed with the cancellation! The customer is entitled to cancel their order at any point. Especially for the cases in which we don't have any estimative for replenishment, we should not try to deflect a cancellation. We don't want to make the customer more unhappy with their experience with us.

​

Canned Responses

Below you can find an example of statement:

​

"Unfortunately, after checking your order [XXX], we must confirm that it has been further delayed and is now expected after [DATE 1/WEEK 1 . We sincerely apologize for this situation.

​

In case your order has been delayed more than 14 days after the original delivery date, we can provide a compensation as a goodwill gesture on our part. If this is your case and you are willing to wait for the order, please reach out to us once you receive the order so we can calculate the compensation.

If you prefer to cancel your order at this time, we can suggest you a similar item that is in stock (IF MARKET HAS A COUPON THAT WE CAN USE ON THIS SCENARIO, then add: If you wish to do that, we can provide you a coupon that can be used on your purchase). Please let us know by responding to this email and we will assist you in doing so.

We want to emphasize that we take these issues seriously. Our team is working closely with partners to resolve these issues and improve our services. Thank you for your understanding. We look forward to providing you with a better experience in the future."
