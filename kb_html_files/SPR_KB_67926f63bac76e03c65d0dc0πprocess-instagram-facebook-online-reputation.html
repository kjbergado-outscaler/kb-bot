How to handle Instagram/Facebook cases

​

If a Comment:

  1. Assess Tone and Frustration Level:

    • Evaluate the level of frustration expressed in the comment. Use the appropriate tone and language based on the guidelines.

  2. Reply to the Comment:

    • Acknowledge the issue and inform the customer that you’ve sent a direct message to gather more details and investigate further

      Example message, personalize first: Hey @instagram_handle. We're sorry to hear about the trouble with your order. We've sent you a direct message to sort this out. If you could share your order number there, we’d be happy to help you resolve the issue. Thanks!

  3. Send a Direct Message:

    • Reach out to the customer via direct message to request additional information (e.g., order details, contact info).

  4. Investigate the Issue:

    • Gather all necessary information, check the customer’s history, and review any previous interactions to understand the context (if available).

  5. Resolve and Confirm:

    • Resolve the issue and confirm with the customer that it has been addressed.

    • Apply ACW: categorize the case & mark the case as closed in the system.

​

If a Direct Message:

  • Treat the direct message as a standard case and process it accordingly.

​

If a Story Mention:

Review the Content:

  • If Negative:

    • Reach out to the customer to request more details. Process the case as a standard issue.

  • If Positive:

    • Show appreciation by thanking the customer for their mention in a friendly and professional manner.

If Related to a Store Order:

  • Process the case as a standard store order issue.

  • If you’re unsure about the next steps, escalate the case internally with all relevant details.

How to reply to social media public threads

Q&A

1. There are customers who leave comments on our Facebook/Instagram posts, and often, many of these comments are the same.
Question: If we are required to reply to each individual comment, can we send the same response to the customer, even though this may reduce the level of personalization in the replies for comments from the same customer?

Answer: If a customer leaves multiple comments, it's best to maintain consistency while still personalizing the response. You can use a similar core message but adjust it slightly to acknowledge the specific comment or concern. If comments are the same, your replies can be the same as well.

Why: Social media comments are public! Our goal is to improve Emma's image on social media. When potential customers see agitated comments left unanswered— even if you responded to the same comment on a different post—it doesn’t build trust or encourage purchases. To maintain a positive brand perception, it's important that customers see Emma's team as responsive and engaged.

2. We receive comments from customers mentioning other customers, and these comments aren't related to Emma, as they are having a conversation between themselves. These comments are also routed to Sprinklr.
Question: Do we still need to respond to these types of comments?

Answer: Please reply to every customer comment addressed to Emma, but no need to respond when customers are simply talking to each other. However, feel free to step in and offer help or assistance if there’s an opportunity to support either customer. 

​

3. Most of the negative comments are hidden and we no longer reply to these comments.
Question: Do we still need to respond to these types of comments?

Answer: Yes, reply to these comments to reassure the customer that they are satisfied with the solution provided by the Emma team.

Why: Our two main goals are:

  1. Helping customers

  2. Maintaining and improving Emma's image

If the issue hasn’t been resolved, the customer may continue posting negative reviews elsewhere. Please use every opportunity to assist Emma customers
