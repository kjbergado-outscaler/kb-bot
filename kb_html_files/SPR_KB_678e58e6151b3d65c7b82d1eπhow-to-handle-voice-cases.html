Answering the Call

You have 15 seconds before the call is auto-answered. After this period, the call will be answered automatically, and you cannot decline it. You can see the countdown in the incoming call window:

IMPORTANT:

You can NOT use the "Decline" button. Rejecting a call is never a procedure.

Handling and Ending the Call

Signaled in red below, you can see the Call Console. This is an interactive bar that allows you to:

  1. Put the customer on Hold & Mute yourself;

  2. Pause/resume the Call recording;

  3. Transfer cases;

  4. End the call (this option should not be used).

1) Hold and Mute Features

You have the option to manage the call with the following actions:

  • Put on Hold: The customer will hear hold music, and the call will remain in the queue. For that, you must click on the “Pause” icon. To return to the call, simply click in the “Play” icon.

    ​

    As per procedure, while on Hold, agents need to give the customer feedback every 2 minutes. If the agent feels the situation will take longer than expected to sort out, the agent should give the customer the option to receive a follow-up contact at a later date. You can review all communication guidelines on the related Quality Grids: HERE

  • Mute: The button allows you to temporarily mute your microphone while continuing to listen to the customer. IMPORTANT: We avoid the use of this feature as it can lead the customer into thinking the call has dropped or that the CS agent is not listening to them. If you need to check information with a colleague or a supervisor, please use the Hold button and inform customer accordingly.

Note: These actions are recorded in the Conversation History.

2) Call Recording

Call recording begins automatically once the customer gives consent during the IVR.

  • Stopping Recording: If the customer requests to stop the recording, you can pause it by clicking Record Call → Secure Pause.

  • Resuming Recording: To resume the recording, you must click on Record Call → Record.

Note: Always inform the customer when recording starts or stops to comply with legal requirements.

3) Call Transfer

IMPORTANT:

We DO NOT transfer the case to another agent even if the customer is asking to speak with another colleague or if the case was previous handled by a colleague. When you receive the case, you have the onwership to handle it from now onwards. Make sure that you review the previous interactions and get a understanding of what was happen before.

If you need to transfer the call to another market or line of business, you must click on the “Transfer” option:

You have two options when transferring the call:

  • Warm Transfer:

    • Put the customer on hold and speak with the other agent or supervisor first. Once you’re ready, transfer the customer to the other agent or queue.

  • Blind Transfer:

    • Directly transfer the customer to the other agent or queue without any communication with your colleague.

4) Ending the Call

​

After you handle the case and the conversation is coming to an end, you need to properly inform customer about transfering them to the CSAT Survey. The customer will hear a few questions, and the call will automatically be closed once it is completed.

MANDATORY: Before transfer customer to the survey, inform them accordingly to this template:

​

"In that case, I will then finalize our contact and redirect you to a one-question service survey. If this is not okay with you, you may end the call. Thank you for calling and have a nice day."

Transfer to Survey IVR

  1. Click the Transfer button in the left-hand menu.

  2. Select Internal from the drop-down options

3. In the second drop-down, choose Survey IVR → Transfer Call.

After transferring the call, you will be required to complete the After Call Work (ACW).

Triggering the ACW

After transferring the call, the After Call Work (ACW) window will pop up. Click on Trigger ACW to enter all necessary information and finalize the case.

​Case Console during a Call handling

Let's check some buttons and options that you can use during a call handling.

1) Adding Internal Notes

Internal Notes can be added at both the Case and Profile levels. You can use @mentions to tag agents or supervisors for visibility and collaboration.

1.1) Profile Notes:

You can add your note directly in the Omni-Channel Interaction window. Make sure to include all relevant details, then click Send once you're finished. Once submitted, your note will appear in the Profile, indicated by the red arrow below.

1.2) Case Notes:

To add a note to a case, please refer to the article "Sprinklr Internal Notes: Case Colaboration" .

2) Historical Case Information

The Historical Cases section in the Care Console provides visibility on the number of times a customer has contacted support using the same phone number. This might be useful to identify the customer’s previous cases and expedite the troubleshooting.

  • To see this information, you need to simply roll down the page:

3) Case Details

On the this section, you can see the case’s history and overall information. To access detailed case information, click on Show All, which will direct you to the Properties page. This will display the full set of case properties, including:

  • Case Number;

  • Status (e.g., Open, Pending, Resolved);

  • Categorization;

  • Customer's Phone Number;

  • Customer History: Past interactions history from the customer.

Under the Case Content section, you'll see the ticket's categorization, which provides additional context to the case.

The Overview panel offers additional information regarding the recording consent and associated timeframes.
