Introduction


In this article, you can learn about the payment method of PayPal, about what are the differences between disputes, claims and chargebacks, and how to handle those cases.



How to access PayPal Portal?


The credentials to access the portal will be shared by managers and/or supervisors with the respective agents who are responsible for those payment tasks.

General Information


PayPal works as an intermediary between customers and their banks. A customer creates an account with the Paypal system adding to it a bank account or credit/debit card and whenever they're making an online payment, they can select which account to debit. All transactions are processed through PayPal, instead of the bank.


When selecting Paypal while purchasing with Emma, PayPal proceeds the payment in our system and activates the order; and charges the customer directly via their selected payment method.






Paypal cases


Situations in which an unhappy customer who purchased with Paypal decides to have their money back will appear to our CS as a case to be handled with specific steps.

After logging in to the Portal, the customer service team is responsible for handling cases in the Resolution Center at the portal, which concentrates the list of open cases.


Open cases are payment petitions raised by customers to Paypal. After a dispute from the customer, Paypal opens cases for us to "respond" to the related claim raised. Our goal is to investigate the case, take a decision about the possible outcome of the claim and reply to the portal in accordance with it.


The claims are raised for several reasons such as product not received, product claim, or even an incorrect dispute for the charge raised by the customer.


Here you can check the list with types of cases related to PayPal payment that you may need to handle. Below in this article, you will learn about each of them with more details about the process.


Types of cases:

  • DISPUTES: It is a pre-claim process that allows the customer to open a dispute for the payment based on 2 reasons: not receiving item or item received is not as described.

  • CLAIMS: Customer is unhappy with the outcome of a dispute and the case was escalated as a claim.

  • CHARGEBACK: Customer or Fraud Victims reporting straight to bank / CC company to report the charges.

  • APPEALS: Emma’s actions towards cases.




IMPORTANT - Responding cases


When handling a case, have in mind:


  • Paypal is not a customer service portal, and should not lead to conversation or claim escalation. It is a portal dedicated to approving or not disputes and related refunds.

  • Always attach related proof to the answers (proof of delivery, as an example).

  • We are given 20 days to respond to disputes (open case will always have a precise deadline). If we do not reply to the case on time, the dispute will be processed in favor of customer.

  • We don't give discounts or compensations via PayPal portal.

  • We just approve the refund of canceled or returned items.




How to handle Paypal cases?


Disputes


In this one, PayPal does not intervene. The customer is entitled to open the dispute and it is up to Emma to investigate and provide proof and/or justification.


If a customer opens a dispute they are informing us about an issue and a dissatisfaction with their Emma experience or product. We need to acknowledge their frustration and handle the case with empathy and accurate information.


Time frame: Emma has 20 days to resolve the dispute with the customer directly before the case is escalated.


Handling a dispute:


Before anything, it is necessary to check PayPal's Resolution Center and verify the reason for the dispute: "Item not received" or "Item not as described".

Possible Outcomes


Each open case needs to be evaluated and you can proceed with the following outcomes:




1. Send a message
When the dispute is not valid, ensure you Send a message including the reason why is not valid.


2. Add Tracking info

If the order is on its way, you should add track info for visibility.


3. Issue Full Refund

Confirm the dispute and proceed with the refund for the customer


4. Send an Offer

To be used when the amount was disputed incorrectly and the refund should be a different amount. Use it to correct the amount.
