What is the Self-Identification Chatbot?


At Emma Sleep, our goal is to provide seamless and efficient customer support. Recognizing the increasing role of automation in enhancing customer service, we've implemented a chatbot, referred to as our Self-Identification Bot, on our website. This intelligent assistant helps categorize customer requests, gathers preliminary information, and in some cases, collects personal details such as name, email address, order number, and phone number.



What changes for a CS agent / Sleep Expert agent?


The Self-Identification Bot works as a "pre-chat" in which the customer will provide details, so once the preliminary categorization and data collection are done, the inquiry might be directed to live chat, our email support, or a phone call (based on availability and the customer's preferences).


Due to that, it's important to have a look at those previous automatic interactions, so you can be aware of what the customer already informed us and prepare yourself for the new interaction. The goal is to assure our customers feel that they are receiving a complementary and individualized suppport, and that we avoid asking again information that the customer already gave to us.


This article is designed to guide our agents on how to handle these post-chatbot inquiries, ensuring a smooth transition and continued exceptional service.

How to handle

Live Chat Inquiries


At Emma Sleep, our live chat is a crucial bridge between automated chatbot interactions and real-time human support. The self-identification bot serves as the primary filter, ensuring that when a customer reaches our live chat agents, they are equipped with context and information to provide swift assistance. Here's a guide on how to effectively manage these inquiries:


Accessing Chatbot Conversation History

Upon receiving a live chat inquiry, agents can view the entire chatbot interaction within Freshchat. This conversation history will appear directly in the chat window. Agents have to review this before engaging, ensuring they are up-to-date with the customer's needs.


Response Time

Time is of the essence when it comes to live chat. Customers may navigate away from our website quickly, so agents must address inquiries promptly. The expectation is to respond as soon as possible, keeping the customer engaged and providing timely solutions.


Greetings and Authentication

Always begin by acknowledging the information the customer has already provided. This shows that we value their time and are attentive to their needs. For inquiries related to existing orders, it's crucial to validate the customer's details against our order management system. This step ensures compliance with GDPR regulations and confirms the customer's identity. An example greeting could be: "Hello [Customer Name], I see you have an inquiry about your order #[Order Number]. For security reasons, could you please provide [Specific Detail] to verify your identity?"


Recategorization

It's possible that the chatbot may not always categorize a customer's inquiry accurately. After reviewing the conversation history and acknowledging the customer's initial request, agents should confirm with the customer that they've understood the inquiry correctly. If a re-categorization is needed, agents can adjust the category within Freshchat.


Escalation

If an issue cannot be resolved during the live chat, it's important to ensure the customer still feels supported. Agents should obtain the customer's email address, assuring them that we'll be in touch shortly with a resolution. If the customer prefers, agents can also arrange a follow-up phone call.

Email Ticket Inquiries


Emma Sleep's commitment to exceptional customer support extends to email interactions. The integration of our flowbot has added another layer to our email ticketing system, providing agents with a richer context before even starting the conversation. Here's how our agents can navigate and utilize these enhanced email tickets:


Ticket Prioritization

Currently, email tickets originating from the chatbot are treated the same as other tickets in our system. However, it's worth noting that the pre-categorization feature of the chatbot may be utilized in the future to gauge the urgency of inquiries, potentially leading to a prioritization system.


Response Protocol

Given the enriched context provided by the chatbot, agents have an advantage when addressing these tickets. However, the fundamental approach remains unchanged. Agents should treat these tickets similarly to traditional email or contact form inquiries. The only difference is the added information at hand, ensuring a more personalized and efficient response.


Recategorization

It's essential to remember that while the chatbot is a powerful tool, it's not infallible. There may be instances where the customer's inquiry wasn't categorized correctly by the chatbot. In such cases, after reviewing the details, agents should adjust the categorization within the ticket to ensure accuracy and that the right department or team addresses the issue.


Ticket Prioritization

Currently, email tickets originating from the chatbot are treated the same as other tickets in our system. However, it's worth noting that the pre-categorization feature of the chatbot may be utilized in the future to gauge the urgency of inquiries, potentially leading to a prioritization system.


Seeking Clarifications

Transparency and clarity are paramount. If any aspect of the customer's inquiry isn't clear, agents should acknowledge the information already provided and then seek further clarification. This approach ensures that we're addressing the exact concern of the customer and not making assumptions.

Phone Call Inquiries


The phone remains an integral channel for customer support at Emma Sleep, providing a personal touch and immediate assistance. Leveraging the data collected by our chatbot, we aim to make phone interactions even more streamlined and efficient. Here's a breakdown of the process for handling inquiries that transition from our chatbot to a phone call:


Transition to Phone

If a customer opts for phone support at the conclusion of their chatbot interaction (and if it's within business hours), they will be prompted to call our hotline. Simultaneously, a ticket is generated in the system, and the customer is provided with this ticket number. During the call, they should relay this number to the agent. The ticket number serves a dual purpose: it allows agents to quickly pull up the customer's information, and it authenticates the customer since it confirms they've just interacted with our chatbot and provided Order Number, Email Address and Full Name. The agent still needs to validate this info with the info in the OMS (Magento/Fluent).


Pre-emptive Information Access

Instead of agents making proactive calls based on these tickets, the system is designed for customers to reach out. When they do, agents can use the provided ticket number to instantly access the chatbot's interaction history. This means there's no need to request further authentication details from the customer, making the process smoother and more efficient.


Centralizing Customer History

To maintain a consolidated customer history, agents must follow a strict protocol. When a customer indicates that they've registered their inquiry through the website's chatbot, agents should:


  1. Open the corresponding ticket using the provided ticket number.
  2. Add the customer's phone number to their contact details in Freshdesk. This ensures that the ticket arising from the phone call is integrated into the customer's timeline, providing future agents with a comprehensive view of all interactions.
    1. Open the customer's profile in Freshdesk from the Ticket view.
    2. Locate and click on the 'Edit Contact' option.
    3. Input the customer's phone number in the designated field.
    4. Save the changes to update the customer's profile.
    5. Should there be any discrepancy, like an Aircall ticket not appearing in the timeline, agents should:
      1. Copy the phone number displayed in Aircall (removing any spaces).
      2. Search for this number in Freshdesk.
      3. Locate the Aircall ticket and verify why it isn't linked to the appropriate contact.


Ticket Status Management

Once a chatbot-generated ticket is created, it initially holds the status "Waiting for Customer". If the customer doesn't call within a stipulated timeframe and the ticket remains unattended, its status transitions to "Open". Agents should then treat it like a regular email ticket. This ensures that even if customers don't follow through with a phone call or opt out of a long wait in the phone queue, their concerns are still addressed in a timely manner.
