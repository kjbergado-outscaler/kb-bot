Making Impact on Returns

Customers can reach us for different reasons requesting to return their recently bought mattress as they have the country Guarantee (a campaign that can offer up to 100-nights of trial period) and the Warranty (right of delivering the product up to 10 years).

Always investigate the claim of the customer and identify if there is something to be done to support the product issue and avoid the customer's stress and frustration of returning or replacing the mattress. Our goal is to take all opportunities possible to deflect the return intention by improving our customer experience with their product and making them happy and satisfied with it.

​

First Investigate the reason:

  • What is the problem reported by the customer?

  • Is the claim related to the time adaptation?

  • Is it maybe related to bad usage of the mattress? How is the customer using the mattress? Common reasons upside-down mattress lack of proper slatted frame.

  • Is the claim impacting the usage of the mattress? (e.g. stain or color claim does not impact)

  • Is it clear the product is a manufacturing topic?


Returns Deflection Arguments

Let's recap some arguments related to the return reasons Customers may provide to us:

TIME ADAPTATION

Changing mattress is a big difference to your body. It is expected to the customer to feel the difference when start using the new mattress.

Explain: other customers have faced this kind of discomfort at the beginning, but it is resolved during the adjustment phase.

The body may need a period for the adjustment that can take 3-6 weeks.

BAD USAGE OF THE MATTRESS

Upside down mattress

If the customer is using the mattress on the wrong side you can always recommend him to give time for adaptation using the proper side.

Also, using the mattress on the wrong side could also lead to the mattress being too hot because the blue layer ("Airgocell" foam) also drives away heat and moisture. If the blue layer is not on top, this might reduce this effect.


Absence of Emma slatted frame / Emma bed

The slatted frame is the foundation to support your body, but at the same time, it also provides the first adjustment. The slatted frame makes up approx. 30-40% of the lying comfort.

Even with very high mattresses, you can feel the influence of the slat base. For example, if you place a folded folding rule lengthwise between the mattress and the slatted frame, you can immediately notice a change in the way you lie down.

The slatted frame should not be more than 8-10 years old, as the mattress follows the sagging slatted frame and sags faster.

TOO HARD/TOO FIRM

A comfort layer is an extra foam layer designed specifically for Emma to adjust the lying sensation.

We have two options available: soft or firm comfort layer.

HEAT SENSATION

Heat sensation ("customer sweat" or "the mattress is too hot" claims are usually due to the hug sensation the Emma mattress can provide. While hugging the customer, the customer may have the body warmed up as the body may sink to the mattress.

To avoid the sinking process, customers can add Comfort Layer.

Check if the mattress is not being used on the wrong side. It could also lead to the mattress being too hot because the blue layer ("Airgocell" foam) also drives away heat and moisture. If the blue layer is not on top, this might reduce this effect.

Use comfort layer deflection only if instructed so!

COMFORT LAYER

The comfort layer is and remains free of charge for Emma customers and you can always offer it if needed for a better customer's product experience, regardless of whether they are still in the trial period or not.

​

Why is so important to convince the customer to try a comfort layer?

To leverage the customer's product satisfaction and avoid the return.

On average, 40% of customers refuse to try the comfort layer when they find the mattress too hard/too soft.

But…60% of the customers who accept to try the comfort layers, 85% keep the mattress.

To achieve the maximum effect:

Softest Mattress: Firm inlayer on the top.

Hardest mattress: Soft inlayer on the top.

SMELL

Why does the mattress smell?

Most new mattresses will have a certain chemical odor when they are first delivered. This is especially true for those that come in a box, rolled-up and vacuum-sealed because the lack of airflow through the package can cause the “new mattress smell” to become concentrated.

When mattresses are vacuum-packed, the VOCs (Volatile Organic Compounds) remain trapped in the mattress. As soon as you open the box and remove the plastic foil, the foam expands and some of the synthetic materials in the mattress break down. These are released as a gas that causes the off-gassing smell (usually also associated with a new car, clothes or shoes)

When customers open their mattresses, they will probably notice this odor.

This is entirely normal, and the smell should fade within a few days.


Solution

  • If still partially packed, suggest the customers finish unpacking their mattresses in a well-ventilated area and tt is needed to airs the mattress. If the customer can leave it like this for a couple of days would be better.

  • Also, washing the mattress cover can help.
