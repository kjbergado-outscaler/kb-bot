Customer requests with legal positions involved


Most of the requests are complaints based on the history of the communication between Agent and customer.

After reading the inquiry (and pre communication!), it is very important to find out, how to help the customer/lawyer in the easiest way as possible.

What are the more common requests?

- Refunds

- Returns

- Claims

- Legal advices (always use the formal templates form L&C)

Most requests from lawyers are because of earlier customer experience with the Emma customer service.

It is important to note that if you encounter a mistake on our team's or the customer's part, do not fingerpoint on someone because our concern is not to "put the blame" on anyone, but to resolve the customer's concern with high satisfaction.


Process flows:

-> Please note that this article does not replace the individual steps involved in handling customer inquiries, but only instructs how to respond to a legal position in the given cases.

Complaint: customer did not received return/pick up date

1st step:

Read communication history + check order in Magento

2nd step:

If no return is received, please process as given in Knowledge Base article for return according to your country.

Sometimes the customer already got pick up dates but however they did not work out (mostly because of us).

Please make sure that the return is set correctly in Magento (see: Return Requests )

3rd step:

After making sure, that there is a fixed pick-up date that the customer can rely on, inform the lawyer that the refund will be made as a result of the return and that the proof of return should be withdrawn.


Mandatory: Use on of given templates to answer:


Legal requests - Return info (to lawyer)

Legal requests - Pick up info (to lawyer)


Complaint: customer did not received refund

1st step:

Read communication history + check order in Magento

2nd step:

If no refund is processed, please check the refund conditions according to your country (return received, tracking number provided, e.g.)

and process as given in Knowledge Base article for refunds (see: Refund requests )

3rd step:

If return is already received, you can process the refund for the affected item.

4th step:

If refund is already done, please provide lawyer proof of refund (also see: Refund requests )

Mandatory: Use template to answer:

Legal requests - Refund info (to lawyer)

Customer asks for legal advice

1st step:

Please check if the customers request is based on a legal problem, which can not be handled without help of Legal&Compliance.

2nd step:

If so, only than you can open a L&C Jira ticket. Do not forgot to put in all relevant information for the case and the customer in the ticket. Please note that L&C can only help in giving advice, but will not take on the whole case.

Also mind when escalating to L&C to translate the inquiry in English.

3rd step:

Wait for response of L&C and forward received information to customer.

NOTE: No escalations from Freshdesk anymore!

Please do not put L&Cs email address in any notes or do not escalate the ticket to L&C through Freshdesk.

Lawyer + Court fees

IMPORTANT:

- ALWAYS PROCESS THE REFUND FOR LAWYER FEES FROM REFUNDS AND NOT FROM THE ORDER.

- ONLY PROCEED WITH THE REFUND FOR LAWYER FEES AFTER THE WHOLE CASE IS SOLVED.

Escalation guildeline:

Up to 800 EUR: Need approval from supervisor

Up to 1000 EUR: Need approval from Emma Intern (CECE Managers)

From 1000 EUR: Need approval from Legal&Compliance


After getting approval from Supervisor / CE Manager / L&C escalate, as usually, to Refunds.


NOTE:

  • Please note that we mostly receive requests from lawyers in PDF format, so it is important to read this carefully;

  • These documents can be sometimes too complicated to understand due to formalities, so asking for assistance is not a problem;

  • Lawyer fees: Always refund the lawyer fees after the whole case is solved;

  • If you do not understand the content of one of these documents you can also use the same forwarding macro to forward the inquiry to L&C and ask for support.


Important: Legal will tell you what the issue is and whether you should review the refund, return, claim, etc. L&C will not take over the ticket. After that you can process normally as given in this article.

Guidelines for reporting legal notifications, letters and inquiries


When an agent receives any notification from an entity (e.g., courts, criminal authorities, law enforcement, public prosecutors, public or private entities, and so on) related to data breaches or legal inquiries (e.g. court letters, requests from police or other government agencies, etc.), it must be immediately forwarded to the Legal and Compliance Team (“L&C Team”).  

If you are unsure whether to notify the L&C Team, here are some examples of notifications that need to be notified of to the L&C Team: 

  • “Notice – Court Summons” 

  • Public Prosecutor Inquiry Regarding Consumer Claims” 

  • “Claim for Damages from XXXX” 

  • “Notice of Pending Class Action Lawsuit” 

  • “Investigation Notice from Law Enforcement” 

  • “Investigation by Public Authorities Regarding XXXXX” 

  • “Legal Demand Letter Regarding Breach of Contract” 

  • “Request for Legal Opinion on Privacy Policy Compliance” 

  • “Urgent: Regulatory Compliance Issue for Immediate Review” 

  • “Tax Investigation or Audit Notice” 

  • “Notice of Investigation / Inquiry by a Data Protection Authority” 

 

Therefore, the actions required when a notification is received are:  

Notify L&C immediately by sending an email to legalcompliance@emma-sleep.com AND providing in the email all relevant details and documentation related to the notification, including the nature of the inquiry or data breach, and the ACTUAL document from the sender. DO NOT DIRECTLY REPLY TO THE AUTHORITY/SENDER. 

 

IMPORTANT NOTE: Inquiries and/or reports related to data breach/security incidents must be sent to privacy@emma-sleep.com, following the guidelines previously shared. 

 

This process ensures that legal matters are handled promptly, and that EMMA remains compliant with the applicable laws and regulations.
