USPs


Why is temperature so important?


To ease into sleep and maintain undisturbed deep and REM sleep phases, your body needs to stay at its optimal temperature (between 34 and 35°C). However, as we tuck ourselves in bed and our skin temperature increases, it can create excess heat that needs to be eliminated. This mattress is optimized to take away excess heat, making sure your temperature is ideal for entering these stages earlier and staying there for longer.



How does it work?


The thermoregulating technology handles temperature changes and maintains a constant temperature while we sleep. Not too cold, not too hot- always the right temperature adapted to the needs of your rest.


What is the benefit and the result?


You will not toss and turn, and you will wake up less times during the night. The result: You sleep deeper and wake up refreshed.




Thermoregulation and Breathability

The layering is optimized to maximize air circulation and dissipate heat away from the body. The top layers are very open (hyper soft is a very open-cell foam, and micro coils are tiny springs so mostly air). Any excess heat created by the body overnight gets conducted away via the micro coils and the graphite-infused DD foam. This means the heat will not disturb the person and the sleep ends up being more efficient with fewer interruptions.

Better comfort

Progressive hardness across the depth of the mattress, which means the further you sink into the mattress, the more supportive it becomes. This gives you a soft, welcoming reception, and progressively firmer support. Therefore, people who need less support (lighter people) get a nice softer experience and at the same time, people who need more support (heavier people) get extra support. This can be interesting for couples.


Better Support and Ergonomics

A combination of different strength springs (3 different measures) gives the person support (in the hips) and conformity (in the shoulders) where needed. A total of 5 zones that provide more nuanced zoning for all body types and better overall support / ergonomics. Ideal for any sleep position. Normally in most mattresses, there is a tradeoff between optimization for sleeping on the side and on the back, but this zoning makes it ideal for both types.
