What is a guarantee?



A guarantee is defined as the promise for the after-sales performance of a product or service.

At Emma, we guarantee the quality and durability of our selling products.


In case the obligation is not fulfilled, Emma is responsible to replace the product, or the money paid as consideration will be refunded to the customer.



Emma works with several guarantee durations and they vary accordingly to the product type and the country. The most common guarantees offered are: The 10-years guarantee, is related to mattresses, and the 2-years guarantee is related to accessories.



Please, check your country folder for more specificities regarding products and their guarantees.




Warranty and Guarantee


A warranty is a legal requirement and a Guarantee is what the company is offering as a promise to ensure the warranty is available to the customer.


A guarantee is closely related to a warranty, as a guarantee is the promise included in the formal (and legal) warranty.


As an example, legally in Germany, any company that sells a mattress is required to offer a 2 years warranty over the product. At Emma, we grant an extended guarantee of 8 years on Emma mattresses in addition to the statutory of 2 years, therefore a total of 10 years guarantee.


There is a total guarantee, free of charge, for the first two years after purchase (based on the Warranty conditions). From the third, or fourth year of usage, the guarantee may be reduced for a certain percentage of the purchase price.

The conditions may vary from country to country.




The 10-years guarantee (Mattresses)



This guarantee starts on the day of the delivery and covers the first 10 years of use. If the customers want to avail of the guarantee, they need to send us photographic proof of the issue so we can investigate if it qualifies. The guarantee does not renew or reset if the customer receives a replacement mattress under the guarantee.



Validation criteria



The Guarantee can only be validated under the following conditions:



Sags or dips








Permanent sags or dips greater than 2.5cm and visible to the naked eye.




Cracks or hollows to the foam















No proper inflation







  • Not inflating to the correct size with a deviation of over 2cm. The mattress needs to be given 2 weeks to fully inflate.














Cover damage









  • Cover damage, like faulty zippers and stitching, or any manufacturer-identified issues. Where possible, Emma reserves the right to send a new mattress cover only instead of replacing the entire mattress








General conditions (mattresses)


  • Customers are entitled to a maximum of 3 replacement mattresses within the 10-year period.
  • If the 2 exchange or replacement mattresses also develop a fault within the 10-year period, the customer is entitled to a full refund of the purchase price upon collection.
  • The guarantee is non-transferable and is only available to the recipient of the mattress.
  • The guarantee only covers products sold on the official website, marketplaces, and retails. This does not extend to secondhand products bought from unofficial channels.

If the customers want to avail of the guarantee, they need to send us photographic proof of the issue so we can investigate if it qualifies.

It is applied mainly to Emma accessories, please check your country folder specificities. In this case, the customer is responsible to send the product to exchange.

Guarantee General conditions



The Guarantee policies do NOT extend to:

  • Normal wear and tear or any condition resulting from misuse or abuse of the product.
  • Commercial use (e.g. hotels)
  • Standard softening of the mattress foam resulting from regular use.
  • Comfort preference after the Trial period, including preferences of firmness and/or heat retention levels.
  • Damage caused by mold, odor, mildew, or discoloration caused by abnormal care such as liquid spillage, improper ventilation, or excessive humidity.
  • Mattresses that have been damaged or soiled due to improper use.
  • Cleaning the cover at excessively high temperatures;
  • Faults or damages that have occurred due to the mattress not being unboxed within a reasonable time frame (up to six weeks after delivery).
  • Mattresses that have been transported outside of the country's or our serviced delivery areas.
