If you think about a recent experience you had with a brand, you’d likely think back to a specific moment, situation, or interaction you had between product research and post-purchase. This timeline is commonly referred to as the Customer Journey.



What is Emma's customer journey?


More specifically, every time Emma welcomes new customers, those customers go through a specific decision-making process. A customer journey is an entire experience a customer has while communicating with a brand.

High-quality customer service is a crucial part of a successful customer journey. The more value we offer to potential clients, the more likely they are to complete the journey:




It is about understanding how our customers behave

and what they expect.




Pre-Sales Stage


The pre-sales phase includes all aspects of a customer’s interaction with Emma before a final purchase decision has been made. At this point of the journey, we count on a joint effort of the most diverse Teams. For example, Online and Offline marketing, and User experience. In this phase, customers are aware they have a need and are searching for possible solutions.


Awareness:

I want to sleep better!

This is probably the first thought in the customer's head, before starting to become familiar with Emma. In most cases, they have a problem that our products will be able to solve. Here, the online and offline marketing teams play an important role, they create all the communication that will awaken customers' interest to know our brand. They portray the solutions to our customer's needs. In this way, the customer starts to become familiar with Emma including through channels such as social media, website, advertising campaigns, word-of-mouth, etc.





Consideration:

Should I get an Emma Mattress?

The customer realizes that his need must be met and actively considers whether or not to buy our products. Other competitors can be also considered.

Here, the customer tends to navigate our Social Media and especially on our websites. In this stage, the User Experience Team plays an important role in making information clear and easily available to boost customer consideration. Check below some typical touchpoints during this stage.

During this time, some customers tend to contact our customer support agents to understand more about the Emma 100 nights trial period, referral program, delivery time, discount codes, guarantees, product curiosities, payment details, and more.




Pre-Delivery Stage


The Pre-delivery stage covers all interactions customers have with Emma after completing the payment until the product arrives at their doorstep.

Purchase:

Order completed with success!


During this phase, the order confirmation email with the shipping status, the estimated and actual delivery time, and any change of the confirmed order by the customer can be done.

At Emma, we manage our orders using an Order Management System. That's how a customer service agent can track and edit any existing order, or solve customers' issues. For example, if the customer wishes to change the delivery address or product after the purchase, we can support that!



Post-Purchase Stage


The Post-delivery stage covers all interactions customers have with Emma after receiving and using the products purchased.


Product Experience:

Good news!

Your Emma product just arrived!

The wait time came to an end, it's time to try your brand new Emma product. In this phase, customers are excited and full of expectations. They will finally pass through all the experiences Emma promised to deliver, from unboxing to actually trying the product. It's time to sleep better!


Naturally, in this phase, some of the customers will seek guidance from Emma. It is important to highlight that lot of the hard work that went into encouraging the sale is wasted through poor management of the post-purchase customer experience. In this way, it's required from Emma Customer Service enough skills to manage customers' expectations, solve doubts, and even the ability to deflect a return.


It’s significantly easier and approximately five times less expensive to retain

customers than it is to bring in new ones.
