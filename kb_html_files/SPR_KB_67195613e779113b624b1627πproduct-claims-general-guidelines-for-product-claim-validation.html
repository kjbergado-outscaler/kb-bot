When a customer reports an issue with the quality of their Emma product, it's considered a product or warranty claim. Properly handling and logging these claims is crucial for Emma. This ensures our product quality team can track and enhance the quality of our products.

Not all claims can be accepted. To determine which claims are valid, please refer to the Product Quality Guidebooks. It's essential to follow these guidelines precisely.

1. Assessing the claims with the Validation Guidebooks

Note: Need a refresher on the product claim categories ? See the article 'Claims: Product Claim Categories'

Each instruction page contains the following items

  • Claim Component: The part of the product that is affected

  • Claim reason: Classification of what is wrong with the product

  • Description​: Description of the defect reason

  • Acceptance Range​: If the issue from the customer is within the range, the product is not faulty and you must reject the claim (look at the 'Note' section). If the issue is out of the acceptable range, we must accept that the product claim is valid.

  • Note​: A description of what you can tell the customer to explain why we can or cannot reject their claim.

  • Pictures: Example pictures of the information you need to collect to process the product claim case.

3. Verify if the images are legit

Always double-check what kind of photo is being provided. Ask yourself these questions:

Does the photo provided match the product the customer ordered? You can check this with Fluent Commerce / Magento.

Does this photo look like something taken off the internet?

Suspicious behavior

If you suspect the photo may be a photo that was scrapped (taken off the internet), there is a simple way to check that:

  1. Open Google Reverse Image Search

  2. Click on the "Search by Image" option (camera icon):

  3. Select the "Upload an Image" option, and select the photos provided by the customer

Once you have done this, Google will show you if there are any visually similar images on the internet, which may lead to the conclusion as to if they are legitimate proof of claim.

If you find that the pictures are not original or not from an Emma product, reject the claim and inform your supervisor.

4. Data Privacy - Canned response

To ensure that we comply with the data minimization principle, and we do not collect unnecessary personal data from our customers, we should properly inform customers beforehand when addressing product claim requests/inquiries.

When faced or working on a ticket related to product claims, make sure to send the following disclaimer to the customer before asking them to send the photos.

Dear [Insert Customer's First Name],

Thank you for reaching out to us.

We are very sorry to hear about the issue you encountered with your order, but we can assure you that we are fully committed to resolving this as quickly as possible, to avoid any further inconvenience.

At this point, so that we can better understand the issue, and provide you with an appropriate solution, we would kindly request that you follow the picture guidelines we have attached to this email, and send us the pictures or videos requested there. Please ensure that these images or videos clearly capture the issues you encountered, as this will help us expedite the review process.

Important guidelines for submitting photos or videos:
1. No personal data: Please do not include any personal data in the photos or videos you will send us. This includes:

  • Faces or voices of individuals;

  • Any identifiable information such as names, addresses, or contact details.

2. Privacy presumption: By submitting photos or videos, we presume that you have ensured no personal data is included. This measure is in place to protect your privacy and comply with data protection regulations.

3. Use of photos and videos: The photos or videos you provide us will be used solely for the purpose of assessing and resolving your product claim. We will not share these files with third parties except as necessary to process your claim.

Disclaimer:

  • By submitting photos or videos, you grant us the right to use these materials for the sole purpose of evaluating and resolving your product claim;

  • Additionally, by submitting the photos or videos, you declare that they do not contain any personal data of any individuals.

Thank you for your patience and cooperation on this matter. We look forward to hearing from you soon and remain available in the meantime if you should require further assistance.
