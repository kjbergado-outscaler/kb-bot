Introduction

This article contains the most up-to-date version of our quality grid for frontline contacts. At the moment this is being used in all markets for normal Customer Service quality audits and is fully integrated in Miuros.

5.
 Communication

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Empathy and
Cognitive Harmony (S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
showed empathy in a truthful and impactful way. All statements/reactions made
during the contact felt genuine, were appropriate to the situation at hand
and showed a willingness to connect with the customer.

Agent
failed to react to the customer's state of mind, especially when the customer
was disappointed.



Agent said/wrote things that were in cognitive dissonance with the
customer's state of mind and the situation at hand.

 

Agent's
empathy statements/reactions should resonate with the customer. The goal here
is to amplify a positive occurrence and minimize a poor experience.

20%

25%

Emma Tone
(S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
communicated with the customer using the Emma tone of voice. As such, the
agent displayed a positive and engaging attitude throughout the interaction,
showed genuine willingness to help and was always professional, even if the
communication style was informal.

Agent
was numb and/or robotic throughout the contact.



Agent had a negative approach to the case and/or engaged in negative
communication or negative reinforcement.



 

Note
1: Communication should be fluid and feel like an actual conversation.

Note 2: Agent should not engage in negative communication or negative
reinforcement.

Note 3: Agents should always address the customer in an informal way,
unless the market specifically requested for a formal approach to be the
standard or if the customer had a negative reaction to an informal approach.

Personalization
(S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
followed the contact opening and closure guidelines.

Communication (O)

Pass/Fail/NA

Management (S)

Pass/Fail

Agent
took only the time required to handle the interaction and apologised when our
