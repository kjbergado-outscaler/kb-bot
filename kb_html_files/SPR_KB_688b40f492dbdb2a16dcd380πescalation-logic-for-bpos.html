1. Global Process and Workflow

​Global Process and Workflow / Market specifics: Is your market present? Yes: Click your Market tab, ignore Global Process. No: refer to the Global Process and Workflow.

1.1 Overview

The process flow outlines the movement between teams and roles based on the type of escalation and the source that initiated it, ensuring a clear understanding of responsibilities and actions. If you have to escalate one of your cases, do it manually according to the following flow.

​

​Let's start by understanding roles:

​

1.2 Roles

Different intervenients have different roles for an escalated ticket.

Role
Description

Junior Agent

Level 1 Agents: First contact with customer.

​

OR only: OR Agents are not responsible for resolving issues that came in through social channels (Meta, IG, TP) — only for public replies and case creation.

​

OR agents perform content moderation. They reply to interactions with customers. e.g.: Customer: "Hey, I love Emma, haven't sleep this good in years!" OR Agent's reply: "Great to hear such feedback, makes us sleep much better too!"

​

If at any point the customer needs customer support or assistance, escalate to a Senior Agent, Level 2

Senior Agent

Level 2 Agent: Experienced agent (Email cases); for OR - Investigates and resolves reputation-related cases

Escalations Squad

Level 3 support: alternative support team for Legal and Complex cases.

Supervisor

Level 3 support: Last line of support, when Level 2 support can't solve an issue / situation.

​

Exception: HLE cases from Emma stores escalate from HLE Agent straight to a Supervisor.

​

Escalates to: Level 2 Agent or (Exception: if HLE agent and needs to escalate a non-complex case, escalates directly to a Supervisor)

The legal team receives a court letter or an email from a customer’s lawyer about a legal case against Emma.

​

Cases already involving legal action or reputational risks (ex. court letters, lawyers contacting Emma, GDPR breaches and cases that seem to require urgent attention from higher management or legal teams).

​

Note: if a customer simply threats to take us to court, but does not present an official document, it is not considered a legal case. Apply your best effort to aknowledge their situation and strive for a solution. If need be, escalate.

Complex cases happen when a customer’s issue remains unresolved despite repeated contact, requiring intervention from other teams beyond the initial support level (exceptional situations: tried to follow standard process multiple times and it's not working, lack of information on Knowledge base to address the case, lack of responsiveness of internal team, possible errors from internal teams).

1.4 How to Escalate a case

Find here the flow to escalate a case:

Acknowledge the customer’s concern

​

  • Respond with empathy and confirm that you understand their issue.

  • Explain that their case will be reviewed by another team, an internal team, or a supervisor (e.g., Senior Agents, Escalation Squad).
    Example:

“I understand how frustrating this must be for you. I’ll forward your case to our specialized team for further review.”

​

  1. Verify customer information

    • Ensure all details are accurate (e.g., order number, customer name, email address).

  2. Document the reason for escalation

    • Add clear and concise case details in the Collaboration Note, explaining why further escalation is required.

  3. Ensure an email is linked to the case

    • If the case is not already associated with an email, click Set Email and add the customer’s email address.

  4. Assign to the correct team

    • For internal review: Assign to Escalation Squad/Senior Agent.

    • For supervisor review: Assign to [Supervisor Name or Queue].

    ​

​

  1. Review the case history

    • Check the case notes and the escalation justification before taking action.

  2. Investigate the issue

    • Gather all relevant information to understand the customer’s concern fully.

  3. Contact the customer

    • Provide a clear, empathetic, and complete response, ensuring their issue is resolved or next steps are clearly communicated.

What to do if you disagree with the Escalation

  1. Record your feedback

    • Add your reasoning in the Case Notes.

  2. Report the case

    • During the Hypercare (first weeks of implementation) we will have an excel file for you to report this cases, please request the file to your team leader

      ​

  3. Resolve situation - We still want to make sure the situation is resolved for the customer
