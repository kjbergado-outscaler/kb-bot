Introduction

About 80% of all Emma orders are delivered on time. We globally dispatch various trucks loaded with products every day. The remaining 20% of orders might include delays due to various reasons, such as production or logistic capacity, unforeseen events impacting infrastructure or resource availability, and system issues.


This article will help you understand what to do in case a customer is reaching out to ask you:


"Where Is My Order?"

Finding an order and understanding its status.

Agents should look for each of the products that are part of the order directly on one of the available tracking sheets depending on the customer's country:

CountryTracking sheet
MexicoKPIs Mexico
ChileKPIs Chile
ColombiaKPIs Colombia
US TBD
CanadaTBD


From the file, you should be able to determine if an order is on time or delayed.


Only If you can't find an order on the tracking sheet or it is missing information like: tracking label, delivery date, etc; please raise it through the communication file between support and the warehouse (request order).


If the order is on time, share the estimated delivery date with the customer along with the tracking label (this should also be on the tracking file).


You should always keep in mind that some products may be out of stock, so you should check the Stock Visibility file daily.

It will give you detailed information about when the products will be arriving to our warehouse and when it will be arriving to the customer.


If the order is delayed or has any error (stuck), determine the cause and length of the delay and communicate with the warehouse through the appropriate request order file depending on the country:

CountryRequest order
MexicoRequest Order MX
ChileRequest Order CL
ColombiaRequest Order CO
US TBD
CanadaTBD


Once you have determined the cause and length of the delay, communicate it to the customer along with the estimated delivery date (if available). This is also the time when you should offer alternatives appropriate to the reason of the delay (Compensation, change of product, etc.)


Additionally, you should request an update on the order status and an estimated delivery date (if there is currently none) through the request order file. While you receive a response, you should keep the case In Progress, check the request order file and update the customer daily.


You should only consider the case resolved once you have provided a delivery date & a tracking number to the customer.


If despite your best efforts, the customer decides to cancel the order. Please follow the Cancellations article.
