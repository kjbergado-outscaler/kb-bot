Introduction

This article is meant for the agents working in the Online Reputation team for Brazil Reclame Aqui website.

What is Reclame Aqui?

Reclame AQUI is the OFFICIAL channel for Brazilian consumers! It acts as an independent communication channel between consumers and businesses.

Contrary to what many think, Reclame AQUI is not just a complaints site, but a research site! Did you know that over 92% of consumers use Reclame Aqui website to research a company's reputation before making a purchase?

It is a channel in which the consumer can expose the problems and inefficiency of the companies' service channels, which is currently considered one of the main sources of information about consumption in the country.

How does it work?

Reclame AQUI is a very important channel for consumers to solve their problems with companies, but much more than that, Reclame AQUI is a research site used by millions of consumers before making a purchase or closing a deal, so it is very important that some basic guidelines are followed, so that the complaint process is fair and honest!

The reputation of the companies is automatically calculated by Reclame AQUI, with no manual interference in the calculation of data or updating of indexes.

The Reclame AQUI database is completely confidential and non-marketable and the data of companies and consumers that are registered on the site only serve to enable access and service between the two parties. Reclame AQUI will, at its discretion, publicize the media and forward complaints to bodies and authorities that can collaborate in the defense of collective interests.

All information on the origin of consumer messages is stored in our database and all public content on the site is automatically indexed by Google and other search engines.

Always remembering that your personal data are not exposed publicly, as long as they are not inserted by the consumer or company in the body of the public text.

Reclame AQUI does not accept anonymous contributions or those registered as nicknames, as all complaints are signed and are the responsibility of the interested parties.

You also must not post complaints against natural or political persons.

It is important to know that there are Terms of Use and that every user must know it.

To complain, the customer just need to register on their website, as without it they will not be able to post a complaint.

Any Brazilian citizen over 18 years old and registered with a valid CPF (NIF) on the Reclame AQUI website can file a complaint.

In addition to the two factors mentioned, it is important to remember that in order to claim they must have a consumer or commercial relationship with the complained company.

Reclame AQUI does not carry out any type of prior analysis of the complaint, that is, before it is published on the website.

It is a consumer's right to expose their dissatisfaction in a consumer relationship with a certain company.

However, some pejorative terms ('crime', 'injured', for example) can be edited automatically.

In addition, when a complaint violates the Terms of Use of Reclame AQUI, both companies and consumers can contact us requesting the moderation of inappropriate content.

How is the status of a claim defined?

The complaint in Reclame AQUI has different statuses, understand better:

The little red face means that the complaint has been posted, but has not yet been responded to;

The little blue face means that the complaint was answered by the company;

The yellow icon means that the complaint was responded to by the company, but the complainant/company made a reply after the response;

The green face means that the complaint was answered by the company and the complainant evaluated saying that the problem was solved;

The little purple face means that the complaint was answered by the company and the complainant evaluated saying that the problem was not resolved.

How to reply - Guideline

Before responding, we must try to contact the customer by telephone to provide support in whatever they need, if the customer does not answer, they must send an email asking for the best time to contact and send the resolution of the case by email as well, always make yourself available and ask the customer by e-mail what is the best time to call again, so you can hear them out.

On a phone call or by e-mail – The agent should:

  • Greet the customer;
  • Introduce himself;
  • Offer to help.
  • Show empathy if the situation calls for it;
  • Take notes, to prevent going back to the customer if the agent happens to forget something;
  • Align with the customer to make sure everything is clear and that the queries were understood correctly.
  • Gather all relevant information regarding the customer’s queries;
  • Clarify any doubts that may have come up regarding the situation at hand;
  • Clarify what happened and (if possible and applicable) why it happened;
  • Inform what was done on our end and what the next steps are;
  • Set expectations regarding timelines (when applicable);
  • Educate the customer on our processes and procedures (when applicable) , in order to avoid replicas on the platform.
  • Offer availability to assist with other queries;
  • Bid the customer farewell in a pleasant way.

And then, after contacting the customer by phone or e-mail the agent must reply on the platform

Reputation

The company's reputation is generated from the reviews of consumers who have posted a complaint against a particular company.

Reclame AQUI presents the reputation in 3 ways: in the 6-month tab, 12-month tab and in the general tab.

It is important to remember that the consumer can only evaluate the service after a public response from the company, but the deadlines below also need to be considered:

• Companies with less than 50% of complaints answered:* the consumer is able to evaluate, even without the company's public response, after 3 consecutive days of publication.

• Companies with more than 50% of complaints answered:* the consumer is able to evaluate, even without the company's public response, 30 calendar days after publication.

Note 1: the percentage of complaints answered rule is considered from the 6-month tab of the reputation panel;

Note 2: Complaints evaluated without a public response from the company will have the status updated to “Evaluated”, but will continue to count as “Unanswered” for companies.

Based on consumer evaluations, Reclame AQUI analyzes some criteria for formulating reputation. Are they:

Response Index (RI) - Percentage of complaints answered, considering that only the first response is considered;

Average Ratings (Consumer Rating) (MA) - Takes into account only finalized and rated complaints. It corresponds to the arithmetic mean of the scores (ranging from 0 to 10) given by the complainants to assess the service received.

Solution Index (IS) - Takes into account only finalized and evaluated complaints. It corresponds to the percentage of complaints where consumers, at the end, considered that the problem that originated the complaint had been resolved. These complaints are represented by the green status icon. (Click here to learn more);

New Business Index (Would you do business again?) (IN) - Takes into account only finalized and evaluated complaints. It corresponds to the percentage of complaints where consumers, at the end, informed that, yes, they would do business with the company again.

To calculate the Evaluation of the Reclame AQUI (AR) a weighted average is made based on these criteria above.

All criteria have values ranging from 0 to 100, with the exception of the Average (varies from 0 to 10), so the average value of the evaluations is multiplied by 10, to be on the same scale as the others.

The criteria have different weights, as follows:

Response Index - Weight 2

Average Ratings (Consumer Rating) - Weight 3

Solution Index - Weight 3

New Business Index (Would you do business again?) - Weight 2

Thus, the formula for obtaining the Evaluation of the Claim HERE is as follows:

AR = ((IR * 2) + (MA * 10 * 3) + (IS * 3) + (IN * 2)) / 100

For 8 =< AR <= 10 , GREAT

For 7 =< AR <= 7.9 , GOOD

For 6 =< AR <= 6.9 , REGULAR

For 5 =< AR <= 5.9 , BAD

For AR < 5 , NOT RECOMMENDED

This formula is only applied if the company undergoes 2 prior assessments:

1 - Have a response rate greater than 50%. If it has a response rate of less than 50%, it is automatically categorized as NOT RECOMMENDED. This is done to ensure that companies that do not respond to their consumers are not benefited, as all the analyzed parameters are obtained from the service provided by the companies;

2 - Have a number of reviews equal to or greater than 10. If you have less, the company will have a reputation WITHOUT INDEX.

Selo RA 1000

The Selo RA 1000 (RA seal 1000) was created with the aim of rewarding companies that have excellent service rates in Reclame AQUI. Companies that have this seal demonstrate their commitment to their after-sales service to their consumers, raising the level of confidence in their brand, services and products.

For a company to be qualified with the RA 1000 seal, it must meet the 5 criteria listed below:

Have a number of reviews equal to or greater than 50;

Have a Response Rate equal to or greater than 90%;

Have a Solution Index equal to or greater than 90%;

Have an Average Rating (Consumer Rating) equal to or greater than 7;

Have a New Business Index (would do business again) equal to or greater than 70%.

No company that reaches such a level receives the seal immediately.

Those that meet the objective criteria necessary to obtain the seal for the first time undergo a technical audit before granting the seal, which assesses various aspects in the set of complaints, including deactivations.

This prevents companies that have somehow circumvented the rules from being qualified as RA 1000.

Companies that present a history of analysis on their reputation are not eligible for the RA 1000 Seal for a period of 1 year, from the reputation regularization.
