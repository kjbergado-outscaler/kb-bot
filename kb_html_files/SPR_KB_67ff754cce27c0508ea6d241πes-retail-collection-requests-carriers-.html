Returns products in GLS

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to create a pick-up request in the GLS carrier portal when a retailer informs that the customer wants to return a closed product:

Steps for Creating a Pick-Up Request:

  1. Log in to the GLS Carrier Portal:

    • Access the GLS carrier portal with the appropriate login credentials.

  2. Create a New Pick-Up Request:

    • In the portal, find the section for creating a " Solic. Recogida" request and start a new request.

  3. Fill in the "Datos de Recogida" (Pick-Up Details) Section: Complete all the necessary fields as follows:

    • Customer's Name:

      • Enter the name of the customer who is requesting the return.

    • Address:

      • Provide the address where the pick-up should take place. This is the customer’s address for the return.

    • Phone:

      • Enter the customer's contact telephone number. This will be used by the carrier in case they need to contact the customer for any pick-up related inquiries.

    • Email:

      • Enter the email address of the customer or the retailer (whichever is relevant for communication regarding the pick-up).

    • Vehículo (Vehicle):

      • Set this to “Furgoneta” by default, as this is the typical vehicle used for the pick-up.

    • Obs (Observations):

      • Add any relevant notes or observations. For example, you can mention that pick-up times cannot be guaranteed. You could write: "We cannot assure pick-up time."

    • Contenido (Content):

      • Specify the SKU(s) of the products that are being returned. List the items that need to be picked up.

    • N Bultos (Number of Packages):

      • Indicate the quantity of packages that need to be picked up. This should match the number of items or packages being returned.

  4. Review the Information:

    • Double-check that all the details are filled out accurately to avoid any issues with the pick-up process.

Summary of Fields to Complete in the GLS Portal:

  • Customer's Name: Full name of the customer.

  • Address: Pick-up location.

  • Phone: Customer’s contact number.

  • Email: Customer or retailer’s email.

  • Vehículo: Furgoneta (default).

  • Obs: Additional notes (e.g., no guaranteed pick-up time).

  • Contenido: List of SKUs for return.

  • N Bultos: Number of packages to be picked up.

​

By following this process, you can ensure that the pick-up request is submitted accurately and efficiently, allowing GLS to handle the return process as smoothly as possible.

Returns products with charities/NGO

Here is the updated process for handling the return of open, damaged, or broken products with the additional steps for contacting NGOs:

Process for Handling Open, Damaged, or Broken Products:

  1. Identify the Issue:

    • When a product is open, damaged, or broken, it needs to be handled differently from regular returns.

  2. Request Collection from the Closest NGO:

    • Action: Contact the NGO nearest to the customer’s location for product collection. This can be done through the local NGO’s collection process.

    • NGO List - Spain

  3. Wait for NGO Response (5 Days):

    • Action: Allow up to 5 days for a response from the NGO regarding the collection.

    • If the NGO responds and confirms the collection, proceed accordingly.

  4. Follow-up with Mobel if No NGO Response:

    • If there is no response from the NGO within 5 days, contact Mobel for assistance.

    • Important: Use Mobel as a last resort to reduce costs. Contact Mobel only in strictly necessary cases.

  5. Mobel Collection (If Required):

    • If the NGO does not confirm the collection or if the NGO is unavailable, contact Mobel for arranging the return and collection of the open/damaged/broken products.

  6. Inform Mobel that the Products can be destroyed or taken to a Recycling point:

    • Action: If Mobel is contacted for collection, inform them that the damaged, opened, or broken products can either be destroyed or taken to a recycling point (authorized waste disposal facility) for proper disposal.

    • Note: This step should be avoided unless absolutely necessary, as it involves higher costs.

  7. Mobel Confirmation and Final Procedure:

    • Wait for confirmation from Mobel and proceed with the indicated option (destruction or delivery to a recycling point).

Returns products for bulk orders: Special specifications for Retailers

The retailer places a bulk order for their warehouse and then distributes these products to end customers. However, in situations such as warranty claims or product returns, the retailer doesn't have direct access to the order number or specific information related to the end customer.

Current Process:

  1. Request the Retailer’s Purchase Invoice:

    • Action: If the retailer has not sent the purchase invoice via email, the first step is to request it.

    • Objective: The purchase invoice is necessary to verify the transaction details, ensuring that the product in question was correctly purchased and is within the warranty or claim period.

  2. Search for Information in the "B2B Pallets Iberia.xlsx" File:

    • Action: Open the "B2B Pallets Iberia.xlsx" file and go to the IBERIA Pallets- Complete in FC tab.

    • Objective: This file contains the order and sales information for retailers, allowing you to trace the SKU and the retailer who made the purchase.

  3. Search for the Retailer’s Name:

    • Action: In the mentioned tab, go to column D, which contains the retailer names.

    • Objective: Find the specific retailer who made the purchase in order to verify the transaction details.

  4. Search for the Product’s SKU:

    • Action: Once the retailer’s name is located, check the SKU of the product in column I.

    • Objective: The SKU is crucial for identifying the exact product that was sold, which is essential for processing any claims or warranty requests.

  5. Verify Information:

    • Action: With the retailer’s invoice and the SKU obtained from the spreadsheet, you can confirm that the product corresponds to a legitimate order and verify if it meets the conditions for a claim (warranty, returns, etc.).

    • Objective: Ensure the product is within the agreed terms for claims or returns, and meets warranty policies.
