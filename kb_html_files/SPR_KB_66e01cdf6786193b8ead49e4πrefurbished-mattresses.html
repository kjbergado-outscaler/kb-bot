Emma One Refurbished & Emma O2 Refurbished


Goods receipt and goods acceptance

A truck consists of 7-8 pallets with approx. 10 mattresses each, with some of the mattress models and sizes being mixed.

Sorting process


Step 1: Sorting with cover


  • Identification of the mattress type (Emma One Medium / Hard, Emma Original, or Emma O2

  • Removal of faulty mattresses from the delivery (mattresses of quality C for example, non-standard dimensions (height, width, length).

  • deformed foam

  • deformed surface

  • deformed corners or from the mentioned categories (photos appendix 2 in this article).



These mattresses are quality C (destined for disposal)

Before disposal, the covers and tricots must be removed from the quality C mattresses.



Step 2: Sorting without cover


  • Remove the cover and tricots from the remaining mattresses to check the quality of the foams.

  • Remove mattresses whose foam is not compliant, e.g.: Loose layers of foam, stains, and dirt on the foam, mold, cuts, holes, or similar (see photos Appendix 2 in this article).


The mattresses with foam defects are considered quality C (destined for disposal) and are treated as such (see Step 1).

The remaining mattresses are quality B (destined for reprocessing).


Refurbishment Process


Of the remaining mattresses, it is checked that they all comply with the reprocessing. These mattresses are quality B and the following is done:

  • Quality B mattress cores are fed into the UV sanitizing machine in batches, ensuring high utilization of the machine to increase throughput.
  • For large mattresses (wider than 1 meter), 2-man handling is recommended.
  • Collect the sanitized mattress cores in single-sort pallets (size and hardness) in preparation for vacuuming and rolling.

Packing

According to the size and type of mattress, a new tricot and cover are put on (Emma One, Emma Original/ Emma O2).


The mattress is wrapped in plastic, vacuumed, folded (for mattresses wider than 1 meter), and rolled (automatic process).


Appendix 1: Visual product identification

The returned products are mostly the mattresses that are now offered for sale on the EMMA website or through secondary distribution channels, namely Emma Original, Emma One Hard/Medium, and Emma O2.







Appendix 2: Photos for every quality category

QUALITY C


Is defective due to:

  • Dimensions not conforming to standards

  • Deformations

  • Irregular surface/ rounded corners

  • Foam not properly bonded

  • Dirty/ stained/ moldy

  • Foam broke or cut





This quality can NOT be refurbished.







QUALITY B


Mattresses with yellowed foams, and stains if these can be removed by disinfection, missing or torn/perforated nets.

In this case, the netting can be removed and the foams should be checked to ensure they are still well bonded.



This quality can be refurbished.

QUALITY A



Mattresses packed in an Emma box that has no quality defects and can be sold to the Emma customer. These can be new products or refurbished products.
