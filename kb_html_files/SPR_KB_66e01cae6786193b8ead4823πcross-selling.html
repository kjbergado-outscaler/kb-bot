What is Cross-selling?


Cross-selling is one of the advanced sales techniques commonly used to increase revenue. It means supplementing an existing product with another product, in order to improve the functions of the actual product, or increasing the utility value of the original product.


In a nutshell is: to offer more products based on what the customer is originally looking to buy. Through this, we can improve the overall experience of the client.


For example, a customer wants to buy a notebook and you manage to sell them a set of pens because they are frequently bought together.


How to Cross-sell


The first important thing for cross-selling sales is the mindset: At Emma, we don't sell individual mattresses. At Emma, we sell a sleep system and offer holistic solutions to our customers.


From all of Emma's products portfolio, it's important to select what you will offer based on the functionality of the item. Here it's how we can distinguish between the following purpose of the products to offer in a cross-sell:



Necessary items


Those will make the main item ready for use. For example, a customer that is buying an Emma mattress, but doesn't have the proper bed support, will need to buy a slatted frame.



Complementary items


These are useful additions to the main item, which completes it to make it better. You can obtain more results by using those items together.

An example is buying a pillow together with a mattress since the pillow will lead to spine alignment for the neck vertebrae. Or even a mattress protector, that preserves the mattress quality for longer.


Extra items


These maybe not be directly related to the usefulness of the main item, it's an extra element. It can add value to it or extend its range of uses by combining it as an additional element.

The Emma Sleep App can be an example of this, as the client can use it to solve sleep problems.




Examples of products to offer in a Cross-sell approach


Some products lend themselves to being sold in combination. In all cases, itÂ´s important to have in mind that you can solve customer problems with different solutions.


Let's see some examples of product combinations and the arguments you can use to do the sales speech for offering them to the customers:


Mattress + Pillow


"The mattress is only responsible for orthopedics up to the shoulder. It is important that you have a pillow at the right height".



Mattress + Mattress protector


"We lose about 1.800 liters of fluids over a 10-year period, the mattress protector helps you to keep the durability and quality of your mattress".


"You said you have an old mattress that you now want to replace. We always recommend replacing the slatted frame along with the mattress, as this is made of wood, which is known to work. But back to your mattress..."



Mattress + Slatted frame


It is important that the question of the slatted frame becomes second nature. After the mattress sale is over, the question should be part of the standard repertoire:


"The mattress can only be as good as the frame that supports it. The slatted frame should therefore not be older than 8-10 years."



Pilow + Duvet


"Many people also renew their duvet with their pillow to create a fresh new feeling in bed."



Mattress + Duvet


"You said you tend to sweat. Did you know that besides the mattress, the duvet plays a decisive role here? Warm air rises and the removal of vapor moisture from sweating is also regulated by the duvet..."



Box Spring Bed + Topper


"Remember that the pressure relief on the box spring bed is almost entirely through the topper. We have two toppers to choose from..."



Each market has different products available on the webshop, and due to this the products combinations to cross-sell can also be diferent. Please always check your market strategy before doing a cross-sell.





The anchor technique


In addition to obvious offers, you can root for other offers early in the conversation. The anchor technique is perfect for this. During the needs analysis, we can mention naturally that there is another product we need to look into during the conversation.

Let's check the examples below:


Example 1 -> Mattress + pillow


Agent: "You have back pain?"

Customer: "Yes."

Agent: "Normally back and neck pain occur together, is this also the case for you?"

Customer: "Yes."

Agent: "That is a pity and I hope we can solve the problem in the best possible way. That's why we'll take up the subject of the neck again later and talk about a pillow, but let's focus first on helping you to find the perfect mattress...".



Example 2 -> Mattress + mattress protector


Agent: "You said you have a small child who also sleeps in your bed, right?! I have a good tip for that, there is the mattress protector. But first, let's take care of your mattress".
