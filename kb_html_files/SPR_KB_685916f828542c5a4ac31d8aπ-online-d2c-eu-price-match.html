What a price-match is

A price-match request is when a customer who has already placed an order (or attemped)—whether or not the items have shipped/delivered—discovers that the exact same product (identical SKU, colour, size, model, etc.) is now advertised on our website at a lower price. The customer reaches out and asks us to refund the difference so their final cost reflects the new, lower price.

This situation typically occurs when the order was placed before a promotion went live and the markdown appears afterward.

​

Why do we price-match?

This is a retention tool: it keeps the order (and the customer) with us instead of driving them toward a cancellation or return.

The one-question test

  • If the customer cancelled/returned today, could they immediately reorder the same identical item from us at a lower price?
    Yes → We should price-match.
    No → A price-match isn’t applicable;

Honouring the lower price up front is more cost effective—and far friendlier—than processing a return shipment or cancelling the order.

IMPORTANT points to consider before proceeding

  • Trial-period only – We honour price-match requests solely within the customer’s trial window (e.g., the 100-night mattress trial).

  • Exact item, no bundles/packs or give aways – Match the price only for the identical SKU. Bundle deals, free-gift promotions, are excluded.

  • No external or store pricing – For now we don’t match prices from third-party retailers or Emma stores (Except NL, check market specific guidelines)

  • Market-specific rules – Some countries have additional conditions or caps. Always check the Market-Specific Policies section before confirming a match.

  • Multiple drops, multiple price-matches – If the price drops more than once during the trial period and the customer requests it each time, grant every valid price-match request.

  • Document in Fluent – Add a clear note for each refund

  • Follow refund guidelines– Process the adjustment exactly as outlined in the refund guidelines to avoid rejections.

  • Price matching covers only reductions in the item’s price. We compare what the customer originally paid with the current price for the identical SKU—nothing more. If a customer simply forgot to apply a promo code, that is not a price-match case and should be handled under the standard guidelines.

  • No senior or supervisory approval is required; however, if a situation falls outside these guidelines, please escalate it for further support or authorization.

  • By default, refunds are back to the original payment method and may take up to 14 business days to reflect in the customer’s account, from the moment the refund is issued. If the original payment method is clearly unavailable (e.g., a closed account), note this in the Jira ticket and ask the Refund Team to issue the refund via IBAN instead. Inform the customer that they will receive an email requesting their IBAN details.

STEP 1 - Check the order status

Order not yet delivered

  • First, verify the payment status.

  • Then follow the appropriate price-match steps for Approved, Pending, or Rejected payments, as per below:

Payment status

What to tell the customer

Actions

Approved

“Your payment has cleared. We can adjust the total to today’s lower price.”

Request the price-match refund

Rejected

“The payment didn’t go through. Please try again with another payment method or contact your bank. Once the new order succeeds, we’ll match the lower price.”

No price match until a successful payment.*

Pending

“Your payment is still processing. Let’s wait up to 24 hours. I’ll follow up then.”

Re-check in 24 hours:

  • If approved → treat as Approved.

  • If declined → treat as Rejected.

*If an order is initially rejected—say, because the payment fails and the order is cancelled—but the customer later manages to complete the purchase, you may honor the lower price shown on their first attempt once the new payment is confirmed. Ask the customer to place the new order at the current price, then contact back so we can refund the difference.

Order already delivered

  • Confirm whether the item is still inside the trial period

    • Within the window: request a refund to the price-match.

    • Outside the window: explain that the eligibility period has passed and a price match is no longer possible.

Market Specific Guidelines

  • UK & IE - If the customer is requesting a price- match against a give-away/bundles/pack item eg: The mattress now comes with a pillow for examples, please escalate to a senior/supervisor for a 10% promo-code.

  • DE,BE,AT,PT & ES- If the customer is requesting a price- match against a give-away/bundles/pack item eg: The mattress now comes with a pillow for examples, please escalate internally for a final decision.

  • NL - Price matching is available for e‑commerce retailers; please include the retailer’s name in the subject line of the refund request.

    If a customer requests a price match against a giveaway, bundle, or pack item (e.g., a mattress that now comes with a free pillow), please escalate the case internally for a final decision.
