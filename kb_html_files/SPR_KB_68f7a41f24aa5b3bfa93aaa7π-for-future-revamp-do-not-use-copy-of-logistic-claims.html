Logistic Claim: What

Logistic claims are complaints of the customers related to inconveniences experienced during the delivery of the products that they’ve ordered. Claims can lead to a cancellation, return, replacement, or even monetary compensation (which is a possible refund of a certain amount of the cost paid by the Customer, based on the country-specific Compensation matrix).

Note: Logistic Claims can now be submitted for incidents during the Return/Pick-up of an Item.

Logistic Claim: How to register

Logistic claims should be logged using the Guided Worfklow in Sprinklr, you no longer need to log them inside Magento or Fluent Commerce, so make sure to fill accordingly the Logistic Claim field when managing your ticket.

For more information about the Guided Workflow, Review the artile How to Claim Management (Product & logistic claims), in the Claims folder.

Claim Reasons

Customers may reach us for multiple reasons, whether it be a carrier misbehavior, or due to an item never being received (as a lost item by the carrier, for example).

Note: A delay in delivery should not be logged as a logistic Claim, but instead, under WIMO category.


First Decision to take: What is the reason of the customer claim?

You can find below the reasons you may face when handling a logistic claim and an explanation of when to use each of them.

​


Lost In Transit

Lost in Transit is when the customer may get a notification of receiving the product, but they contact us informing that they didn't received. There is a miscommunication between both parties and we need to take the required actions depending on the situation.

​

Lost in Transit can be classified into:

  • Lost in transit: A tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier website. However, the latest update on the carrier website was over 10 working days ago (e.g. the website says that the parcel is in transit). A whole parcel is missing (not a part of a product).

    ​

  • No tracking possible: A tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier website. However, there has never been any update on the carrier website for over 10 working days (e.g. the website says that the order information has been transmitted).

    ​

    EXCEPTION FOR CH: For CH orders please consider a timeline of 20 days instead of 10 , to accommodate for Operational Delays ( Warehouse and Customs). No supplement should be created for Lost in Transit and No tracking Possible before the 20 days!

    ​

  • Lost or stolen: A tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier website. However, the tracking update states "delivered" but the customer didn't received the product or one of the items they should've (OBS: here we consider items under the same fulfillment and tracking number, meaning: items that should've arrived altogether).

​

What actions can you perform?

Identify if the parcel can be considered lost
or if somehow, as an example, it's going to be delivered to the customer in a later stage - such as an attempt of delivery that failed and they will try to reship the product - this should not be considered a claim, but just a clarification for the customer on what happened and the next steps. To identify the parcel status you need to check the carrier tracking link.


Documentation: Binding declaration & Proof of shipment

After you verified the carrier tracking link:

  • If the status is not moving for > than 5 business days: if there's no update on the delivery, please contact the carrier and ask about the order using this link. If carrier doesn't reply or status don't change in 10 business days, follow the same steps as if the order status was "delivered" in the tracking link.

  • If the order status is delivered: the next step will be to check the proof of shipment. Legally, carriers must deliver the product only for the buyer or authorized people to receive it, with a signature available upon delivery time. This document should be available to our Customer service teams. Where to find this document can vary depending on the market partner's website.

    • If no proof of shipment is available or the document has no signature, we must consider the claim of the customer valid and assume the shipment was incorrectly delivered.

      • As such, we must require a legally binding declaration signed by the customer - legally binding declaration (RVE Template)​

      • You also must attach the proof of shipment - a screenshot of the delivery status as per the reference by the end of this article (Parcel Perform screenshot).

If you need to manually add the documentation to the ticket and don't know how: review the article Sprinklr: How to handle tickets

Carrier verifies that the order is lost in transit
If the carrier confirms that the order is lost in transit, we do not require the customer to sign a binding document. In this case, simply take a screenshot of the delivery status, attach the confirmation of the carrier about the lost in-transit parcel, and proceed with the logistic claim creation and expected outcome.


​



Incorrect Shipment

Incorrect Shipment claims will apply only to the Wrong SKU Received, The received product does not match to what the customer ordered.

If something was delivered wrong, we need to make sure the wrong item is returned to our warehouse and the proper ordered one is reshipped as a supplement order (zero cost for the customer).

​

ATTENTION - Mandatory step

Any Logistic Claim due to DAMAGES (only) needs to be reported in a maximum of 5 business days to the carriers.

So, every time you receive a request for damage due to a logistic claim, you must immediately fill the ticket categorization in Sprinklr accordingly.

The claim doesn't have to be finalized in 5 days. We CANNOT wait for the resolution of the case to fill the ticket in Sprinklr. The ticket categorization needs to be informed right away even if you are still waiting for the customer to send more information.

Damages are claims from the customer regarding the quality of the package during the delivery time.

The damage will always be classified between:

Product & Box damaged - or - Box damaged (Product intact)

Products that were damaged during the delivery will need to be replaced by a new product under a logistic claim. If the box is intact and only the product inside is damaged, this means the issue was not due to the delivery experience, but its a product quality issue, it would fall under a product claim.

If only the box was damaged and the product remains intact, we guarantee the quality of the product and it should not be replaced or either the customer's compensation. For exceptions, please ensure you follow your market-related guidelines.

  • Package (only) damaged: Only the package of the parcel was damaged. The product was not affected (Plastic protection was not broken).

​

  • Product damaged: The package was damaged and the product inside was damaged due to the parcel damage. Alternatively, the product was damaged by the carrier during assembly.

For claim validation and product replacement, it is mandatory to attach both proof pictures of the damage to the claim to prove the damages by the carrier. Claims without proof cannot be validated by the country related and results in direct financial loss for the market.

Examples of valid pictures (box and product damaged)






Carrier Misbehaviour

Carrier Misbehavior claims will apply to:

  • Additional service not provided: The extra service was not provided by the carrier (dis-/assembly service, old mattress removal, room of choice delivery, did not show up to pickup/return appointment).

  • Carrier unprofessional behavior: The customer complains about rude or unprofessional behavior from the carrier which does not cause any monetary damage (e.g. confrontations or inappropriate interactions with customer).

  • Delivered to wrong address/wrong person: The product was initially delivered to a different person or different address but the customer received the product in the end.

  • Property damaged - financial cost: The carrier damages the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, resulting in monetary damage.

  • Property damaged - no cost: The carrier damaged the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, but the customer does not provide an invoice and does not suffer from monetary damage.

  • Returned without delivery attempt: Customer claims the carrier did not ring the bell or leave any note. No re-delivery attempt.

  • Should we compensate the customer for a delay after or before they receive the order?
    Always when the customer receives the order. Please don't mention %s, just calculate the full value with the OD calculator (as we can do regular updates on the minimum and maximum values).

  • What's the step-by-step process for providing compensation for an order delay?
    When an order is delayed, we should inform the customer of the new Updated Delivery Date (if applicable). If there is no UDD information (invalid date), we should warn them that we don't expect the order to arrive in less than 8 weeks. Always let them know that we offer compensation after 14 days of delay. If they are willing to wait and be compensated, ask them to alert us as soon as they receive the order so we can calculate the compensation. Please avoid mentioning specific compensation rates.

​

  • Should we roundup the compensation values (ex. if it's 61.77€, offer 65€):
    Only cents, for example: if it's 35.62, offer 36€. Ideally we should always offer the exact values that appear in the OD calculator.

  • What to do if acustomer ordered a product with assembly included and the carrier didn't assemble during the delivery?
    The correct process is to contact the carrier in order to schedule a new assemble. If that happens, we don't offer compensation. But, if the customer contact us explaining that the carrier did not assemble the product but he did, please offer a maximum value of 25 euros in compensation.

  • Can I provide the maximum of compensation for Order Delays whenever I believe the customer should have it?
    No. For Order Delays, taking into account the compensation percentages in each bucket and the maximum values, the compensation will always be the lowest value between the two. Example: if the compensation value is 45€, but the maximum value for the bucket in the file is 50€, we will still pay 45€.

  • Can the customer cancel the order due to a delay?
    Yes, but if they have received compensation for a delay, this must be deducted from the full refund.

  • Should we offer compensation for the entire order or on a per-item basis if only one item (SKU) is delayed?

    If the situation is not with the entire order and we have the price split per item in OMS, we should be able to compensate on an item specific level. One example is a customer that bought a bundle with a bed, mattress and pillow. If there's a delay with only the pillow, the agent needs to specify the SKU that requires compensation in the Order 2 Cash Jira portal in order for us to compensate the customer just for the pillow's delay.

  • What to do when the order/item value is low and therefore offering the minimum compensation is still a lot?

    On W26, 2024 we decided to lower the minimum values to address this situation. An example is a $30 order where the reward for scenario 2 is 5% ($1.5). Considering that our minimum payout is $5, we should still pay $5.

  • Do we have to transfer the tickets to the HLE queue?
    No, you shouldn't need to transfer it. As stated, we should provide compensation ONCE the order is delivered to the customer. We understand that the prior process was to proactively compensate customers on some countries. For complaints that happen before June 5th, if repeating WIMO for +3 times (3 wrong updated delivery dates) in the same order, it's possible to transfer to the HLE queue. Before transferring it, please ensure that the item is not phased out (if applicable to your country) and write an internal note explaining why the ticket was escalated to the HLE queue.

  • What if the customer doesn't accept the compensation values?
    The compensation values are final and if the customer don't agree he can always cancel the order and get a full refund. The only exception is if it's a regular CS case but it's a legal threat. If this is the case, please escalate the situation to your TL and If he agrees, please open the ticket explaining that it's a legal threat, then it's possible to use the HLE Compensation Matrix instead of the regular EU CS one.

  • If we have initiated a compensation and the customer wants to cancel their order, do we refund them the full amount or do we deduct the compensation value from the full price?
    We will deduct the compensation value from the full price.

  • Will HLE initiate the compensation or will they reassign the ticket to our queue with an approval?
    They will initiate the refund.

  • If the customer was promised an incorrect amount by a previous agent according to the previous policy, what do we do?
    Send proof in Jira that the date of the promised compensation is before May 25th, 2024.

  • If a wrong product was shipped and there is a price difference that should be refunded to the customer, what should we do?
    If a customer places an order for $1,000 and mistakenly receives a similar product that costs $800 and wants to keep the product, we will refund the difference between the two ($200) plus the compensation for the $800 product. If the customer doesn't want to keep the product, they can return it as normal.

When the carrier inappropriately or fails to comply with expected standards during the delivery or handling of the goods.

1. Types of Carrier Misbehaviour cases:

  1. Additional service not provided: The extra service was not provided by the carrier (dis-/assembly service, old mattress removal, room of choice delivery, did not show up to pickup/return appointment).

  2. Carrier unprofessional behavior: The customer complains about rude or unprofessional behavior from the carrier which does not cause any monetary damage (e.g. confrontations or inappropriate interactions with customer).

  3. Delivered to wrong address/wrong person but customer received the product: The product was initially delivered to a different person or different address but the customer received the product in the end.

  4. Property damaged - financial cost: The carrier damages the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, resulting in monetary damage.

  5. Property damaged - no cost: The carrier damaged the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, but the customer does not provide an invoice and does not suffer from monetary damage.

  6. Returned without delivery attempt: Customer claims the carrier did not ring the bell or leave any note. No re-delivery attempt. The carrier did not ring the bell or leave any note or there's no re-delivery attempt.

  1. Is the costumer claiming a cost/asking for a reattempt?

    1. If the answer is yes:

For property damage: the customer will need to sign an RVE (Legal Binding declaration) template, to provide a picture of the damage, a picture of the repair and an invoice for the repair costs. As soon as it's done, you'll need to open a ticket on the ARD2C Jira. Please leave a note on the case mentioning which service was not provided, when, why it wasn't provided, if the customer was contacted by the carrier and how the case was solved.

      3. For returned without delivery attempt: please review the Tracking information on Order Status Page or the Tracking website of the carrier. If the carrier failed to provide/provided inconsistent information for the customer and the item was Returned to Sender, proceed with supplementing the order.

Legally Binding Declaration (RVE Template):

​

  1. What is it? It is a signed statement from the customer that is used to officially confirm events such as: non-receipt of products, damaged or tampered packages or unauthorized charges or deliveries. Once signed, the person becomes responsible for the accuracy of the information and could face legal consequences if it's false.

  2. When to send the RVE to customer? When the customer claims the product has not been delivered and if the tracking doesn't clearly state that the parcel was not returned to the sender.

  3. How to send it? RVE forms can only be sent and received via email, and will need to be sent to and received from the same email address that is connected to the order in question on OMS. Each market will have a document in your customers' language to attach to the Logistic Claim, please find the RVE forms: here.

    1. Notes:

      1. BR market can be covered by the PT Template

      2. AU market can be covered by the EN Template
