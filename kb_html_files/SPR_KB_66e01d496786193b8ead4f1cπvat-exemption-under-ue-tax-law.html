Introduction


In this article, you will learn the correct procedure regarding VAT (Value Added Tax) exemption and how to deal with customer requests for exemption under the UE Tax Law exemptions.



VAT context


The customers that make a purchase on Emma's website have the option to input their VAT in the order for fiscal purposes. This is possible if is someone buying for their own use, or if the person represents a business and it's buying for the company.


According to the UE Tax Law, when purchasing from a company from a different country, people can ask for a VAT exemption and have the VAT cost refunded. BUT this cannot be done at Emma! Let's understand better the reason why.



VAT exemption conditions

  1. The VAT exemption can be done if the invoice given to customers is going with another country's VAT number than the one the customer is shipping the products to - again, not the case at Emma.

  2. This would only happen in case we allowed customers to buy on a country's website to deliver in a different country and that is not allowed on our checkout.

  3. Invoices are not being corrected automatically on OMS and workarounds for this are not compliant (not to mention the tax on the refund).


Guidelines for VAT exemption


-> Under NO circumstances we should accept VAT exemption at Emma under the UE Tax Law.


-> Emma only allows website sales to be delivered to addresses of the countries for the correspondent markets.


-> Once the VAT on the invoice will be on the same market, it makes it a non-intracommunity purchase.
