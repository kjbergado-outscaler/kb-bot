Return Deflection


Let's start with a question: Why do people send their products back?

It's normally a combination of wrong expectations and a lack of knowledge. And the first step to resolving this is to connect with the customer, make questions, and show that you care about their issue and that your goal is to find a solution with and for them.

The return process can be a difficult situation for the customer and also for the support team. On the one hand, it is in our best interest to prevent the return, and on the other hand, it is in the customer's best interest to return a product that they do not like. So, it is important to find the right balance in dealing with the customer. Acknowledge their frustration and show empathy, making them feel heard and understood, so they can be open-minded to your suggestions.



To understand better how you can make an impact to reduce the returns, please check the article "Return Deflection: Making Impact on Returns"


How to do a Return Deflection

-> Verbal Deflection



How to do it?

Explanations about how the product work, harmonization phase; muscular adaptation; the smell is harmful and disappears quickly.


Outcome:
Customer stays with the product (no money loss).


Example of Harmonization phase argument:

  • "The body needs about four weeks to get used to a new sleeping surface. Tension does not come and go immediately."
  • "It is quite normal that the mattress does not feel ideal in the first few weeks. Your body, especially the muscles, have adapted to a possible misalignment. Now the body needs a few weeks to compensate for this. As soon as this has happened, you will sleep wonderfully on your mattress. That's why you should take full advantage of our 100-night sleep trial.”
  • If the reasoning is too soft, the cores of foam mattresses can also be turned over. This does not affect the durability: ”You said your Emma Original was much too soft? Then open the cover, turn the core with the blue side up and you will get a much firmer lying comfort.”



The arguments can be applied to pillows, mattresses, box spring beds, and slatted frames. Please read the article "Deflection Arguments" to better understand the explanations you can use with customers.


Deflecting attacks


How to respond to an “attack”?


Below, we have an example of an unhappy and stressed customer, and after 4 tips to better deal with this situation:


Imagine the situation in which the customer says: “Your mattresses are the worst I have ever tried! This Emma One is so hard that it deserves its name: you can only spend one night on it! For me, not being able to test it in a store is the biggest problem.”


  • First step –>Praise the customer: "Thank you for taking the time to contact us and provide your honest feedback."

  • Second step ->Show interest in the customer problem: "I admit that it is difficult to find a mattress with the right firmness. Especially with the wide selection of mattresses offered".

  • Third step –>Address the deficiencies: "Unfortunately, trying it out in a store wouldn't have made it easier as well, as you would only be able to spend a few minutes with the mattress. And, as I think you will agree, testing it at home is way more comfortable."

  • Forth step –> Open the door for solutions: "You talked about your mattress being too firm. We have some options to solve this issue, so you can keep the mattress you bought and have a better night's sleep. If you would allow us, we would love to present you with some solutions."
