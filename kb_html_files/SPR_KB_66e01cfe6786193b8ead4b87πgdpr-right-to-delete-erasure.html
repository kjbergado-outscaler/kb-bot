What is the Right to Delete/Erasure?


The "right to delete" means that individuals can ask to have their personal data deleted from a company's records. For example, if someone requests that their account be deleted from a website, or if they ask for specific information about them to be removed from a database, they are exercising their right to delete.



Example:


Imagine Sarah, who registered in an online shopping website, decides she no longer wants her account with them. She contacts the company and requests that they delete all her personal information, including her purchase history and saved payment details, from their system.


In this case, you must forward the data subject request to privacy@emma-sleep.com.


Other examples of right to erasure/deletion requests you may receive:


"Please delete my account and all my information."

    • This is a request for erasure, another right under GDPR. They're asking us to get rid of all their personal data from our systems.


"I accidentally provided incorrect information when signing up. Please delete my account so I can create a new one with accurate details."

    • This request is from a user who wants their account deleted in order to start fresh with correct information.


"I no longer wish to be associated with your company and request that all my personal data be permanently removed."

    • This request is from a user who wants the deletion of all their personal data.


"I received an email from your company but have never signed up for your service. Please remove any information you have associated with my email address."

  • This request is from someone who received unsolicited communication from the company and wants their information removed from the company's records.


"I was notified of a data breach involving your company, and I'm concerned about the security of my personal information. Please delete my account and all associated data as a precaution."

  • This request is from a user who wants their data deleted due to concerns about data security following a reported breach.


How to handle requests for "Right to Delete/Erasure"?



Step 1: Forward the request to Legal & Compliance


If you receive the request via Sprinklr, chat, email, call or face-to-face please forward the request right away to:


privacy@emma-sleep.com

The Legal & Compliance team will reply to the DSR.




Step 2: Reply to the Customer using Canned Response


Suggested Response to Reply:


Dear (Customer's first name), Thank you for your inquiry regarding personal data. We have forwarded your request to our Data Protection team, and they will reply to you in due course. Thank you, Your Emma Team
