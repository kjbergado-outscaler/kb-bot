Warranty Definition

Most of our retail partners offer a 2-year statutory warranty, in accordance with German consumer protection
laws. This warranty covers defects that were present at the time of delivery.


After the initial 2-year period provided by the retailer, Emma GmbH takes over with its voluntary 10-year
warranty. This extended warranty covers issues related to material and manufacturing defects that may arise
after the statutory warranty has expired.


A warranty applies when a product experiences defects in materials or workmanship within the specified
warranty period highlighted below. It covers issues that arise under normal use and are not caused by
accidental damage, misuse, unauthorized repairs, or neglect. Customers must provide proof of purchase. If
the issue qualifies under warranty terms, the customer may be eligible for a repair, replacement, or refund as
outlined in this manual.


Important to note: The statutory warranty provided by the retailer is entirely separate from any return policies,
such as the 100-night trial or return periods based on customer preference (e.g., disliking the product). These
are commercial goodwill offers and not part of the legal warranty obligations.


Warranty periods for Emma vary by product type:
• Mattresses and Toppers: 10-year guarantee
• Pillows: 2-year guarantee
• Beds: 5-year guarantee
• Mattress Protectors and Bed Linen: Only statutory warranty period~


What does the guarantee cover?
Emma offers a warranty on dimensional stability and durability of the core materials.

Scope and Transfer of Warranty Responsibility
After the 2 year purchase period, Emma assumes full responsibility for the handling of warranty cases under
the manufacturer warranty.
This includes direct communication with the end customer as well as the evaluation and execution of
appropriate measures such as replacement, repair, or credit, in accordance with the applicable
warranty terms.
From this point on, direct contact between the customer and Emma is expected and required.

Standard Operating Procedure

Within the 2 years retailer warranty:
During the first two years after purchase, the retailer is the primary point of contact for all customer service
requests, as they are responsible for the statutory warranty under German law. Although Emma is not in
direct contact with the end customer during this period, retailers may escalate or forward certain requests to
Emma for support, clarification, or processing. The handling of these requests varies from retailer to retailer,
depending on individual contractual agreements. Refunds or returns may be offered by the retailer, and
collection of the product from the end customer may be organized either by Emma or the retailer, depending
on the specific logistics setup in place. Emma supports the retailer in the background, ensuring smooth
resolution while the retailer remains the direct point of contact for the customer.


After the 2-year statutory retailer warranty:
After the initial 2-year statutory warranty period provided by the retailer has ended, all warranty claims are
handled directly by Emma under our voluntary 10-year warranty. Customers should be directed to contact
Emma directly via email at emmagarantie@emma-sleep.com. Retailers are no longer involved in the handling
of these claims. Under the 10-year warranty, no refunds are offered. Emma will provide a replacement of the
same product or, if the original is no longer available, a comparable product from the same product group. All
communication, processing, and resolution will be managed by Emma's customer service team.

Documentation and Proof

How is proof of warranty ensured after 2 years?
- The customer must provide proof of purchase e.g., invoice and order number to proof the purchase date.


What proof is needed for Emma to process a claim after 2 years?
- After proving the purchase date, The customer will be asked to provide detailed pictures of the claim to make
sure the existing issue was not caused by accidental damage, misuse, unauthorized repairs, or neglect


Once a warranty request has been received :
• The Ema CS agents will verify the details of the warranty claim, including the validity based on the warranty
period.
• The agents will request photographic evidence from the customer.
• Assess the evidence and confirm if the product qualifies for a replacement.
• If approved, the agents will proceed with the replacement process internally.
• For collection and delivery, agents will follow the standard process that works for returns and supplement
orders.

Returns Handling

There are two types of returns based on the condition and size of the item:
• Packed items / Items that fit in a box: These are returned using a standard parcel carrier.
• Unpacked items / Bulky items: These require 2-man handling for collection due to their size or
condition.


If a replacement is offered under warranty, the original item is generally scheduled for pickup as part of the
process. However, whether pickup of the original item is always necessary is currently under internal
discussion and may vary depending on the case, item type, or operational considerations.


Clear instructions regarding the return method and pickup process must be communicated to the customer in
each case.


Packed Items:
• A return label should be issued to the customer, and the item should be returned via standard
carrier. As for DE this would apply to GLS
Unpacked items:
• If the item is opened, for hygiene reasons, we must send protective wrapping material to the
customer before scheduling a 2-man pickup, to ensure the item is properly prepared for transport.

What if we don’t offer the product anymore that the customer is claiming?
• Since we should avoid any form of refund, we should always send out a replacement
product. If we don’t have the same product anymore within our warehouse, we should
opt for a similar product within the same category.
