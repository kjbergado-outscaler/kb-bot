Logistic Claim: What

Logistic claims are complaints of the customers related to inconveniences experienced during the delivery/return of the products that they’ve ordered. Claims can lead to a cancellation, return, replacement, or even monetary compensation (which is a possible refund of a certain amount of the cost paid by the Customer, based on the country-specific Compensation matrix).

​​

Claim Reasons

Customers may reach us for multiple reasons, whether it be a carrier misbehavior, or due to an item never being received (as a lost item by the carrier, for example).

IMPORTANT: a delay in delivery should not be logged as a logistic Claim, but instead, under WIMO category.

When the customer may get a notification of receiving the product, but they contact us informing that they didn't received (a whole parcel is missing (not part of a product - that would be a product claim). There is a miscommunication between both parties and we need to take the required actions depending on the situation.

​

1. Types of Lost In Transit cases:

  1. Lost or stolen: A tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier website. However, the tracking update states "delivered" but the customer didn't received the product or one of the items they should've (OBS: here we consider items under the same fulfillment and tracking number, meaning: items that should've arrived altogether).

    ​

  2. Lost in transit: In that case, a tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier website. However, the latest update on the carrier website was over 10 days ago (e.g. the website says that the parcel is in transit).

    ​

  3. No tracking possible: A tracking number is available in the OMS and it works on the carrier. However, there has never been any update on the carrier website (e.g. the website says that the order information has been transmitted and status is like this for >10 days ago).

​

Market exception: For CH orders please consider a timeline of 20 days instead of 10 to accommodate special operational delays related to customs & warehouse expeditions in the country. No supplement should be created for Lost in Transit and No tracking Possible before the 20 days.

​

2. Actions to perform:

  1. Identify if the parcel can be considered lost: check the parcel status (and the time that it's stuck on it) in the carrier tracking portal or Order Status Page.

    1. Pay attention if there's any sort of observation related to the order, e.g: incorrect address, or an attempt of delivery that failed and they will try to reship the product.

      1. If so, this shouldn't be considered a claim. Just clarify with the customer what happened and the next steps.

        ​

  2. Check the proof of shipment/delivery if the customer didn't received the item and the tracking says "delivered": legally, carriers must deliver the product only for the buyer or authorized people to receive it, with a signature available upon delivery time. This document should be available to our Customer service teams. Where to find this document can vary depending on the market partner's website.

    1. If no proof of shipment is available, we must consider the claim of the customer valid and assume the shipment was incorrectly delivered. ​

    2. If the proof of shipment is available but it has no signature/signature is not clear, send what we have to the customer and check if they recognize it. If they do, it's not a logistic claim. If they don't, also assume that the shipment was incorrectly delivered. ​

      1. Actions: On incorrect deliveries, we require a legally binding declaration signed by the customer (RVE Template). You also must attach the proof of shipment - a screenshot of the delivery status as per the reference by the end of this article (Parcel Perform screenshot). If you need to manually add the documentation to the ticket and don't know how: review the article Sprinklr: How to handle tickets. ​

    ​

  3. Please contact the carrier if the parcel is stuck in the same status for >10 days. Ask about the order using this link.

    1. If carrier replies with a delivery date, inform costumer accordingly (once the order arrives, customer may be eligible for compensation - check the rules).

    2. If it doesn't reply or status don't change in 2 business days, please follow the same process of the items that have the status "delivered" but never arrived.

      ​

  4. If the item is considered lost: please check with the customer if they would like us to re-ship the item by creating a supplement (check here). The other option would be to cancel and to do a full refund. For this, please refer to the following table:

  • If the customer does not want to keep the item → process a return with refund.

If it’s a different SKU → the wrong product was shipped, order was shipped incorrectly.​

            • If there is a similar SKU, but stock is not available → return with refund

Logistic Claims related to the quality of the package during the delivery time.

​

1. Types of Damages:

  1. Product & Box damaged/Product damaged during assembly: when the box/package is damaged and the product inside as well, it means that the damage happened during the delivery and the item will need to be replaced by a new product. If the product was damaged during the assembly, the process is the same.

    ​

  2. Package damaged, product intact: if only the box is damaged, we guarantee the quality of the product and it should not be replaced and customer should not be compensated either. For exceptions, please ensure you follow your market-related guidelines.

    ​

OBS: If the box is intact but the product inside is damaged, we consider it a quality issue, which falls under product claims.

​

2. Actions to perform:

  1. Please refer to the article [Online: D2C: EU] Product & Logistic Claims : Damage & Incomplete item(s).

When the carrier inappropriately or fails to comply with expected standards during the delivery or handling of the goods.

1. Types of Carrier Misbehaviour cases:

  1. Additional service not provided: The extra service was not provided by the carrier (dis-/assembly service, old mattress removal, room of choice delivery, did not show up to pickup/return appointment).

  2. Carrier unprofessional behavior: The customer complains about rude or unprofessional behavior from the carrier which does not cause any monetary damage (e.g. confrontations or inappropriate interactions with customer).

  3. Delivered to wrong address/wrong person but customer received the product: The product was initially delivered to a different person or different address but the customer received the product in the end.

  4. Property damaged - financial cost: The carrier damages the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, resulting in monetary damage.

  5. Property damaged - no cost: The carrier damaged the customer's property during the delivery or collection of products, but the customer does not provide an invoice and does not suffer from monetary damage.

  6. Returned without delivery attempt: Customer claims the carrier did not ring the bell or leave any note. No re-delivery attempt. The carrier did not ring the bell or leave any note or there's no re-delivery attempt.

2. Actions to perform:

  1. Is the costumer claiming a cost/asking for a reattempt?

    1. If the answer is yes:

      1. For property damage: the customer will need to sign an RVE (Legal Binding declaration) template, to provide a picture of the damage, a picture of the repair and an invoice for the repair costs. Also, when filling the claim in the Guided Workshow in Sprinklr, you'll also be asked to input a screenshot from the carrier website of current status. As soon as it's done, you'll need to open a ticket on the ARD2C Jira. If the repair <100€ (invoice), supervisor can approve, for repair >100€, please Emma internals to get approval before promissing payment to the customer. Please leave notes of the case.

      2. For additional service not provided: If the service was expected to be provided (e.g., requested by the customer or advertised by Emma), please contact the carrier to ensure its fulfillment. If that happens, we don't offer compensation. But, if the customer contact us explaining that the carrier did not assemble the product but he did, please offer a maximum value of 25 euros in compensation by filling a ticket to the ARD2C team. Please leave a note on the case mentioning which service was not provided, when, why it wasn't provided, if the customer was contacted by the carrier and how the case was solved.

      3. For returned without delivery attempt: please review the Tracking information on Order Status Page or the Tracking website of the carrier. If the carrier failed to provide/provided inconsistent information for the customer and the item was Returned to Sender, proceed with supplementing the order.

      ​

    2. If the answer is no: it generally means that the customer is upset by the carrier standards but there's no damage in the product. In that case, we don't offer compensation, but you should still create a logistic claim for incident report and leave notes on Sprinklr (what happened, when, where, specify in which way did the carrier behave unprofessionally)

      1. Please apologize the customer for the unmet expectations and thank them for bringing the topic to our attention. Inform them that we will leave the records of the claims in our system and that our logistic team will take this up and improve the services.

Documents and templates

Legally Binding Declaration (RVE Template):

​

  1. What is it? It is a signed statement from the customer that is used to officially confirm events such as: non-receipt of products, damaged or tampered packages or unauthorized charges or deliveries. Once signed, the person becomes responsible for the accuracy of the information and could face legal consequences if it's false.

  2. When to send the RVE to customer? When the customer claims the product has not been delivered and if the tracking doesn't clearly state that the parcel was not returned to the sender.

  3. How to send it? RVE forms can only be sent and received via email, and will need to be sent to and received from the same email address that is connected to the order in question on OMS. Each market will have a document in your customers' language to attach to the Logistic Claim, please find the RVE forms: here.

    1. Notes:

      1. BR market can be covered by the PT Template

      2. AU market can be covered by the EN Template

      3. MX; CL and CO markets can be covered by the spanish Template
