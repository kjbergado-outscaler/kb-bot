Introduction


About 80% of all Emma orders are delivered on time. We globally dispatch various trucks loaded with products every day. The remaining 20% of orders might incur delays due to various reasons, such as production or logistic capacity, unforeseen events impacting infrastructure or resource availability, and system issues.


However, the delays are the top driver reasons for the most negative experience for our customers.


Due to that, this article supports you in identifying what is happening with the customer order, what to do next, and how to correctly and empathetically reply to our customers in case they reach out to you asking:



"Where Is My Order?"




Why do customers contact us?


In WIMO inquiries, we should always understand if it is a Happy or Unhappy path scenario, both we will see in detail further down in this article. The first step is to identify the contact reason, which will determine the actions and processes that you will have to follow.

So, the customers can reach out to us asking "Where is my order?" in 2 different scenarios:

1) Everything is fine with the order, delivery is on time & customer needs additional information -> Happy Path.


Order is on time, and everything flows as expected, but somehow customer can not follow the order path or just need a confirmation of the delivery date. Here, the customer experience is crucial as they need our support for guidance and indication of the next steps, since there is a lack of visibility from customer side about the order.




2) There is an issue with the order & customer wants information and a solution -> Unhappy Path.


When something went wrong with the order, and a resolution is needed. In this scenario, the service is out of our standards and caused the order to be delayed. contacts The customer has no visibility of what will happen next, and contacts us looking for clarification and a proper solution.



Understanding the journey of an item


Once completing the purchase on our website, we want to ensure all communication is properly set and the customer has a seamless experience with us, with the right expectations set and the item arriving under the promised delivery time. Ideally, with all this in place, the customer should not need to contact our customer support.

The Happy Path journey


Under a Happy Path, even if the order is flowing properly in our supply chain, we may receive contact to have a better visibility of some stage that somehow customer is lacking. For example, the customer had an email issue or the tracking link was not working properly.



The Unhappy Path journey


Although, for an Unhappy Path, we have different phases of the process we can face an unexpected challenge, that could lead to a delayed order, and consequently a possible customer inquiry. In the image below, the blue dots highlight the moment of the journey in which those problems can happen.





Issues between steps 1 and 2 (between order submitted and order confirmed), we may face some scenarios such as:


  • Order Stuck: Problems with the transmission of the order like address errors, payment issues or system bugs.

  • Order Out of Stock: The item Item is out of stock and the order has yet not fulfilled. Or is delayed, but already fulfilled



Issues between steps 2 and 3: Once in the hands of warehouse partners, we can face an unexpected delay by their side, preventing the order from being dispatched under the expected time.


  • Warehouse delays: Order dispatched with delay by the warehouse.


Issues between steps 4 and 5: Some occurrences during the delivery of the order, once in the hands of the last mile company, may also influence the delivery time; such as a logistic claim, or an unable-to-deliver scenario (no answer from the customer, or wrong address provided).


  • Last mile delays: These happen with unable-to-delivery scenarios or due to Logistic Claims (parcel lost, partial delivery, etc).



Identifying the Order Status


Now it's the moment to identify if the order and respective items are under expected delivery time and will be delivered on time, or if there is an issue going on and the order will be delayed.



How to identify the order status?


Access the related Order Management System (Fluent Commerce or Magento) and:


  • Identify the promised delivery date
  • Check order status
  • Check all items (fulfillment) status
  • If already shipped, check the delivery tracking link -> if no tracking link, access the delivery partner portal and check the delivery status

Currently, in certain cases in Magento is possible to see updated messages regarding the delivery date. When these messages appear, it's an indication that the delivery was delayed, the system identified and corrected to the new dates. Also, the customer is informed about it via e-mail. This is how it will look in the system:




How to read the Delivery Sign:


1. "Previous updated delivery date -> This means what the customer has been informed about from outside, so you have a history of automated communication related to delivery time updates.

2. "Promised Delivery Date" -> This was the date shown to the customer when they ordered. If today is still within this range, it means the order is not delayed!

3. "Updated Delivery Date" -> This means a new delivery date due to delays. This information is visible to the customer.






Tracking link issues


When investigating where is the order of the customer, you should always access the order-related carrier portal to track the order. In case there was any issue during the delivery, such as the customer being unreachable during the delivery, or a delay during the dispatch, you will be able to find this information on the carrier portal and properly manage the customer expectations.


You can find the tracking link under the Order details, purchase orders section in the Order Management tool:





You can find the carrier portal knowledge article HERE.



When checking the status, please take into consideration the time-frame for the delivery to be completed:


  • If by the moment the customer contacts us, the delivery date to less than 1 week: Order is on its way!
  • If by the moment the customer contacts us, the delivery date for more than 10 days: Order can be delayed

> 1 Week for delivery: reach out to the delivery company and ask for further information.



Website Self-Service: Order Status Bar



The order status bar is a customer-facing section on the website, that displays the status of each barcode within a given order number.


The customer can access the status bar by clicking on the link on the homepage (https://www.emma-matratze.de/bestellstatus/) and inserting the order number and associated email address


The information contains


  • Order status
  • Barcode status
  • Parcel Perform status
  • WIMO (where is my order) Dates, also called "updated delivery dates"

How to do the WIMO Freshdesk Ticket categorization?


One important step regarding WIMO tickets is to correctly categorize it in Freshdesk, so we can keep data updated and accurate regarding delivery issues.


Customer Inquiries for Where is My Order? can be listed as:

  1. Categorization: WIMO -> Delivery Still on Time
  2. Categorization: WIMO -> Delivery Delay



Freshdesk Ticket Accuracy
Ensure you always provide the related Ticket type & subtype based on the customer request, according to what is indicated in the article "Freshdesk - Ticket categorization list"



Make sure to consider the Promised delivery date, if one business day has passed since the promised date we should use the categorization "WIMO -> Delivery Delay".
