Introduction

In our effort to enhance the customer experience, we aim to ensure that our communications with customers are as effective as possible.

Topics that are First Contact Resolution (FCR) oriented should be addressed and resolved as completely as possible to minimize the need for customers to engage with customer support beyond what is necessary. Agents should always strive to close cases in the first interaction whenever possible.

Below, you will find a set of guidelines designed to help ensure effective First Contact Resolution:

Note: Only applicable for the markets: UK; PT; ES

FCR Guidelines

Active Reading & Understanding: Carefully read and acknowledge the customer’s concern. Ensure the resolution fully addresses the issue instead of providing a generic response.

Clear & Concise Communication: Avoid vague responses. Provide a clear, step-by-step explanation or resolution. If needed, include screenshots, links, or additional instructions.

Proactive Assistance: Anticipate possible follow-up questions and address them in the initial response. Offer guidance on next steps, expected resolution times, or alternative solutions.

Consistency in Information: Ensure all details align with internal guidelines, policies, and previous communications to avoid conflicting responses.

Status of Ticket: Use "Internal Hold" when awaiting confirmation from a third party (e.g., carrier, refunds team, OM, or Performance Manager). Otherwise, cases should be marked as "Pending Confirmation Resolution" after the first reply.

​

Non-FCR Guidelines

Information to Include in the First Reply: Even if a case cannot be closed immediately, the first response should be as complete as possible. Follow the FCR guidelines to provide clear, relevant, and complete information, reducing unnecessary back-and-forth.

Awaiting Response: Only use this when additional information is required from the customer (e.g., bank details, damage pictures, address confirmation). Do not use it simply to wait for the customer to confirm resolution—if all necessary support has been provided, set the case as "Pending Confirmation Resolution."

Internal Hold: Use this when awaiting confirmation from a third party (e.g., carrier, refunds team, OM, or Performance Manager).

​

Follow-Up & Resolution Timeframes

Follow Up if Needed: If an issue requires additional steps (e.g., waiting for confirmation from another department), it should be tagged as Internal Hold.

Follow-ups Every 3 Days: Cases in Internal Hold should be checked and updated at least every three days.

Escalation After 7 Days: If no resolution is reached within a week, escalate the case to the Performance Manager.

​

Best Practices

  • Use Standardized Templates, but Personalize: Stick to structured responses to maintain consistency, but ensure each reply is personalized to the customer’s specific issue.

  • Check Customer History: Before responding, check if the customer has previously contacted us about the same issue to avoid redundant or conflicting resolutions.

  • Avoid Unnecessary Transfers or Escalations: Ensure agents have the necessary tools and knowledge to resolve as many cases as possible without escalating.

  • Train Agents on Product & Process Knowledge: Regular training sessions should be conducted to improve efficiency.

  • Use Categories Correctly: Properly categorize tickets to track trends and identify areas for improvement.

  • Weekly Follow-ups: A weekly report with all cases in Internal Hold should be shared with BPO and the Performance Manager.
