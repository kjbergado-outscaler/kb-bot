Springs



When talking about springs is important to consider that: The higher the spring travel, the more adaptable a spring is!



Bonell Spring



For Bonell spring cores, the more rounds a spring core makes, the higher its quality. The correct term for this is gears.


Bonell springs in the base of Emma Boxspring Beds can be very useful, especially if you want a low-priced but long-lasting product. Although, when choosing a mattress, due to the lack of point elasticity of Bonell Springs, it is more advisable to go for the pocket spring core mattress. All Emma Mattresses with springs use pocket springs.

Pocket Springs


The pocket spring core belongs to the genus of barrel springs and is characterized by its double conical shape. That is, the spring core is slightly wider toward the center and narrows slightly toward the top and bottom. The barrel spring core is not completely cylindrical.


This gives it more elasticity. Because each spring moves individually and is connected only by small pockets, pocket spring cores are considered more point-elastic and adaptable than Bonell spring core mattresses.


In addition to the height, the number of springs, and the quality of the springs used are also considered a burr gauge of quality. However, recent studies and tests by Stiftung Warentest show that above 250 springs per square meter there is no noticeable improvement in the lying properties. The materials with which the spring core is padded are more decisive here.


Basically, all innerspring are considered to be particularly well ventilated, since more air can circulate within the mattress. Compared to traditional foams, they are the better choice for people who sweat a lot.


However, since innerspring is made of steel, the mattresses are on average 20 to 25% heavier than an identical mattress made of foam. This is where you need to weigh things up when making a purchase.
