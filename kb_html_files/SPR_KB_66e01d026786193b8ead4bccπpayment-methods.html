Introduction

In this article we will discuss the different payment methods we have in the Swiss market. The customer sees the options when they check out:



Which payment methods do we have in Switzerland?


Creditcard

  • How does it work?
    Put simply, a credit card is a type of loan: the bank or credit card issuer extends the customer a line of credit — money that they otherwise would not have. In exchange, the customer pays them back by the monthly payment due date.

    Customers can pay conveniently and securely by credit card. Their credit card details are encrypted and transmitted using the secure SSL method. We accept Visa and MasterCard.


PayPal

  • How does it work?
    PayPal works as an intermediary between the customer and a bank. A user adds a bank account or credit/debit card to the PayPal system and whenever they are making an online payment, they can select which account to debit. All transactions are processed through PayPal, instead of the user's bank. If the customer chooses this payment method, they will automatically be redirected to Paypal.


Instant bank transfer (SOFORT-Überweisung)

  • How does it work?

    SOFORT is an online real-time bank transfer initiation service from customer to merchant. It is the predominant online banking method in the German-speaking countries of Europe, and also in Belgium. At the checkout,

    customers are redirected to the online banking environment where payment details and beneficiary are pre-filled. The customer completes the bank transfer and uses the authentication procedure enforced by the bank. The purchase amount is instantly taken away from the account and settled to the merchant within one working day.


Installment purchase (Unzer)

  • How does it work?
    If the customer selects the installment option in the shopping basket, they can specify a monthly installment. Depending on the invoice amount, installments of 3, 6, 9, 12, 18 or 24 months can be selected. First, their creditworthiness will be checked by Unzer GmbH (we cannot influence this check). Then the customer can complete the order in installments. They will find more information about the due date of the instalments in the contract documents of the instalment purchase. The installment payment starts immediately, not only after the order has been shipped.

  • See here the article how to handle the Unzer portal.


Invoice (Unzer)

  • How does it work?
    If the customer choose the payment invoice, their creditworthiness will be checked by Unzer GmbH (we cannot influence this check). They will then receive an invoice by e-mail, when their product has been shipped. The invoice amount must be paid within 14 days of the invoice date. They shouldn't pay until they have received the separate invoice. They should transfer the purchase price directly to the account stated on the invoice and not to our Emma bank account.

  • See here the article how to handle the Unzer portal.


Payment in advance

  • How does it work?
    With this payment method (bank transfer), we ask the customer to transfer the total amount to our account, stating the payment reference. They will receive our account details and the payment reference with the order confirmation. As soon as we have received the payment, the order will be activated and the announced delivery time will start.
