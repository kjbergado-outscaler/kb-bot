Improve Quality

In order to increase the quality of conversations and to provide an Exceptional Customer Experience, we standardize the needs assessment for all pre-sales inquiries.

The aim is to improve the needs analysis, increase empathy and find a customer-oriented solution faster. This will both increase the number of items in the basket and increase the average receipt. This article is in line with the new Quality Grid, which takes effect on 06.03.2023.

Pre-sales conversation

1. Introduction sentence

Hello, my name is Aurora, from Emma – The sleep Company, and I am your todays sleep expert.

Who am I talking to?

Alternative:

Hello, my name is Aurora, from Emma -The sleep Company. I am your personal sleep advisor today!

Who am I talking to?

Small break before speaking out your name and the company name.

Start below your normal speaking tempo. Speak clear and distinctly.

Close your sentence always with a question to keep control over the conversation.
Keep using customer's name in your speech. A person likes nothing better than to hear his own name and immediately reacts to it

​

2.Need analysis

​

Mandatory questions:

​

  • What is your preferred sleeping position (side, back, stomach, or combination)?

  • Do you often feel too warm when you sleep?

Optional:

  • What features are most important to you in a mattress?

  • When sleeping, do you prefer a soft or firm mattress?-

  • In terms of comfort, do you give more importance to support and pressure relief, or the slight
    feeling of sinking?

What you want to know: Sinking behavior in shoulder and pelvis, desire for support, desire for pressure relief. Need for breathability. Does the customer have backpain.

​

Alternative

​

“In order to provide you with the optimal sleeping experience, I recommend you the Emma Original hybrid to solve your problem with xyz.” 

Choose max. 2 product features that fit the previously identified needs of the customer. Always argue in sales terms. Product feature - product benefit - customer advantage.

Return deflection

The approach for the return defence builds on the argumentation in the pre-sales area.

The approach is built on making the customer think about positive things as quickly as possible. Here, too, the right questioning technique is crucial for the course of the conversation, because through correct framing we can influence the conversation in our direction.


From a psychological point of view, it matters a lot whether we ask: "What didn't you like about the old product?" Or "What would the product have to be like for you to like it?".



Continuation Return request


“I want to give back my new mattress”

“I'm sorry to hear you're not happy with your product yet. What would your mattress look like in an ideal world for you?”

Discover the needs before checking the personal data!

Ask for the data – while you check the data you place in the end the follow up question

"By the way, what was it about the product you already ordered that convinced you to place the order?"

Discover the diffrences between the customer´s dream product and the customer´s current product.

Just assuming we can change the mattress so that it is no longer too firm, fits your needs and you can sleep well on it, would you continue sleeping on your mattress?


Yes -> Deflect it!
