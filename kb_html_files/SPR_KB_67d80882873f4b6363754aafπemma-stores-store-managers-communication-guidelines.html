In-Store

Customer contacts stores via email or in-person

​

Important note for Store Managers

  • Emails are mandatory for store managers to initiate a case resolution, but the post on the Teams channel is optional. If there's a new contact from a customer that had already an ongoing ticket, the store manager should use the same email thread and/or same Teams channel post to follow up with the CS agents.

  • Avoid as much as possible contacting CS agents via phone (only urgent/critical situation in the store). ​

​

​Note: Phone lines are only open for Iberian store managers and phone numbers should never be shared directly with the customers.

Escalations via Teams Channels

After sending the email to our customer support team (mandatory to send the email), you can also post on the CS Teams channel the information about your ticket and, in that way, receive a faster answer/support by our CS team. ​

  • If a customer goes back to the store with the same inquiry/question/support request, always use the same email thread or channel post to follow up with the agents. That way, it will be easier for them to pick up the case and understand its history. ​

How to better manage customer expectations about CS?

  1. After you finalize the sale & before the customer leaves the store: always share with them the CS email contact for your respective country, and mention that if they have any questions or need any support during the delivery or post-sale experience, they can reach out to that specific email address. ​

  2. Additionally, reinforce the message that Emma stores have a different/exclusive customer support structure than e-commerce, so they should not be reaching to the contacts on the Emma website (phone or email, or returns portal). ​

  3. Customers will also have access to the store's customer support email on their order confirmation email (from shopify) and on the order status portal. You can proactively mention this to the customer. ​
