1. Introduction

This article is meant for the agents working in the Online Reputation team. It summarizes why we use Yotpo for Product Reviews and how we answer the customer queries via Sprinklr.

​

2. What are Yotpo reviews and why are they so important?

​

Yotpo is a digital platform that allows us to collect product reviews and display them in our website directly.

​

Showcasing product reviews in our own websites will help us grow, as it increases customer trust and confidence on the product. Yotpo templates will increase the number of reviews we collect, they allow customers to upload products' pictures or videos and to better engage with customers as they can leave a review directly in the invitation email.

Once a customer replies and leaves a review, it falls on Emma to reply to it and decide to whether publish it or not. As mentioned above, all published reviews will be showcased on our own website, providing a scoring specific for each product.

​

How can you positively impact customer's experience and improve Emma's Online Reputation?

By replying to negative Yotpo reviews, providing help and addressing customers' potential needs.

3. How to process Yotpo cases

We use Yotpo to display product reviews on our websites.

​

What you can do with a review is either publish it or reject it:

​

Publish → the review will be displayed publicly on our websites

Reject publishing → the review won’t be displayed publicly on our websites

​

3.1 Summary - Golden Principles

​

  1. Default to publish when the review contains any genuine product opinion and no disallowed content or service complaints alone.

  2. Reject if it contains disallowed content or service complaints only (see Publish vs Reject)

  3. Reply choice follows moderation choice:

    • If Published → Public reply only

    • If Rejected → Private reply only

  4. No personal information in replies and never ask for order details publicly.

​

4. Publish vs Reject

Click to understand when to publish and when to reject

​

When to reject a review?

​

Reject

A review has to be rejected when it contains one or more of the following:

​

  • Legal transgression: The review contains information that cannot legally be published, for example, a person’s financial, medical, or personal file information or a company’s trade secrets.

  • Profanity / Inappropriate: The review contains libelous, harassing, abusive, vulgar, obscene, or sexually explicit language, and/or the review is inappropriate concerning race, gender, sexuality, ethnicity, or other intrinsic characteristics.

  • Private information: The review contains confidential or private information.

  • Off topic, service only content: the review is completely unrelated to the product and only mentions experience with the service, such as delivery, customer support and/or returns.

  • False or misleading: the review makes favorable performance claims that are clearly false or misleading and that you could not make yourself.

  • Fake: You have actual proof allowing you to say with a reasonable degree of certainty that the review is fake and not written by a real customer.

​

When to publish a review?

​

Publish

All reviews that include the customer’s honest opinion of the product (e.g. “I don’t like it”, “Mattress was too firm”, “The mattress didn’t help with my backpain”) must be published.

​

Important note

​

Remeber: you can only reject a review if there is a valid reject reason as per the list above.

​7. Step-by-Step Processing Instructions

​

1. Review the Customer Submission

Scroll to the bottom of the ticket to read the customer review content.

2. Decide: Publish or Reject

Copy the complete review template into your reply. Use the guidelines in Step 1 and Step 5 to determine whether the review should be published or rejected.

Publish if the review:

  1. Meets policy guidelines

  2. Contains a clear product opinion

​

Reject if the review contains:

  1. Personally identifiable information (PII)

  2. Profanity, hate speech

  3. Spam or irrelevant/off-topic content

  4. Service-only content (no product reference)

  5. Illegal or abusive content

3. Log the Decision

In Text Field 3, set “Publish the review: Yes/No” based on your decision:

a.      Yes = Review will be published

b.      No = Review will be rejected

4. Choose the Correct Reply Format

a.      For published reviews:

 Use Text Section 2: “Write below this line to comment in public.”
 Leave Text Section 1 blank.

b.      For rejected reviews:
 Use Text Section 1: “Write below this line to comment in private.”
 Leave Text Section 2 blank.

5. Important Reminder

a.      ✅ If Published → Use Public reply only

b.      ❌ If Rejected → Use Private reply only

​

[only from September 2025 onward]

10. Special customer requests (GDPR)

​

What to do if a customer asks to stop receiving Yotpo invites? Or what if the customer asks to have his data deleted from Emma and/or Yotpo?

10.1. Unsubscription requests

Yotpo allows customers to unsubscribe directly on review invites, however, it is not connected to our other databases.

Important:

So, if you as an agent ever face this type of request, you should send an email to privacy@emma-sleep.com identifying the request.

10.2. Data Deletion requests

Important:

If you as an agent ever face this type of request, you should reply by saying something similar to the example below and should send an email to privacy@emma-sleep.com identifying the request.

​

Example:
“We are sorry to hear you no longer wish to receive Review invites from Emma.
We will comply with your request and delete all data within 3 working days.”
