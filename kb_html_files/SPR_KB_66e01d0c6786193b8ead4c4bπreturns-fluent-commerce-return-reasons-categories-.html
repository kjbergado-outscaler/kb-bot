Introduction

When handling a return request is crucial to correctly identify correctly the condition and return reason. This will allow Emma to continuously act upon this feedback and improve the quality of our customer experience.

Return Reasons

Fluent Commerce counts 14 distinct return reasons, divided into five macro groups:

Claims

Returns linked to Logistic/Product claims

Customer ordered incorrectly

Whenever the customer states that they’ve had some doubts about which product to select, as well as:

  • For customer ordering mistakes;

  • Whenever the product information & description on the website did not match the product received (e.g. customer is misinformed, Emma needs to improve on customer education)

How to probe: “Was it difficult for you to select which product to buy?” - Customer will typically say Yes

Product unmet expectations

Whenever the customer states that they were super clear on which product to select, but are unhappy with some of the product characteristics

How to probe: “Was it easy to decide which product to buy?” > Customer will typically say Yes

Refused at door/Return to Sender (RTS)

These are returns refused at the door by customers;

This typically happens, for example, in Late Late Cancellation scenarios – when the order is already being delivered and it is too late to cancel

Other Reason

Usage of this reason should be kept to a minimum (<5%), and should almost never be used.

Comments are required and should contain a brief description (max 10 words) of the return reason

Condition

Packed – Never Used: For items that didn't have their packaging open after being received by the customer. Those can be sent back to the warehouse and resold since they were never used.

Packed – Used/Re-Packed: For items that had their packaging open after being received by the customer, but were or will be re-packed by them before the pick-up. Once collected, those items can be refurbished or sent to sent to charity.

Unpacked: For items that had their packaging open after being received by the customer. Once collected, those items can be refurbished or sent to sent to charity.
