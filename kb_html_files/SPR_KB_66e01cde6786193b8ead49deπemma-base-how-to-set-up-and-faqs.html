How to set up your Emma Base


Step1:

Place the box of the bed base in the desired place with the upside of the box facing up. Remove the straps and packaging materials, and ensure that the box is not damaged by sharp objects.


Step 2:

Take the Emma MOTION Base from the box and put the bottom side up.

Step 3:

Plug the input power cable that is connected to the control box into the mains adapter.

Step 4:

On the opposite side of the mains adapter are further possibilities for connecting the mains cable. Connect it with the mains adapter.



Step 5:

Now turn the Emma MOTION Base around carefully with the help of a second person. When doing so, do not pull it across the floor or place it on its side.




Step 6:

Now plug the mains cable into a power source. An overvoltage protection device is recommended.


Step 7:

Now test the function of the enclosed remote control and move the Emma MOTION Base into a flat position before you place your mattress on top of it.


Step 8:

Lastly, insert the mattress support rod before you place the mattress on top of it.



How to set up the Emma remote control


In a single The Emma Motion Base:

When purchasing a single base bed, the remote control that is included in the box as part of the delivery is already paired with the bed frame. If this should not be the case, please perform the following steps:


Step 1:

Disconnect the main cables from the power source.


Step 2:

Wait for one minute and reconnect the mains cable with the power source.


Step 3:

While the LED is lit on the control box, keep the PAIR button on the back of the remote pressed. The PAIR button will start to blink.




Step 4:

When the LED goes out on the control box, release the PAIR button on the remote control.


Step 5:

The remote control is now paired with the Emma Motion Base.



In a double The Emma Motion Base:

When purchasing 2 Emma Motion Bases for your double bed, the delivery includes a SmartSync™ cable. It ensures that your Emma Motion Bases always move in synch. To pair the remote control with your two Emma Motion Bases, perform the following steps:


Step 1:

Disconnect both main cables from the power source.


Step 2:

Connect the Smart-Sync™ cable with the multifunctional port of each control box.


Step 3:

Plug both main cables back into the power source.


Step 4:

Both remote controls now power both Emma Motion Bases at the same time.



Note: If your two Emma Motion Bases were not in the same position at the time of the pairing, you can resynchronise them by pressing the FLAT button on the remote control.

How to connect with the Sleep Assist App


The remote control is only limited to controlling the bed base, but the app gives you many more functionalities. Let's check how to connect and use the Sleep Assist App.


Step 1:

Download the Sleep Assist App from the Apple or Google Play Store.




Step 2:

Open the app and register with your login details if you have not created a user account yet.


Step 3:

When you have created your user account, you can scan the QR code of your bed. You can find it on the Bluetooth box on the bottom side of your Emma Motion Base.





Step 4:

Alternatively, you can also choose to select your Base ID in the app from a list of available Emma Motion Bases near you.




FAQs


Q. Does the Emma Motion Base fits all Emma beds?


Yes, it fits the Emma beds, with the exception of the Emma Metal Bed.



Q. Is it compatible with other mattresses or do I need to have one of the Emma Motion mattresses?


The Motion Base is compatible with all types of mattress (foam, spring, air chambers) that is not higher than 30cm. The sensors on the bed will be able to detect the movement no matter the type of mattress you have, but it cannot be height. Also, you don't need to have the Motion Shift or Emma Sense, but using one of them with the Motion Base boosts the technology developed, of course.



Q. what is the maximum weight the Motion Base can support?


The whole system supports up to 345kg (mattress included), and the motor on the feet area can lift up to 120kg.



Q. How can I operate my Emma MOTION Base in the event of a power outage?


Step 1: Disconnect the input power cable from the mains adapter.

Step 2: Connect the battery clip to the two 9-volt batteries. Use only batteries of the same manufacturer.

Step 3: Connect the end of the battery clip to the input power cable, which is plugged into the control box.


Q. What are the factors comprising my sleep score?


The sleep score consists of different parameters, e.g. length of time sleeping, sleep phases, periods of snoring, heart rate and breathing rate, and the number of movements during the sleep. These data are analyzed by means of an algorithm and the result represents the sleep score.


Q. My sleep score is below 80, what does that mean and how can I improve it?

A permanently low sleep score can have different causes. To rule out medical causes for bad sleep, you should consult a doctor. Your mattress can also have a significant effect on the quality of your sleep. For better blood circulation and quicker recovery, we recommend the Emma MOTION Sense in combination with your MOTION Base. The mattress adjusts each zone by means of an algorithm to the optimal pressure relief and adjusts to your sleeping pattern in real-time.


Q. I sleep about 6 hours per night, does this really have negative effects on my health?

It depends. If you sleep 6 hours because you went to bed late and your alarm clock wakes you early to go to work or because you feel well rested after 6 hours and wake up by yourself? Sleep depends on the individual. Most people need between 7 and 9 hours of sleep to regenerate optimally (about 95%). But there are also people who need more or less sleep. Although it is unlikely that you are among the 2% of people, who simply do not need a lot of sleep, it is still possible. If you actually need more than 6 hours of sleep, it is important how often you only get 6 hours of sleep. Your body can easily handle this occasionally and it compensates for the missing sleep the next night with a particularly relaxing deep sleep. However, if you sleep less than you need over a prolonged period, this will in fact have negative effects on your health. Problems concentrating, headaches, mood swings or depression are just some of the possible consequences. In that case, it is important to either prioritize and go to bed earlier or set the alarm clock to a later time or consult a doctor if the problem cannot be resolved this way.


Q. Last night my heart rate was 124, it usually is always 100 – why is that?

It could be because of an infection or a restless night. If this happens once or only rarely, do not worry about it. If such fluctuations occur several times a week and if they are also concomitant with feeling unwell or fatigued, you should see a doctor.


Q. I feel tired all the time but get enough sleep according to the sleep score. What am I doing wrong?

There can be different causes for why you feel tired even though you get enough sleep:

  • (1) Perhaps you are one of those people, who tend to need 9 instead of 7 hours of sleep and therefore do not sleep enough for your personal need for sleep. The sleep score is always based on the average age and it can only be an approximation;

  • (2) Perhaps you are sleeping outside of your natural rhythm, e.g. irregularly or you go to bed early or late. Try regular bedtimes and go to bed when you are tired;

  • (3) If in doubt, we recommend that you consult a doctor to clarify the cause. Permanent fatigue may be a warning sign from the body.



Q. I move extremely much while I sleep. Is this harmful to my recovery?

In this case, it applies again: everyone is different. On average, people move about 25 times a night. However, there are big differences from person to person. Generally, someone who feels rested when he wakes up, can perform his work during the day, and sleep at night should not worry about moving around too much or too little during the night. Children, for example, move much more during the night than grown-ups do and they have many more deep sleep phases at the same time.


Q. I do not know in what position I sleep. How can the bed help me with this?

The sleep score can be measured correctly also independently of your sleeping position, for as long as you sleep either on your back, front, or side. For this, however, you should not sleep too far from the outer edge of the bed. Pets or another person lying at the side of the bed can also cause a falsification of the results of the sleep score.



Q. What influence does my breathing rate have on my sleep?

The breathing rate gets calmer the deeper we sleep and rises slightly while we are in dreaming sleep. As we always pass the different sleep phases over again at night, fluctuations in the breathing rate are also normal. In addition, the breathing rate is affected by infections and stress. Targeted, calm, and steady breathing during meditation, for example, can help us relax and fall asleep easier.



Q. What influence does snoring have on my sleep?

Studies show inconsistent results as to the influence of snoring on the quality of sleep. Most conclude, however, that snoring by itself initially does not have any effects on the sleep of the snorer (unless their partner shoves them in the ribs).


Q. I feel muscular tensions in spite of the motion what should I have to do?

The mattress can help you loosen back muscle tension by facilitating an ergonomic recumbent position. Unfortunately, it alone cannot resolve all problems. Regular physiotherapy, exercise, sports, and stretching help loosen muscular tensions for good.


Q. How long does it take until my body gets used to the new motion base?

It can take a few days to weeks until your body gets used to the new base you sleep on. Every intervention in our habits can first interfere with our sleep. The new feeling when lying on a new mattress is one of these interventions. That is why you should always wait a few weeks to get used to it. You will only be able to tell how good the mattress rally is after this.
