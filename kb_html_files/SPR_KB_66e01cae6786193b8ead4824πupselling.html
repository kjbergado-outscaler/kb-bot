What is Upselling?


Upselling is one of the advanced sales techniques commonly used to increase revenue. And it is done by advising a customer about the more premium product, which is more expensive and also has more features. For example, the customer wants to buy a printer and you offer them a scanner-printer that is able to fulfill more objectives on top of the printer ones.


The Upselling technique is used by many companies to boost sales. The strategy consists basically in putting in front of the consumer more options related to their principal search but with a different range in price and quality. For online stores, for example, it's likely that the customer will navigate to the different and expensive options, to loom for the features before making a final decision.




The "Good-Better-Best" strategy


This is Emma's strategy for the upselling sales technique. It consists of the marketing approach of having a pricing strategy for some product categories, like mattresses and beds.


The methodology applied here is to divide the products into 3 classifications: Good -> Better -> Best.


Like this, upfront the customer is aware of the product with more benefits and qualities. In case they cannot afford it, then you can present and explain in detail the other two options. In this way, the customers can compare themselves and decide what they are willing to give up (benefits or price).




Why do we start with the higher price at Emma?


  • Competitors: Even in the premium segment, we are often in the medium to mid-high price range compared to competitors. We are generally far away from premium price ranges above 1000+â‚¬. This offers us the possibility.


  • We set a price anchor. The first price the customer hears is the foundation. The probability of selling at least the mid-price range as a more favourable offer increases. All prices below the anchor are perceived as favourable.


  • Higher margin: Products with higher prices often have higher margins, meaning that the profit per sale is higher. This can be advantageous for the company and allow for higher investments in marketing and sales.


  • Value and quality statement: A higher price can also be an indicator of better quality or higher value, which can encourage customers to purchase a more expensive product. This can be particularly important for customers who value quality and durability.


  • Brand image: A higher price can also help create a brand image associated with exclusivity and luxury. This can be very appealing to certain target groups, such as customers with higher incomes.


  • Upselling: Selling higher-priced products can also increase the chance of selling additional products or services. Customers who are already willing to pay for a more expensive product are often also willing to pay more for complementary products or services.


  • Reduce returns: As Emma products are uplifting in terms of features, a high quality product is more likely to meet more of the customer's needs. This also reduces the likelihood of returns.



Products with low prices:


-> Advantages:

  • May be easier to sell as the price is more affordable for customers.

  • Can appeal to a larger group of customers, especially customers with limited budgets.

  • Can serve as an entry point for sales to gain customers' trust.


-> Disadvantages:

  • The low price may reduce the quality of the product in the eyes of customers.

  • May have lower profit margins, which can affect the profitability of the business.

  • May be difficult to convince customers that it is a high-quality product.



Middle-price products:


-> Advantages:

  • Can offer good value for money that appeals to customers.

  • Can serve as a compromise between low and high prices.

  • Can offer higher profit margins than low-priced products.


-> Disadvantages:

  • May be harder to sell because the price is higher than other products.

  • May appeal to customers looking for a lower or higher price range, making the target audience more limited.

  • May be perceived by customers as an average or not particularly outstanding product.



Products with a high price tag:


-> Advantages:

  • Can signal high quality and value, appealing to customers.

  • Can offer a product to customers who are willing to pay more for quality or exclusivity.

  • Can offer higher profit margins, which can contribute to the profitability of the business.


-> Disadvantages:

  • Can be harder to sell as the price can be a deterrent for some customers.

  • May appeal to a limited target audience as not all customers are willing to pay more for a product.

  • May be perceived as overly expensive or redundant if the value of the product is not sufficiently communicated.
