This article is only applicable for contacts made through public messages on Facebook, Instagram, Trustpilot and Yotpo.

​

Purpose 

These guidelines support Emma’s international Customer Excellence Agents in communicating consistently with our brand voice while remaining empathetic and human. They complement the existing Sprinklr Guidelines and focus on initiative, tone, and personalization. 

 

  1. Mindset & Initiative 

  • From script to voice: Templates are a starting point, not a final answer. Use them as guidance but always adapt replies into your own words. 

  • Confident adaptation: Adjusting wording and phrasing is encouraged, as long as our brand values remain intact. 

  • Empathy over efficiency: An authentic, warm reply is more valuable than copy-paste speed. 

Guideline: Use templates for inspiration, but always rewrite replies so they sound empathetic, friendly, and human. 

  1. Encouraging Personalization 

  • Name & situation: Address the customer by name and paraphrase their concern. 

  • Add context: Include small, relevant touches (e.g., week, month, season-dependend. 

  • Balance: Personal, but never casual or unprofessional. 

Guideline: Show that you truly understood the customer’s situation by paraphrasing it and adding small contextual details. 

  1. Translation & Localization Principles 

  • Never literal, always translate for meaning and tone, not word-for-word. 

  • Local expressions: Use phrases that sound natural and authentic in the target culture. 

  • Consistency check: Ask yourself: “Does this sound like Emma?” 

  1. Link to Sprinklr Guidelines 

  • Sprinklr Guidelines provide the structure (Greeting, Empathy, Resolution, Closure). 

  • These additional guidelines emphasize initiative, warmth, and tone as essential layers on top of that structure. 

Summary 

Our customers should always feel that there are real people behind Emma (and our responses everywhere) – people who listen, understand, and provide solutions. Every reply is an opportunity to build trust and reinforce our brand values: optimistic, friendly, competent – and always human. 

Introduction and disclaimer 

 

This article is meant to act as a guideline on what we expect a reply to a public social media contact with Emma to look like. However, it is not meant to be seen as a set of restraints, but rather a platform on which agents can build upon.   

Our main goal will always be to have our contacts come as close as possible to an actual conversation between two human beings. 

Hence, why allowing agents to find their voice and be naturally empathic is so important. We want contacts to be fluid and personalized. Emma is a very agile company, so the agents representing us should never be (or sound) rigid. 

As Emma representatives, we want to show that we are humans, and humans are not perfect. We always acknowledge the complicated bits, so we can honestly and authentically give solutions.

Public contacts structure breakdown 

The four major dividers in a public contact are: 

 

  • Identifying the level of escalation 

  • Initial greeting and acknowledgement 

  • Resolution 

  • Closure 
     

  1. Identifying the level of escalation 

 

  • Opening the contact and understanding the issue* 

 

Note: the levels of escalation are applicable to 1–2-star reviews. 

 

According to the user research, consumers use reviews to assess how well the company handles customer inquiries or concerns to gauge overall user experience and satisfaction. They look for the reviews that articulate the effectiveness of the company's unique selling propositions (USPs), including adherence to return policies, adept problem-solving and the efficiency of cashback processes. 

  • When publicly contacting the customer, we establish indicators of our service quality. Not only we try to create a positive experience for the reviewer, but also showcasing our authenticity and reliability for the rest of the consumers. 

 

We know that some cases are more concerning than others, and some customers might be more sensitive depending on their issue. Some of them might have tried to solve their issue with us several times and couldn’t, causing a lot of frustration. Some might have even contacted a lawyer to take legal action against us. And others might be frustrated with the product they received. 

 

All cases are important and should be treated as such. But depending on the level, we should take different actions and reply in different ways. Here is a quick way to identify the levels of escalation: 

 

 

Level 1 – Mild Frustration / Disappointment 

Level 2 – High Frustration / Anger 

Level 3 – Extreme Frustration / Outrage 

What is this level of escalation? 

Mild complaints about product or service quality where we can still turn it into a neutral/positive experience with the right reply and action. 

High levels of frustration, more damaging to our reputation and need to be sent faster to internal handling.  

 

Possibly longer complaints with more than one paragraph, use of caps lock, and mentions of other unsuccessful attempts of contact. 

Highest level of frustration that can possibly lead to serious company damage.  

 

Possibly longer complaints with more than one paragraph, use of caps lock, and mentions of other unsuccessful attempts of contact.  

 

Specific alarming keywords are used that can give us a hint that the customer will take some action against the company. 

How to identify the level of escalation? 

If any of these words are used: "musty smell", "sagging mattress", "disappointed", "uncomfortable", "discomfort", "generic excuses" 

If any of these words are used: "nightmare”, "joke", "appalling", "terrible", "laughable", “unethical”, “shocking”, “disgusting”, "shocking service", "awful company", "absolute shambles," "incompetence”   

If any of these words are used: "legal advice", "lawyer”, “fraud”, “scam”, “illegal”, “bankrupt”     

 

After identifying the customer's level, you can now act accordingly following the next steps. 

 

To show contrition and empathy, our tone shifts from more lighthearted to more apologetic as we move up the customer frustration levels. Our responses will also be lengthier at each stage, so that we can give customers the required information.   

  

The following templates provide a guide for tone and content, but agents should bring their own personality to their responses, as this will be more sincere and thoughtful. Agents are, however, welcome to mix and match the responses (always checking that the grammar is correct once they combine responses).
