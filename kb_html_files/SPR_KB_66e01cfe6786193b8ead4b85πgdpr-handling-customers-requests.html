How to Identify a GDPR Request:


Customer service often receives DSR (data subject rights) requests. When someone asks about their personal information and what they want to do with it, it probably be a DSR. These requests are like specific messages where people ask for things like seeing their data, deleting it, or stopping Emma from using it in certain ways.

Identifying a DSR request also means recognizing when someone is asking about their rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).


Below you can find the link for the articles related to the DSR Requests that a CS agent may receive:

  • GDPR: Right to object/oppose

  • GDPR: Right to delete/erasure

  • GDPR: Right to access

  • GDPR: Right to portability

  • GDPR: Right to restrict/limit

  • GDPR: Right to rectify/correct



What to do when you receive a GDPR request from a Customer?



If you receive other GDPR requests, you should immediately forward it to the email: privacy@emma-sleep.com.


Don’t forget to send the canned acknowledgment reply to the customer. Make sure you properly tag a GDPR ticket if it is really related to personal data and data protection.



Step 1: Forward the request to Legal & Compliance (except if the request is Right to rectify/correct order data)


If you receive the request via Freshdesk, chat, email, call or face-to-face please forward the request right away to:


privacy@emma-sleep.com

The Legal & Compliance team will reply to the DSR.




Step 2: Reply to the Customer using Canned Response


Suggested Response to Reply:


Dear (Customer's first name), Thank you for your inquiry regarding personal data. We have forwarded your request to our Data Protection team, and they will reply to you in due course. Thank you, Your Emma Team



ATTENTION: Mandatory for marketing unsubscribe requests


If you receive a request to be unsubscribed/opposing from marketing, don’t forget to add this in your DSR acknowledgment response:


“If you have received a marketing communication of Emma products and wish to opt out from receiving it, please reply to this e-mail including the communication as an attachment. Although this is not obligatory, we will be able to identify the sender more easily and communicate your intention to opt out more quickly.”
