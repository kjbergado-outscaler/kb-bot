What is the Right to Limit/Restrict?


The "right to limit" means that individuals can request restrictions on the processing of their personal data under certain circumstances. For example, if someone asks Emma to stop processing their data for targeted advertising, they are exercising their right to limit.



Examples and how to handle:


Example 1:

Michael has been receiving targeted ads from Emma based on his browsing history. He finds these ads intrusive and requests that the retailer stops using his data for personalized advertising.


As a result, Emma agrees to only show him generic ads instead.


Example 2:
A customer who says: "I'm currently involved in a legal dispute with your company and request that you limit the processing of my personal data until the matter is resolved. Please refrain from using my data for any purposes unrelated to the ongoing legal proceedings."


This request is from someone involved in a legal dispute with the company who wants to limit the processing of their personal data until the matter is resolved.



How to handle requests for "Right to Limit/Restrict"?



Step 1: Forward the request to Legal & Compliance


If you receive the request via Freshdesk, chat, email, call or face-to-face please forward the request right away to:


privacy@emma-sleep.com

The Legal & Compliance team will reply to the DSR.




Step 2: Reply to the Customer using Canned Response


Suggested Response to Reply:


Dear (Customer's first name), Thank you for your inquiry regarding personal data. We have forwarded your request to our Data Protection team, and they will reply to you in due course. Thank you, Your Emma Team
