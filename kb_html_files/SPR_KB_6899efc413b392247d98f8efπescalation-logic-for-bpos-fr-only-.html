What is an escalation?

An escalation happens when a case or issue goes out of the normal workflow and is sent to a higher level of support or other departments.

​

Types of escalations

​Description

Complex case

A case that became escalated due to it's complexity.

Requested by customer

When a customer requestes an intervention of a higher level team.

Priority Request

Cases opened by Emma internals or Offline/Stores regarding D2C customer orders.

Legal Cases

BPO receives a case where legal action is required (e.g: court letter or an email from a customer’s lawyer)

1. Global Process and Workflow

​Global Process and Workflow / Market specifics: Is your market present? Yes: Click your Market tab, ignore Global Process. No: refer to the Global Process and Workflow.

1.1 Overview

The process flow outlines the movement between teams and roles based on the type of escalation and the source that initiated it, ensuring a clear understanding of responsibilities and actions. If you have to escalate one of your cases, do it manually according to the following flow.

​

​Let's start by understanding roles:

​

1.2 Roles

Different intervenients have different roles for an escalated ticket.

Role
Description

L1

Handles customer inquiries directly, assisting customers, solving problems, and ensuring that only cases that truly require higher-level attention are escalated, helping keep the support process efficient.

L2/Supervisor

Experienced agent and/or supervisor that handles cases that L1 agents can’t resolve, using deeper expertise and system access (if applicable) to unblock cases.

Escalations Squad

Emma internals that handle escalations coming from L2 and other escalations according this KB.

​

1.3 Types of escalated cases and parameters

Occurs when a customer’s issue remains unresolved despite repeated contact or the situation is exceptional and requires intervential/approval from other teams. Some scenarios:

​

  • Standard process is failing (e.g., correct SKU supplemented but wrong product delivered).

  • There's no information in the Knowledge Base to address the case.

  • Persistent quality issues on the same product on different cases.

  • Lack of responsiveness or possible errors caused by internal teams.

  • Logistic, product, refund, return, or order delay cases with no solution and on hold for more than 5 days.

​​

Escalates to: Level 2. if Level 2 cannot resolve after trying different solutions (check section 1.6 in this article), then send it to Escalation Squad (only L2 can escalate it to the Squad).

Occurs when a customer cannot resolve their issue and requests or demands higher-level intervention. Some scenarios:

  • Customer asks to speak to a supervisor/higher management

  • Expresses extreme dissatisfaction/overly rejects a given solution on >2 contact/interactions

  • Threten to go to the press

​

Escalates to: Level 2/Supervisor (in case it's applicable).

1.4 How to Escalate a case

Find here the flow to escalate a case:

Acknowledge the customer’s concern

​

  • Respond with empathy and confirm that you understand their issue.

  • Explain that their case will be reviewed by another team, an internal team, or a supervisor (e.g., Senior Agents, Escalation Squad).
    Example:

“I understand how frustrating this must be for you. I’ll forward your case to our specialized team for further review.”

​

  1. Verify customer information

    • Ensure all details are accurate (e.g., order number, customer name, email address).

  2. Document the reason for escalation

    • Add clear and concise case details in the Collaboration Note, explaining why further escalation is required.

  3. Validate if an email case exists & merge other email cases from the the same order num

    1. If the case is not already associated with an email, click Set Email and add the customer’s email address.

  4. Assign to the correct team

    • Within BPO:

      • Assign to L2 Agent queue.

      • For supervisor review: Assign to [Supervisor Name].

    • For Emma review: Assign to Escalation SQUAD (L2 agents only)

    ​

​

  1. Review the case history

    • Check the case notes and the escalation justification before taking action.

  2. Investigate the issue

    • Gather all relevant information to understand the customer’s concern fully.

  3. Contact the customer

    • Provide a clear, empathetic, and complete response, ensuring their issue is resolved or next steps are clearly communicated.

What to do if you disagree with the decision (of the previous level) to escalate the case?

​

  1. Record your feedback

    • Add your reasoning in the Case Notes.

  2. Report the case

    • During the Hypercare (first weeks of implementation) we will have an excel file for you to report this cases, please request the file to your team leader

      ​

  3. Resolve situation - We still want to make sure the situation is resolved for the customer

Offer a product instead of finantial compensation

If the customer is really escalated and demanding for a product "gift" instead of a regular finantial compensation, L2 Agent is allowed to offer the customer a product with a price that doesn't exceed the value of the applicable finantial compensation (generally, accessories) before the discount (check webshop).

1. Check the finantial compensation applicable HERE

2. Check possible options available on the webshop of the correspoding market (consider the product's original price, before discount) + stock levels for the SKU ​
