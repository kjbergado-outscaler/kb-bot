Ticket Assignment/Handling

On a daily basis, all agents should be working on their In Progress tickets (in addition to New tickets) to ensure we respond to all customers on time.


  • Handle/Assign tickets in order from oldest to newest (Tickets with the oldest SLA should be handled first).


Do not skip tickets or engage in cherry-picking, it causes users to wait longer for a response and is unfair to the rest of the team.

Ticket Reassignment

It is preferable to have only one agent deal with each ticket, so the customer doesn't think we are passing them off, but our goal is to balance response times with creating and maintaining a great customer experience, so sometimes more than one person may have to help a customer resolve their issue. Follow these guidelines for assigning tickets between agents:


Try not to exceed 3 agents involved in the same ticket.



When should we reassign a ticket to another agent?

  • The previous agent that owns the ticket is on leave, absent or vacation.
  • The original owner has a considerable backlog that will most likely impact response times.


What should I do if a ticket is reassigned to me?

  • Treat the case as if it was your own.
  • Check the case documentation to get context on the ticket's progress and respond to the customer.
  • Keep the ticket if your interaction with the customer leads to resolution.
  • If you interaction is just an incremental update and doesn't solve the ticket, reassign back to the previous owner once they are able to continue handling it (For example: they are back at the office). Don't forget to document!


If multiple agents have handled the ticket, who should keep it for closure?

  • The ticket should be assigned to the last agent who had a meaningful contribution to the ticket. This is likely the interaction the customer will remember most when deciding how to rate our quality of service.


Ticket Merging

On a daily basis, all agents should be working on their In Progress tickets (in addition to New tickets) to ensure we respond to all customers on time.


When merging tickets, always make sure that the merging results in the original ticket going to In Progress, if that doesn't happen, please do it manually.


If a customer has 2 new tickets - no replies have been made

  1. Merge the two tickets together only if they are about the same topic. If different issues, don't merge.
  2. Assign the ticket to yourself and respond to the customer.

If a customer has 1 open (A) and 1 new ticket (B) - about the same issue
Ticket A is open and another agent has replied to the request
Ticket B is new and no replies have been made on the ticket

  1. Do not assign Ticket B to yourself
  2. Merge Ticket B into Ticket A

If a customer has 1 open (A) and 1 new ticket (B) - about different issues

  1. Do not merge the tickets
  2. Assign ticket B to yourself and answer normally

If a customer has 2 (A and B) open tickets - same issue, and both have responses
Ticket A is assigned to another agent
Ticket B is assigned to you

  1. Merge the ticket with the oldest response into the ticket with the newest response.
