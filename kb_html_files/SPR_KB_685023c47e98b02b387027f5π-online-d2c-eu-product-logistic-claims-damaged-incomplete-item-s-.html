Step 1 - Gather all the Necessary Data

a) Ensure Customer is Within the Warranty Period

  • Warranty period validation - here

    • If YES proceed with the process

    • If NO inform customer that the item is outside its warranty period, and that we’re unable to provide a replacement or accept a return for refund.

b) Damaged Component/Part or Item

  • Request clear photos of the damaged part, per Product Claims: Customer Instructions for Product Claims

  • Also request photos of the outer box and the labels (clearly showing the barcode, also known as FAR) - Check the section "What and How to: The fullfilment Article Reference and Where to find it" for more details

c) Incomplete Item

1.Confirm that the order is truly incomplete

  • Open the order in Fluent and inspect the tracking.

  • Make sure every SKU appears under a tracking number.

  • The carrier’s Proof of Delivery (POD) is the fastest way to verify how many packages were actually delivered under a given tracking number versus the quantity that was supposed to be originally shipped.

Possible shipping setups

  • One box, one tracking number – all items should be under that number.Several boxes, one master tracking number – expand the master number and confirm every item is listed.

  • Several boxes, each with its own tracking number – check every number until all items are accounted for.

  • If multiple tracking & still a “missing” package - please update the customer on the package’s status.If you suspect it’s lost in transit, consult the Logistics Claim knowledge base before acting.

  • Single tracking number but items still missing, treat it as an incomplete shipment and move to the next step of the workflow.

2.Do not request photos of the missing item (as it is not present).

3.However, request clear photos of the packaging, including the outer box and the label (clearly showing the barcode, also known as FAR) - Check the section "What and How to: The fullfilment Article Reference and Where to find it" for more details

​

d) Identifying Specific Parts/Components (Beds & Sofas)


If the issue concerns a specific part or component, ask the customer to:

  • Highlight or mark the affected part in the product booklet and share it with us via email.

  • You’ll need to cross-check this information later on with the Spare Parts & Assembly Playbook file to ensure you supplement the correct SKU for the customer.

Step 2 - Validate the Claim

a) Determine whether the claim is valid:

  • If the claim is valid, proceed to the next step.

  • If the claim is not valid, inform the customer accordingly and close the case.

  • For further reference consult Product Claims: General Guidelines for Product Claim Validation

    ​

    ​

b) Logistic or Product Claim:

  • If the outer box is visibly damaged, treat it as a logistics claim.

  • If the outer box is intact and only the product is affected, treat it as a product claim.

    ​

    LOGISTIC CLAIMS

    All logistics damage claims must be entered in Sprinklr—and properly categorized—within five business days of delivery. You don’t need to resolve the claim in that timeframe, but the ticket must be opened and tagged immediately; don’t wait for the case to be settled or for extra customer details before submitting. Update the ticket as soon as new information arrives.

    Note: If the carrier ever contacts us directly and confirms that they damaged a parcel, please attach this email to the claim.

    LOGISTIC CLAIMS

c) Logging the Claim in the Workflow (Mandatory)

  • Use the Claim Workflow to log every claim—this is a MANDATORY part of the process.
    However, only update the workflow once you have gathered all the necessary information (e.g., photos, product booklet references, packaging images, etc.).

  • This step is crucial, as it allows Emma to properly report issues to carriers and suppliers and supports continuous product and service improvements.

Claim flow in Sprinklr is MANDATORY

Always log the claim workflow, even when rejected

Step 3 – Product Usability & Customer Safety

Carefully analyze the pictures and/or customer description. Ask yourself:
Does the reported issue affect the usability of the product or pose any risk to the customer's safety?

  • If YES: Proceed to the next step.

  • If NO: Offer the customer keeps the item as-is and offer a goodwill compensation in line with our Compensation Matrix, if the customer does not accept proceed to step 4

​

  • Small stain on the fabric cover (cosmetic only, no odor or hygiene risk) → Does not impact usability or safety → Offer compensation

  • Noticeable sag in the center affecting sleep posture → Impacts usability → Proceed to next step.Torn cover exposing foam or inner springs → Safety risk → Proceed to next step

  • Slight scratch on an inner panel → Cosmetic only → Offer compensation.

  • Missing screw(s) or broken side rail affecting support → Impacts usability → Proceed to next step.Sharp edge or unstable frame posing a fall hazard → Safety risk → Proceed to next step.

Safety Comes First

When in doubt, always prioritize customer safety and product functionality

Step 4 - Replace the Item or Component/Part

a) Before you Create a Supplement - Guidelines:

  • You can find the Spare Parts & Assembly Playbook file HERE

    Where you will find:

    • All required SKUs for spare parts and mattress covers (mapped to each mattress model)

    • Box assembly details to help you identify which box needs to be sent, in case spare parts are out of stock or not available

    • Additional instructions and important notes for processing supplements

    • When reviewing the box assembly, double-check the bed or sofa size, because both the number of boxes and the specific components vary accordingly

    • When you need to supplement a full item—such as accessories—please note that the SKU file does not include these SKUs.Instead, use Fluent to look up the correct full item SKU based on the original order.In cases where you cannot supplement the full item (using the parent SKU), you will need to create one supplement with all boxes (when applicable)

    • The item names listed in the file are our internal names, which match what is used in Fluent.

      To understand which product is being referred to, please consult the order in Fluent directly.Do not refer to the website product names, as these can vary significantly across different regions and may lead to confusion.

    • If you need to supplement a specific box, gather the SKU from fluent

Reminders:

  • Some spare part SKUs may include additional components. This is highlighted in the SKU file.Please make sure to inform the customer in advance, so they are not surprised upon receiving extra parts. These additional components are for the customer to keep and use in case they are needed in the future.

  • Once a supplement is submitted, it’s treated like a regular order. The customer will receive standard email updates (e.g., order confirmation, shipping details).→ Make sure to inform the customer about this, to set the correct expectation and avoid addtional contacts

  • Always check stock before placing the order.

    • When an item/part is out of stock, the order is put on hold until inventory is replenished, which can cause delays, frustrate customers, and generate additional inquiries, if expectations are not set correctly

    • Always try to supplement the smallest missing piece first—individual part, then box—before replacing the entire item.

    • If a spare part is unavailable: identify the box it belongs to and send a replacement for that specific box.

    • Whichever option you choose, set clear, realistic expectations about timing and availability.

  • Find the “How to Supplement Orders” Knowledge Base article here for your reference and guidance

CHECK STOCK AVAILABILITY

Always check stock availability before submitting a supplement order.

In certain regions, individual spare parts (e.g., components) may be available to supplement.

a) Check Missing/Damage part & Regional Availability

  • Confirm whether spare parts are available

  • Confrim if those parts are available in the customers region

b) If Spare Parts Are Available in the Region:

  • Check if the specific part is in stock.

    • If yes → Supplement the individual component only.

    • If no → Identify the box that contains the missing/damaged part and supplement the specific box instead.

c) If Spare Parts Are Not Available in the Customer's Region:

  • Identify the Box

    • Locate which box contains the missing or damaged part.

  • Check Box Availability

    • If the specific box is in stock, supplement only that box.

d) If Neither the Part Nor the Box Is Available:

  • If the box is out of stock, adding a full-item supplement ( which means we will need to add all boxes to the supplement) doesn’t override the shortage—the missing box remains on hold and will ship only after it’s back in stock. Ideally, supplement only the specific box that contains the missing or damaged part. Let the customer know that this box will ship once it’s back in stock.

    If customer is within the trail period and they don’t want to wait, offer to process a return for a full refund instead.

Mattress

​Individual components such as the cover may be supplemented separately.

  1. Identify the Issue
    First, determine whether the issue is with the mattress itself or just the cover.

  2. If the Issue is the Mattress

    • Supplement the entire item.

    • Mattress SKUs are not included in the file.

    • Use Fluent to locate the SKU from the original order.

  3. If the Issue is Only the Cover

    • Follow the specific steps outlined below (continue with those steps as needed).

​

Mattress Covers

In certain markets, spare-parts for mattress covers are available. Please refer to the Spare Parts & Assembly Playbook to confirm which specific cover corresponds to each mattress model.

​

  • If the cover is in stock → Supplement the cover only

  • If the cover is not available → Provide the full mattress

 

Please note:

  • We do not have replacement covers for every mattress model, but we are able to cover approximately 80% of them.

  • The replacement covers we do provide are often an upgrade in quality and design.

  • It is essential to set proper expectations with the customer before sending a cover replacement, please use the tempalte below for written consent from the customer

  • If the customer does not accept the cover replacement, send the full item replacement

"Dear [Customer Name],
Thank you again for your patience.

This replacement meets the same performance and durability standards as the original — and in some aspects, it even offers improvements. The main difference lies in the design, while the feel and functionality remain very much the same.

We’ve carefully selected this alternative to ensure it delivers the comfort and quality you expect from us.

Additionally, returning a mattress due to a cover issue often results in it being discarded, which we always try to avoid. By choosing this cover replacement, you’re not only getting a reliable solution — you're also supporting a more eco-conscious and less wasteful approach.

Please confirm if you would like to proceed with this alternative, or let us know if you have any questions — we’re happy to help.

Best regards,
[Agent Name]"
