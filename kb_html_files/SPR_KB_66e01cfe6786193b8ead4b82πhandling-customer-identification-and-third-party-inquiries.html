Instructions to confirm Customer Identity and reply to Third Party Inquiries


In order to protect our customers' personal data and comply with GDPR, we must confirm the identity of any customer requesting information about their purchase. This ensures that we only share personal data with the correct individual.

Note: as of 31st of March 2025, all customers with an order in Fluent need to be authenticated through the Authentication flow. (Not applicable for Magento orders.)

Steps to Confirm Customer Identity:

  1. Introduction and Explanation:

    • Script:

      • "Hi, thank you for contacting Emma. To protect your personal data and comply with GDPR, we need to confirm your identity before providing any information about your purchase. This is to ensure that your information remains secure."

  2. Request for Identity Confirmation:

    • Ask the customer to provide the following details:

      • Order Number:

        • "Can you please provide your order number?"

      • Email Address:

        • "Could you confirm the email address used to place the order?"

      • Full Name:

        • "May I have your full name as it appears on the order?"

      • Delivery Address:


        • "Can you please confirm the delivery address for the order?"

  3. Verification:

    • Check the provided details against the information in our system.

    • Script:

      • "Thank you. Let me quickly verify these details."

  4. If Details Match:

    • Script:

      • "Your identity has been confirmed. How can I assist you with your order today?"

  5. If Details Do Not Match:

    • Example Script:

      • "I'm sorry, but the details you provided do not match our records. For security reasons, I cannot provide any information about the order. You may want to check the information and try again."

  6. When a Different Person is Asking for Information:

    • Example Script:

      • "For privacy and security reasons, we can only provide order information to the person who made the purchase. If you have their consent, please ask them to send an email from the address used to place the order, authorizing us to share the information with you."


Real-Life Example Scenarios
-> Correct Identity Confirmation:

  • Customer: "I would like to know the status of my order. My order number is 123456, my email is john.doe@example.com, my name is John Doe, and my delivery address is 123 Sleep Lane, Dreamtown."

  • Agent: "Thank you, Mr. Doe. Your identity has been confirmed. Your order is currently being processed and will be shipped within the next two days."

-> Incorrect Identity Confirmation:

  • Customer: "Can you tell me about my order? My order number is 654321."

  • Agent: "Can you also provide the email address used to place the order?"

  • Customer: "Uh, I'm not sure. Maybe john.doe@example.com?"

  • Agent: "I'm sorry, but the details you provided do not match our records. For security reasons, I cannot provide any information about the order."

-> Third-Party Inquiry:

  • Caller: "I want to check the status of an order placed by my friend. The order number is 123456."

  • Agent: "For privacy and security reasons, we can only provide order information to the person who made the purchase. Please ask your friend to email us from the address used to place the order, authorizing us to share the information with you."

-> Third-Party Delivery Request:

  • Husband: "Hi, my wife ordered a mattress and I want it delivered to a different address. The order number is 789012."

  • Agent: "For privacy and security reasons, we can only change the delivery address if your wife, who made the purchase, sends us an email from the address used to place the order, authorizing this change."

-> Third-Party Refund Request:

  • Father: "I am calling on behalf of my daughter. She ordered a pillow and wants the refund to be processed to my bank account. The order number is 345678."

  • Agent: "For privacy and security reasons, we can only process refunds to the original payment method. If your daughter wants the refund to be processed to a different account, she needs to email us from the address used to place the order, authorizing this change and providing the new IBAN."

-> Heir Requesting Data of a Deceased Person:

  • Heir: "Hello, I am the son of Jane Doe, who recently passed away. I need information regarding her recent purchase from your company. The order number is 987654."

  • Agent: "I am very sorry for your loss. For privacy and security reasons, we require certain documentation before we can provide any information. Could you please send us a copy of the death certificate and a legal document proving you are the executor or authorized person handling her affairs? You can send these documents to our email address [email@example.com]. Once we have received and verified them, we will be able to assist you further."



Important Note: Always maintain a polite and professional tone. Ensure the customer understands that these steps are necessary to protect their personal data and comply with GDPR regulations.

Additional Resources: For any questions or further clarification, please refer to our internal GDPR compliance guide or contact the data privacy team at privacy@emma-sleep.com.
