Identification factors

For any customer contact (phone, email, and chat) in which the customer requests information on their Emma account, information or action on an existing order, we should never confirm the existence of the order or provide any information to the customer, concerning the order or the account, until the customer's identity has been verified.

However, if the customer is contacting us via email and the email address being used is the same as the one used to place the order with us, then we can consider the customer automatically authenticated.

Additionally, we can change this information in the Order Management System (Magento or FluentCommerce), but we need to make sure that we authenticated the customer first, using three additional factors, excluding the one we will be amending. So, for example, if the customer wants to change the email address connected to the order we must authenticate him/her first using three of the following five elements: name under which the order was placed, order number, delivery address or phone number.


How do we identify the customer?

​

We must match 3 identification factors, before giving any information or acting on an existing order.

​

Those factors are important to verify the customer's identity before acting on their requests. This will avoid disclosing data to a party that is not entitled to it and also potential fraud.


  • Name under which the order was placed (do not confuse this with the name under the billing or shipping addresses)

  • Email address used to place the order

  • Order number

  • Delivery address

  • Telephone number included in the billing address

It is necessary to leave a note (Yes / No / N/A) in Magento for each customer contact if the identity has been verified:


  • Yes: Identity successfully verified with at least 3 matching identification factors from the order.


  • No: Identity could not be verified - could not proceed with the request.


  • N/A: Identification was not necessary.


Verification is not necessary for requests that do not relate to an order or a customer account.

IMPORTANT: We should not ask a customer to provide us with any additional authentication factors after they have given us 3 valid ones. After this is done, if we continue to ask for more authentication factors, we are blocking the customer's access to information that they are at this point legally entitled to.


Third-party verification

​

If a third party contacts us requesting information/action on an order that he/she did not place themselves, we may NOT give out any info in the first instance.


IMPORTANT


  • If police officers or lawyers contact us regarding an order or a customer account, information is not allowed to be provided on the phone or via chat.


  1. Phone Contact: We ask third parties to put the person who placed the order on the phone, verify his/her identity, and proactively ask for their authorisation to talk about the order with the third party.

    If the customer is not there, they must contact us themselves by email or by phone at a later time to request the information in question. Alternatively, during the phone call, we can send an email to the email address that the customer left in their order without providing said email address to the person contacting us on the phone.

  2. Chat Contact: If the person contacting us identifies himself/herself as the customer that placed the order and correctly provides 3 identification factors, we proceed with the contact.


However, if the person contacting us via chat identifies himself/herself as someone else, we then have two alternatives. Either that person provides a written authorization letter (CE_Authorization Letter, file attached to this article) signed by the customer that placed the order, allowing us to talk and disclose information to the current requester (wife, son, friend, etc) or we ask to be contacted using the email address that was used to place the order (which will bypass any need for further authentication.


  • Email Contact: If the contact is coming from the email address that was used to place the order on OMS, even if it is signed by someone else, we can proceed and do not require further authentication. However, if the person is contacting us using a different email address than the one that was used to place the order, the agents should ask the customer to contact us using the email address that was used to place the order and tell the customer that, if that is no longer possible, they should provide us their name (as present in the order), the order number and the email address used to place the order, so that we are able to assist further.


Third-party verification in cases of death/bereavement

For verification purposes, in cases of bereavement the person contacting only needs to provide the 3 authentication factors, following the same process as if it was the customer contacting us (No relationship documentation is needed).

However in case changes are requested, e.g.: customer wants to have the receipt sent to another email. We will require the customer to provide a death certificate for the person that originally placed the order.
Again no relationship document is needed.

Once you have received a valid death certificate you can proceed with the change requested and afterwards the document (death certificate) will need to be deleted.

Death certificate is needed only for due diligence and is deleted as soon as possible.
