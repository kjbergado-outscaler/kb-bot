Introduction


The Emma Quality Team found an issue in the Emma Original Bed - BoxBed V3, that some units are being affected by a bent vertical beam.


Due to this, we are expecting customers to reach our support line looking for support. In this article, then, you can find the mandatory guidelines created by the Quality team to help you handle those cases.

How to handle the claims


Step 1:


When the customer contacts us complaining the BoxBed V3 is damaged, we need to validate the proof of the claim. The customers must provide Emma Customer Service a proof of the problem they are facing in order to proceed to the next steps.

Proof of the claim:


  1. Photo of the claim reason/defect presented of the product;

  2. Picture of the label of the product: This can be taken either from the product box or from the product itself;

  3. Invoice of the purchase: The invoice is received by the customer via email when the purchase is concluded. Those elements are essential to improve our products and business, and the proper team takes action over the complaints we are handling.



-> For more details about "Validation", you can review the article about "Product Claim".


Step 2:


Once the defect was identified, the agent should focus first on solving the customer's problem. For this defect usually, the parts damaged will be P007 and P017 (see image at the end of the article).


If other parts are damaged, please check the Spare Part Process: here



Actions to be taken for the parts P007 and P017:



2.1: DACH, BE and NL


Create a supplement order for the two spare parts:

  • P007: EACBBMCB999AAA

  • P017: EACBBVBM200AAB (for beds 200cm long) or EACBBVBM210AAB (for beds 210cm long)



2.2: UK

  • 135x190cm: Order the spare parts EACBBMCB999AAA and EACBBVBM190AAA as supplement order.

  • 150x200cm: Order box 3 as supplement order, SKU EBDBB150200X31

  • 180x200cm: Order box 3 as supplement order, SKU EBDBB180200X31



2.3: Other markets


Order Box 3 as a supplement order, SKU should be the one in the original order ending in X31 or X32. The SKU should follow the logic EBDBB******X3*, as shown in the picture below:




Step 3:


The agent must gather all information possible from the client regarding the problem. The message should be: “This is not Emma’s quality standard, and we need more information for further investigation”. The main questions are:

  • Do you feel that it bent gradually or all at once? Did anything feel odd with the bed before the beam bent?

  • When the beam bent, was there more load over the bed? If there was, could you give more details?

  • When assembling the bed, did you notice or suspect any defect in the beam or any other element - screws, for example - missing?



Step 4:


Finally, do not place a return request. Escalate it to giovanne.micheletti@emma-sleep.com, from Emma’s Quality Team.
