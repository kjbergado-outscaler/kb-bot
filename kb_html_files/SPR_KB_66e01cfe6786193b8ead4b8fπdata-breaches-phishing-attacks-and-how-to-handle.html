Phishing Attacks


Definition


A phishing attack is a type of cyber-attack where attackers impersonate legitimate institutions or persons to trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers, through deceptive emails or websites, and may cause a data breach.


Examples


Examples of fishing attacks may include:


  • An agent receives an email that appears to be from a legitimate customer asking for account details or other sensitive information.

  • Fake login pages that attempt to steal usernames and passwords. Emails claiming urgent account issues and requesting immediate action.

  • Spoofed emails pretending to be from well-known companies or government agencies.

  • Emails promising prizes, rewards, or financial benefits in exchange for personal details.


How to detect Phishing Attacks


  • Suspicious Sender: Check if the email is from a legitimate source. Be wary of email addresses that look similar to the company's but are slightly altered;

  • Urgency and Threats: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency or threat, such as claiming your account will be locked;

  • Links and Attachments: Do not click on links or download attachments from unknown or suspicious emails;

  • Request for Sensitive Information: Legitimate companies will not ask for sensitive information via email.



How to handle a phishing e-mail


  1. Do Not Respond: Do not reply to the email or provide any requested information;

  2. Report: If you receive a ticket related to phishing emails and happen to click on it, you should immediately report it to your supervisor. Your supervisor should urgently report it to the Emma IT team;

  3. Delete: Delete the phishing email from your inbox;

  4. Monitor: Keep an eye on your accounts for any unusual activity and report it immediately.


NOTE: Before opening a ticket in case of doubt, please reach out to your supervisor for support.
